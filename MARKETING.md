# Starting Monday — Marketing & Growth Plan

**Product:** startingmonday.app  
**Audience:** Mid-to-senior executives — Director level and above  
**Model:** Freemium  
**Stage:** Pre-launch / Founder-led growth  

---

## 1. Positioning

Starting Monday is the first AI-powered executive career intelligence platform built specifically for senior operators. Not another job board. Not a resume tool. The thing that makes you walk into every interview owning the room.

The core promise: **elite preparation, without the executive coach price tag.**

Competitors are either too junior (LinkedIn Learning, The Muse), too passive (job boards), or too expensive (retained outplacement firms at $5K–$25K). Starting Monday occupies the uncontested space between "do it yourself" and "hire a coach."

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary Persona — The Active Executive
- Title: VP, SVP, EVP, CXO, Managing Director, Director of [Function]
- Status: In active search or quietly exploring
- Pain: Prep is time-consuming, generic advice doesn't fit their level, they're too senior to ask their network for basic help

### Secondary Persona — The Quietly Ambitious
- Employed, not openly searching
- High-performing, wants to be ready if the right opportunity lands
- Values discretion above all else

### Psychographic Profile (both)
- Intelligent, pattern-matching thinkers — they'll recognize quality instantly
- High bar for tools; will abandon anything that feels junior or generic
- Status-conscious — the brand matters as much as the product
- Time-scarce — value leverage over effort
- Skeptical of AI hype; won over by specific, accurate output

## 3. Founder-Led Content Strategy

### Why Founder-Led
Richard is the target user. He knows the pain, speaks the language, and has earned credibility in the room. Authentic content from a peer in the search converts far better than brand content at this stage.

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel)
LinkedIn is where the audience lives. Every post is a demo.

**Cadence:** 3–4 posts per week (mix of formats below)  
**Voice:** Direct, confident, zero corporate softening — like advice from a peer who's been through it  

**Content pillars:**

1. **War stories** — Real moments from executive interviews, board rooms, offer negotiations. "Here's what I said when they challenged my public sector experience..."
2. **Prep intelligence** — Specific tactics: how to research a company 48 hours before an interview, how to counter a lowball offer without blowing the deal
3. **Product transparency** — Show the brief. Screenshot the output. Let the product sell itself.
4. **Industry commentary** — Observations on the executive job market, what's actually happening vs. what career sites say
5. **Counterintuitive takes** — "Stop tailoring your resume for every job" / "Your LinkedIn headline is costing you interviews"

**Format mix:**
- Long-form narrative posts (highest reach for executives)
- Screenshot carousels ("I asked AI to build my interview brief — here's what it produced")
- Short sharp observations (3–5 lines, high engagement)
- Polls ("Which part of interview prep takes you the longest?")

**Engagement rule:** Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards it and it builds the community that becomes the moat.

### X / Threads / Instagram

**X:** Shorter, sharper. Mirror the best LinkedIn posts. Engage with executive career conversation threads. Tag journalists covering the AI/future-of-work beat.

**Threads:** Cross-post LinkedIn content. Growing executive audience that LinkedIn hasn't captured yet.

**Instagram:** Visual-first. Design the prep brief outputs as clean graphics. "Before/After" narrative frames. Behind-the-scenes product builds. Less polished = more authentic.

**Execution:** Batch-produce once a week, schedule across platforms. One person (or your wife) handles scheduling, monitoring, and DM responses.

## 4. Strategic Seeding — Human Networks

### Career Coaches
Individual executive career coaches are a force multiplier. Each coach serves 10–30 active clients. One adoption = 10–30 users.

**Targets:**
- Coaches listed on International Coaching Federation (ICF) directory filtered for "executive" + "career"
- Coaches with LinkedIn followings of 5K–50K (influential but reachable)
- Coaches who publish their own content (they'll share tools that make them look good)

**Offer:** Free Pro access in exchange for honest feedback and referrals to clients. No affiliate pressure.

### Outplacement Firms
Firms like Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, and Challenger, Gray & Christmas serve thousands of executives simultaneously — all in active search, all with a budget attached to their transition package.

**Approach:** Position as a white-label or referral partner (see Section 6). Entry point is a regional director or VP of Client Services, not the C-suite.

### University Alumni Career Centers
Alumni centers serve two segments simultaneously: recently graduated MBAs and senior alumni in transition. Both fit the profile.

**Priority targets:**
- Harvard Business School Career Services
- Wharton Executive Education / Alumni Career Resources
- Stanford Graduate School of Business Career Management Center
- Kellogg, Booth, Tuck, Darden alumni networks
- Duke Fuqua Executive MBA alumni

**Offer:** Free institutional access for staff to evaluate + a co-branded landing page for their alumni. Warm introductions through the boards and advisory roles Richard already holds.

### Professional Associations
- Association of Executive Search Consultants (AESC) / BlueSteps — direct pipeline to executives in search
- Chief — senior women executives, high engagement with career development tools
- YPO / EO — peer communities of business owners and senior leaders

## 6. White Label & Moat Strategy

### The White Label Opportunity
Outplacement firms and career platforms already have the clients. They lack the AI infrastructure to deliver personalized prep at scale. Starting Monday can be the engine under their hood.

**Offer structure:**
- White-labeled version of the platform with their branding
- Per-seat monthly license or revenue share
- Custom onboarding flow with their logo and intake questions
- API access for deeper integrations (Phase 3)

**Target partners for white label:** Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, Challenger Gray, regional boutique outplacement firms

### The Moat
The moat is not the AI — it's the data and the relationships.

1. **User data flywheel** — Every brief generated, every refinement requested, every company tracked teaches the model what elite executive prep looks like at a granular level. This gets harder to replicate the longer Starting Monday runs.
2. **Integration depth** — The more a user's pipeline, contacts, scan results, and notes are inside Starting Monday, the harder it is to leave. Switching cost grows with usage.
3. **Partner exclusivity** — Lock in preferred-partner agreements with the top 2–3 outplacement firms and career platforms before a well-funded competitor does.
4. **Community** — A private community of senior executives who trust the platform becomes self-sustaining. Peer referral inside senior networks is the highest-quality growth lever available.
5. **Brand association** — Be the tool career coaches recommend. When coaches recommend it, executives buy it. When executives tell their displaced peers, the loop closes.

## 8. Automation & Scaling

### What Can Be Automated
- **Social scheduling:** Buffer or Hypefury for LinkedIn + X + Threads batching
- **Partner outreach tracking:** Simple CRM (even Notion) to track who's been contacted, response status, follow-up dates
- **Inbound lead capture:** Landing page form → email nurture sequence in Resend
- **Referral tracking:** UTM parameters on all partner links; dashboard view of conversion by source

### What Cannot Be Automated (Yet)
- Founder voice on LinkedIn — authenticity is the product at this stage
- First-meeting conversations with partners — trust is built person-to-person
- Community engagement — replies, DMs, comments must be real

### Division of Labor
- Richard: Writes LinkedIn content, takes partner calls, approves all outreach
- Spouse support: Schedules posts, monitors comments/DMs, flags anything needing response, tracks outreach pipeline, handles logistics

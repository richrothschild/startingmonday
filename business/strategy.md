## 1. Executive Summary

Starting Monday is a SaaS platform purpose-built for senior technology executives in active or approaching job search. It provides pipeline tracking, early role intelligence, AI-powered interview prep briefs, and a daily morning briefing. The platform is designed to make an executive search invisible, deliberate, and structurally superior to what any individual can build manually.

The business targets a $15M exit in Year 5 through a combination of direct B2C subscriptions, a B2B channel through executive coaches and outplacement firms, and international expansion into English-speaking markets. The path to that exit runs through proving the B2B channel by Year 3, reaching $2M+ ARR by Year 5, and positioning the platform as the recognized category leader in executive search infrastructure.

### B2B Channel

Two B2B channels materially expand revenue without one-at-a-time customer acquisition.

**Outplacement Firms**
Companies pay outplacement firms $10,000-$50,000 per executive to manage transitions. Starting Monday is sold as a per-engagement add-on at $350/executive. Zero CAC once the contract is in place. Key targets: Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, Korn Ferry Advance, Challenger Gray & Christmas, Randstad RiseSmart.

**Executive Coaches**
~2,000 coaches in the US specialize in executive career transition. Monthly seat license at $30/seat/month, billed to the coach. Average coach carries 8-10 active clients. Coach retention is structurally longer than consumer — coach stays subscribed as long as they practice.

**B2B Revenue Per Unit**

| Outplacement | $350/engagement | $69 | 80% |

**Annual billing:** Offer 2 months free (~16% discount) for annual payment. Reduces Stripe fees from 3.2% to ~0.4% per month equivalent. Improves Year 1-2 cash flow materially. Should be the default offer.

## 5. Exit Strategy

### Target

**$15M exit in Year 5 (Q3/Q4 2030)**

Validation: $2.13M ARR × 7x = $14.9M. $840K EBITDA × 18x = $15.1M. Both approaches converge on the target, suggesting it is achievable without requiring a premium multiple.

### Exit Conditions Required

To command a $15M price, the business must present all of the following at time of sale:

1. ARR at $2M+ with >40% YoY growth rate
2. Two active outplacement firm contracts (proves institutional B2B channel)
3. UK market live with demonstrated revenue (proves global TAM)
4. Clean GAAP financials, two years of reviewed statements
5. Gross margins held at 85%+
6. Documented CAC and LTV by channel
7. NPS data from recognizable CIO and CTO customers
8. Proprietary company-targeting dataset (5 years of pipeline data)
9. No key-person dependency in operations (founder extractable)

### Likely Buyer Profiles

**Strategic buyers (pay 7-10x ARR)**
- Large outplacement firms (LHH, Right Management) — want the AI prep technology
- HR tech platforms (Workday, Cornerstone, iCIMS) — want the executive segment
- Executive search firms (Korn Ferry, Heidrick) — want the candidate-side data and prep
- LinkedIn / Microsoft — want the brand and the executive niche they do not own

**Financial buyers (pay 4-6x ARR)**
- Private equity roll-up of HR tech or career management SaaS
- Search fund acquisition
- Independent sponsor

Strategic buyer is the target. They pay a premium for distribution they cannot easily build. The outplacement firm contracts are the most compelling asset to a strategic buyer because they represent captive deal flow.

## 6. Key Risks and Obstacles

### By Year

**Year 1 — Discovery**
The audience does not search for this product. Zero Google query volume for "CIO job search platform." Every customer in Year 1 requires a personal touch — Richard's network, LinkedIn reach, or organic blog traffic. The blog takes 6-12 months to rank. The first 50 customers are the hardest to acquire in the company's history. The critical metric is time-to-first-value: a user who generates a prep brief in session one converts. A user who does not, churns.

**Year 2 — Conversion and B2B credibility**
Trial-to-paid conversion will be approximately 20-25%. 75-80% of people who start a trial will not pay. Most will not explain why. The B2B channel requires a credibility signal that does not yet exist — a named CIO who will publicly vouch for the product is worth more than any marketing spend.

**Year 3 — The hiring transition**
The founder becomes the bottleneck. Content, sales, product, and customer success are all founder-dependent. The first external hire (B2B sales) is high-risk: a bad hire at $140K all-in costs seven months of EBITDA and delays the outplacement channel by a year. The first outplacement contract takes 4-6 months from first meeting to signature.

**Year 4 — Competition arrives**
If the platform is working, established outplacement firms may build internally rather than license externally. LinkedIn adding CIO-specific prep features inside LinkedIn Premium is the single largest existential risk. They cannot build what Starting Monday has built in the same time, but they can approximate 70% of the value for free. The defense is brand recognition, data depth, and the specificity that a mass platform cannot match.

**Year 5 — Scale or sell**
At $2M ARR, the business faces a fork: grow organically toward $4-5M ARR, raise a Series A to accelerate, or sell. The growth ceiling without external capital is approximately $4-5M ARR. A raise buys 18-24 months of accelerated growth but dilutes significantly and introduces investor expectations that change the exit calculus.

### Structural Risks (All Years)

- **LinkedIn dependency:** The LinkedIn import is core onboarding. LinkedIn's ToS changes or a blocking event disrupts acquisition.
- **Founder personal brand dependency:** Richard is the brand. Valuation discount applies to any buyer until operations are founder-independent.
- **AI cost exposure:** Claude API costs will likely decrease but model availability is not guaranteed. Building on a single AI provider is a risk.
- **Counter-cyclical exposure (upside risk):** A recession increases demand — more executives searching, longer durations. Model assumes steady conditions. Downturn in Years 2-3 could accelerate the curve.

### What Is Not In This Model

- **Annual billing impact:** 30% of customers on annual billing improves Year 2 cash by ~$40K and eliminates the Stripe cost drag.
- **Premium concierge tier:** 20 customers at $700/month = $168K ARR at near-zero incremental infrastructure cost.
- **Proprietary data product:** By Year 4-5, the accumulated dataset of executive company targeting has standalone value to search firms and PE. Not modeled — potential $500K-2M incremental revenue.
- **Mark Horstman / Manager Tools partnership:** One endorsement to that community could deliver more Year 1 revenue than the entire model projects. Not forecastable.
- **Content monetization:** Newsletter sponsorships, executive search firm advertising. Small but meaningful by Year 3.

## 8. The Honest Summary

This is a $15M exit business with strong gross margins, no external capital required to reach profitability, and a defensible niche. The path is clear. The obstacles are real but manageable with focused execution.

The thing most likely to prevent reaching the target is not competition or market size. It is founder bandwidth in Years 2 and 3, when B2B relationship-building (slow, personal, non-scalable) competes with content production (the primary B2C acquisition channel) for the same hours. Both are founder-dependent. Both are critical. Managing that tension is the central operational challenge of the business.

The B2B channel is the business. Without it, the ceiling is approximately $700K ARR and a $4-5M exit. With two proven outplacement contracts, the business commands a strategic multiple and the $15M target is achievable.

The blog and LinkedIn content compound over time and become a genuine moat. A library of 40+ deeply specific posts on CIO search strategy, indexed and ranking, is something a well-funded competitor cannot buy overnight. Start now, publish consistently, and protect that asset.

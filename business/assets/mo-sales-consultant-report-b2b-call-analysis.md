## What Worked Across Both Calls

1. Problem relevance was real
- Timing, access, and execution consistency were acknowledged as meaningful pain.

2. Founder credibility created trust
- Senior operating background and systems-building credibility lowered skepticism.

3. Collaborative stance increased goodwill
- Inviting criticism and co-creation encouraged honest engagement.

4. Trial-first posture reduced friction
- Free trial language made exploration easy.

## What Did Not Work Across Both Calls

1. Qualification did not go deep enough, early enough
Missing or light in both calls:
- Current conversion baselines
- Cost-of-delay economics
- Buying authority and procurement path
- Timeline urgency and consequences of inaction

2. Value narrative drifted into mechanism
You explained how it works in detail before fully anchoring what business result will change first.

3. Concession timing eroded value position
Offering broad flexibility and free-forever language before KPI framing weakens enterprise signal.

4. Close lacked operational specificity
No hard next-step with:
- kickoff date
- owner on both sides
- success KPIs
- formal decision gate

## ICP-Specific Buying Psychology

## Executive Coach ICP (Penny-type)
Primary buying logic:
- "Will this improve client outcomes and my coaching leverage?"

What they fear:
- Tool sprawl without behavior change
- Generic messaging that dilutes leadership positioning
- Time spent implementing software that does not change outcomes

What wins:
- Clear integration into existing coaching method
- Reduced recap/admin burden
- Better conversion in networking-to-interview progression

## Recruiter/Search ICP (Sylvie-type)
Primary buying logic:
- "Will this create earlier and better BD opportunities than my current stack?"

What they fear:
- Another data source that duplicates LinkedIn
- No measurable uplift in meetings or qualified opportunities
- Time investment without pipeline impact

What wins:
- Explicit "before public posting" edge
- Cleaner account prioritization
- Faster path to decision-maker conversations

## What A World-Class BD Leader Would Say

1. Discovery must precede explanation
"Do not spend 10+ minutes on mechanism until you quantify pain and urgency."

2. Sell a business case, not a product tour
"Translate capability into first-order commercial outcomes in the prospect's language."

3. Protect your value anchor
"Use structured pilot offers. Avoid ad hoc concessioning in first call."

4. Convert interest into commitment on-call
"A good call ends with a date, KPI plan, and mutual owner assignment."

5. Run ICP-specific talk tracks
"Coaches buy leverage and client behavior change. Recruiters buy timing edge and BD efficiency."

## Call Architecture Mo Should Run

## Minute 0-6: Qualification
Ask:
1. "What is your current source mix for opportunities?"
2. "Where do opportunities die today: timing, targeting, conversion, or follow-up?"
3. "What is the cost if this remains unchanged for 90 days?"
4. "Which tools are you using now and what is missing?"
5. "Who decides and by when if we run a pilot?"

## Minute 6-15: Hypothesis + proof
- Present one ICP-specific hypothesis.
- Share one numerical outcome target tied to their workflow.

## Minute 15-24: Focused walkthrough
- Show only the workflow relevant to their stated bottleneck.

## Minute 24-30: Close
"Based on what you shared, I recommend a 30-day pilot with [KPI set]. If we hit targets, we scale. If not, we pause. Does [date/time] work for kickoff and [date/time] for decision review?"

## Objection Handling Playbook For Mo

1. "We already use LinkedIn / SourceWell"
Response:
"That stack is strong for active visible demand. Our edge is improving timing and prioritization before visibility peaks, so your BD effort lands earlier and with better odds."

2. "I need to think about it"
Response:
"Makes sense. Rather than broad commitment, let us run a bounded pilot with explicit stop/continue criteria so the decision is evidence-based."

3. "Price is high"
Response:
"If the pilot creates even one additional qualified opportunity or shortens time-to-opportunity materially, the economics are positive. Let us validate that quickly before scaling."

4. "My clients will not execute"
Response:
"That is exactly why the workflow layer matters. We can test on execution-ready clients first and measure completion and conversion change."

# Coaches Landing Page Change List

Refined through three council passes:
- Practitioner lens: remove scroll-heavy explanation.
- Practice-owner lens: move pricing and ROI earlier.
- Trust lens: make privacy and control visible at the top.

## What Needs to Change

- Replace the current long-form partner-guide feel on the main coach landing page.
- Move to a main-landing-page structure: hero, proof, at-a-glance, next step, footer.
- Reduce the amount of explanatory copy on the landing page.

- Make the coach journey explicit.
- Lead coaches and firms to the right next step: persona route, preview, trust pack, or economics.
- Stop asking every visitor to read everything before they understand the offer.

- Surface coach outcomes earlier.
- Put prepared clients, less context rebuild, and visible weekly momentum in the first screenful.
- Replace feature-first framing with outcome-first framing.

- Clarify the pilot.
- Show the 30-day preview package and success criteria on the landing page.
- Make it obvious that the buyer can test with a small number of live clients.

- Clarify pricing without turning the landing page into the economics page.
- Show the coach buyer plans in a compact strip.
- Keep the full economics detail on the economics page.

- Make trust impossible to miss.
- Add a clear client-control and no recruiter-side-sharing statement near the hero.
- Link to the trust pack from the main landing page.

- Bring the council-supported proof forward.
- Use pilot metrics and coach-specific operating evidence.
- Avoid burying proof inside long FAQ or economics sections.

- Make support pages support the journey instead of competing with it.
- Keep the FAQ, economics, worksheet, and micro-products visible but secondary.
- Use those pages as the next layer after the core landing page tells the story.

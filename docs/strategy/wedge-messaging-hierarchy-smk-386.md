## 1) Positioning Core

### Category
Pre-posting executive opportunity intelligence and relationship execution.

### Wedge
Executives win before postings go public.

### Product Truth
Starting Monday identifies likely-to-open roles early, maps who influences hiring decisions, and drives weekly relationship actions.

## 2) Message Hierarchy

### Hero (single line)
Be on the shortlist before the role is posted.

### Sub-hero
Each week, get likely-to-open executive roles, the decision-path people to engage, and your exact next actions.

### Proof Pillars
1. Timing advantage: likely-to-open role detection before posting.
2. Decision-path clarity: who shapes the hiring decision, not generic networking.
3. Behavior execution: weekly action plan with follow-through visibility.

### Support Pillars
1. Private-by-design workflow for confidential transitions.
2. Premium experience for senior operators, not mass application volume.
3. Decision-ready prep once role and relationships are identified.

## 3) Objection Handling (Sales + Product)

### Objection A
"I can do this with Claude."

Response:
Claude can help generate prep content. It cannot reliably tell you which executive roles are likely to open this week and who in the decision path to build relationships with now. Starting Monday is timing + influence execution, not just content generation.

### Objection B
"This sounds like a job board."

Response:
Job boards are reactive and public. Starting Monday is proactive and private: a curated shortlist before posting plus relationship actions tied to decision influence.

### Objection C
"I already have a strong network."

Response:
Strong networks still need timing and precision. Starting Monday helps prioritize where to activate your network now and who matters most for each likely opening.

### Objection D
"I do not want another tool to manage."

Response:
The workflow is intentionally narrow: one weekly shortlist, one decision-path view, one action queue. It is built to reduce search overhead, not add process.

## 5) Messaging Guardrails

1. Do not lead with "AI prep" language.
2. Do not position as a job board alternative only.
3. Do not present content-heavy pages before wedge value appears.
4. Every top section should answer: Why this over DIY AI?
5. Emphasize measurable behavior outcomes, not generic motivation.

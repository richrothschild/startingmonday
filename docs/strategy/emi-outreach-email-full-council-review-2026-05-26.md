# EMI Outreach Email Full-Council Review

Date: 2026-05-26
Scope: Outreach email templates in current stack

Templates reviewed:
1. Executives initial outreach (in-app template engine)
2. Executives follow-up 1
3. Executives follow-up 2
4. Executives follow-up 3 (close loop)
5. Search firms initial outreach
6. Coaches initial outreach (in-app template engine)
7. Outplacement firms initial outreach (in-app template engine)
8. Coach campaign email sequence (Day 1, Day 7, Day 21, Day 30) in outreach docs

Method:
- Treated each council member as a reasoning lens, not a voice imitation.
- Aligned recommendations to EMI pillars: emotional-state clarity, momentum loop, measurable proof, trust-safe tone, role-specific readiness framing.

## Cross-Template Findings

Strong already:
1. Most templates are trust-first and avoid hard pressure language.
2. Several templates already include role-specific stakes and concrete assets.
3. Close-loop and low-friction CTA patterns are mostly healthy.

Systemic gaps:
1. EMI language is inconsistent: "practical execution system/layer" appears, but "Emotional Motion Intelligence" and "momentum state" framing are not explicit enough.
2. Emotional-state segmentation is weak in outbound copy. Panic, optionality, burnout, and board-track modes are mostly implied, not named.
3. Proof lines read generic in places and are not consistently denominator-backed.
4. CTAs still over-index on "send asset" rather than "small diagnostic + next decision".
5. Search-firm and outplacement variants are less differentiated than executive and coach variants.

## Proposed Rewrite Guardrails

1. One trigger, one tension, one ask per email.
2. Max one proof claim unless second claim is denominator-backed.
3. Keep first touch under 140 words when possible.
4. No generic urgency language.
5. Every message must pass trust-first tone and legal-safe claims checks.

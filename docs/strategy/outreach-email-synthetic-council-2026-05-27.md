## Missing Reader Personas Added

### Executives

Persona: Displaced CIO under time pressure

What they care about:

1. Fast clarity.
2. Immediate usefulness.
3. Reduced panic.
4. A better first conversation, not a bigger process.

What makes them open:

1. The subject line says what they get in plain language.
2. The opening sentence names the career-risk or transition pressure.
3. The email promises speed, clarity, or recovery.
4. The ask is tiny and safe to ignore.

### Coaches

Persona: Solo executive coach operator

What they care about:

1. Less prep drag.
2. Less admin overhead.
3. Better between-session momentum.
4. Stronger client outcomes without extra work.

What makes them open:

1. The subject line names a workflow win.
2. The opening sentence sounds like one operator talking to another.
3. The body gives a concrete coaching outcome, not a platform pitch.
4. The ask is a simple yes or pass.

### Search Firms

Persona: Retained search partner

What they care about:

1. Mandate economics.
2. First-touch quality.
3. Shortlist credibility.
4. Avoiding wasted partner time.

What makes them open:

1. The subject line signals a better mandate process.
2. The opening sentence names a live search problem.
3. The email makes the business consequence obvious.
4. The proof is short and tied to search quality.

### Outplacement Firms

Persona: Outplacement program director

What they care about:

1. Cohort consistency.
2. Counselor workflow.
3. Client confidence.
4. Lower coordinator burden.

What makes them open:

1. The subject line promises a measurable program improvement.
2. The opening sentence names the cohort or program pressure.
3. The email explains the operational benefit immediately.
4. The ask is to see one usable asset, not adopt a system.

## Test Of The User's Notion

Claim being tested: the emails are too vague about the value to the reader.

Council verdict:

1. True for coaches.
2. Mostly true for outplacement.
3. Partly true for executives.
4. Least true for search firms.

Why:

1. Search-firm emails already imply a business outcome because mandate quality and first-touch quality are concrete.
2. Coach and outplacement emails still lean on framework language like signal map, momentum, readiness, and benchmark.
3. Executive emails promise a plan, but the reader still has to infer why the plan matters right now.

## First Principles For Reading A Cold Email

The council's first principles are:

1. I open when the email clearly connects to my current work or risk.
2. I open when I can tell, in one glance, what I get if I keep reading.
3. I open when the sender respects my time and does not ask for a big commitment.
4. I open when the language sounds like a person who understands my job, not a template.
5. I open when the proof is brief, believable, and not inflated.
6. I open when the email offers a small next step that is easy to say no to.
7. I do not open when I have to infer the value myself.

## Bottom Line

The notion is correct: the current emails still need more explicit reader value, especially in coaches and outplacement.

The best fix is not more persuasion language. It is making the subject line and first two sentences answer three things immediately:

1. Why me.
2. Why now.
3. What do I get.

## Executive Summary Findings

Top systemic blockers to A+:
1. Subjects are still "Quick question..." heavy and under-leveraging trigger specificity.
2. Body length is too high for first touches (most are 160-240 words).
3. Redundant sentence blocks reduce scanability and first-response momentum.
4. CTA is strong (yes/pass) but often appears after too much context.
5. Some templates still feel framework-led rather than recipient-led.

Most important upgrades for open and reply lift:
1. Use trigger-plus-role subject pattern (Dunford, Barrows, Sutherland).
2. Keep first-touch emails to 90-130 words (Braun, Godin, Clear).
3. Move CTA up so it appears before final paragraph in every template (Thaler, Barrows).
4. Keep one proof line only; remove secondary proof sentences in first touch (Cialdini, Grant, O'Carroll).
5. Use one emotional-state cue only in opening and avoid repeating mode labels (Bourgoin, Kim Scott).

## A+ Open-Rate and Reply-Rate Operating Targets

Open-rate target improvements:
1. Subject includes role + trigger + consequence signal.
2. Subject length target: 42-68 characters where possible.
3. Avoid generic opener terms such as "Quick question" unless paired with concrete trigger.

Reply-rate target improvements:
1. First touch <= 130 words.
2. One proof line only.
3. CTA appears before final sign-off and uses yes/pass binary.
4. One asset per email.

# 90-Day Jira Epic: Competitive Positioning Strengthening

Date: 2026-06-07
Owner: Product + Growth + Engineering
Status: Ready for Jira ingestion
Horizon: 90 days

## Epic Summary
Strengthen Starting Monday market position by converting product differentiation into measurable GTM advantage across three vectors:
- Buyer clarity
- Proof/trust
- Channel distribution

## Epic Objectives
1. Increase homepage-to-signup conversion by 20%.
2. Increase trial-to-paid conversion by 20%.
3. Increase WAU of core discover-prep-action flow by 20%.
4. Improve 30-day retention by 10%.
5. Source 20-30% of qualified pipeline from partner channels.

## Scope
In scope:
- Positioning and messaging refactor for core pages
- Public proof center and evidence instrumentation
- Core moat reinforcement in signal/prep/workflow surfaces
- Coach and outplacement pilot channel launch

Out of scope:
- Broadening into generic job board functionality
- Building full coaching marketplace business model
- Building done-for-you reverse recruiting service layer

## Success Metrics and Gates
Primary metrics:
- Signup conversion rate
- Activation completion rate (first signal run + first narrative open)
- Trial-to-paid conversion
- 30-day retention
- Partner-sourced pipeline share

Diagnostic metrics:
- Narrative open rate
- Outreach action start rate
- Suggested people acceptance rate
- Add-to-watchlist lift vs baseline
- Campaign health score coverage

Decision gates:
- Day 30: messaging and conversion gate
- Day 60: proof and trust gate
- Day 90: moat and distribution gate

## WBS to Jira Stories

### WBS 1: Positioning and Message Architecture (Day 1-30)
Story 1.1: Lock category narrative and taxonomy
- Deliverables:
  - Canonical category definition
  - "for / not for" matrix
  - Message hierarchy by ICP
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Narrative approved by Product, Growth, Sales
  - Reused across homepage, pricing, product tours

Story 1.2: Homepage and key landing rewrite
- Deliverables:
  - Homepage copy refresh
  - 5 role pages (CIO, CTO, VP Eng, CDO, CISO)
  - Comparison pages vs job boards, coaching, done-for-you services
- Acceptance criteria:
  - All pages include clear value stack: signal + prep + execution
  - All pages include measurable proof blocks

Story 1.3: Pricing and packaging clarity update
- Deliverables:
  - Plan comparison by buyer mode
  - Trial expectations and first-week outcomes
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Pricing page and in-app plan copy aligned

### WBS 2: Proof and Trust Engine (Day 15-60)
Story 2.1: Public proof center launch
- Deliverables:
  - Evidence page with KPI definitions and update cadence
  - Role-specific outcomes section
- Acceptance criteria:
  - 4 core KPI trend cards live
  - Source notes and methodology section included

Story 2.2: Case study and social proof pack
- Deliverables:
  - 6-10 customer stories (exec tech leader segments)
  - Structured before/after narrative and quantified outcomes
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Minimum 6 publishable stories live

Story 2.3: Trust and governance visibility layer
- Deliverables:
  - Security and privacy summary for buyers
  - Data provenance and governance statement
  - High-integrity prep positioning statement
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Trust content linked from homepage and pricing

### WBS 3: Product Moat Reinforcement (Day 30-90)
Story 3.1: Signal quality surface
- Deliverables:
  - Signal freshness score
  - Provenance coverage indicators
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Score present on discover recommendations

Story 3.2: Prep quality loop
- Deliverables:
  - Prep scorecard in recommendation detail and brief flow
  - Outcome-linked prep guidance nudges
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Prep quality metrics written to events table

Story 3.3: Campaign health system
- Deliverables:
  - Campaign health score (cadence, follow-up, conversion progression)
  - Alerts for stalled campaigns
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Health score visible in dashboard and admin views

Story 3.4: Closed-loop analytics
- Deliverables:
  - Attribution joins for signal->open->action->add->outreach start
  - Weekly insights digest for internal teams
- Acceptance criteria:
  - Query pack and dashboard cards live

### WBS 4: Partner Distribution Motion (Day 45-120)
Story 4.1: Coach pilot setup
- Deliverables:
  - Coach pilot package, ICP list, outreach sequence
  - Shared-view workflow for coach and client
- Acceptance criteria:
  - 10 design partners targeted, 3 active pilot starts

Story 4.2: Outplacement pilot setup
- Deliverables:
  - Outplacement partner deck, ROI framing, governance checklist
  - Cohort dashboard requirements
- Acceptance criteria:
  - 5 targets sourced, 2 pilot conversations in late stage

Story 4.3: Partner reporting and renewal packet
- Deliverables:
  - Monthly partner report template
  - Renewal decision packet template
- Acceptance criteria:
  - First partner report issued during pilot period

## Owners
- Product owner: narrative, roadmap, acceptance
- Growth owner: conversion tests, case studies, landing pages
- Engineering owner: instrumentation, score systems, dashboard surfaces
- Partnerships owner: channel pilots and reporting
- Design owner: information architecture, proof UX, partner assets

## Risks and Mitigations
Risk 1: Message drift across channels
- Mitigation: single source positioning doc + mandatory copy checklist

Risk 2: Proof lag blocks conversion gains
- Mitigation: publish baseline proof quickly, then iterate with monthly refresh

Risk 3: Pilot partners stall due to unclear ROI
- Mitigation: partner ROI packet + weekly pilot check-ins + executive sponsor

## Weekly Operating Cadence
- Monday: KPI review and experiment decisions
- Wednesday: build and content readiness sync
- Friday: leadership update and blocker removal

## Exit Criteria
Epic can close when:
1. All Day 90 gate metrics are met or have approved variance plan.
2. Partner pilots are live with first reporting artifacts delivered.
3. Positioning stack is deployed to primary acquisition and conversion surfaces.
4. Moat scorecards are visible in product and admin analytics.

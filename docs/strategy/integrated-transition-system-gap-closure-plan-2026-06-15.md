# Integrated Transition System Gap Closure Plan (2026-06-15)

Owner: Product + Growth + Engineering
Status: draft
Horizon: 90 days
Canonical source: docs/coach-outplacement-gap-analysis-report-2026-06-15.md

## Objective

Close the highest-value gaps across the integrated Starting Monday system so coaches, outplacement teams, and executives experience one coherent operating system for executive transitions.

The plan focuses on four execution epics:

1. Trust, proof, and claims discipline hardening
2. Coach and counselor operating system completion
3. Executive lifecycle expansion
4. Partner operating system and sponsor reporting

## Success Criteria

By the end of this plan, Starting Monday should:

1. Present only verified or appropriately caveated public claims.
2. Make coach pre-session preparation visibly faster and more structured.
3. Cover executive optionality, active transition, and post-landing as one lifecycle.
4. Provide credible partner-grade operator and sponsor artifacts.
5. Reduce mismatch between public promise and visible product proof.

## Epic 1: Trust, Proof, and Claims Discipline Hardening

Goal:
Make the public trust layer evidence-safe, legally coherent, and conversion-strengthening.

Outcomes:

1. No unsupported public compliance or proof claims remain.
2. Verified, directional, and diligence-gated claims are explicitly separated.
3. Named proof assets exist for coaches, executives, and partner contexts.

Stories and tasks:

1. Audit all coach public trust and compliance claims.
2. Audit all outplacement public trust and proof claims.
3. Publish claims taxonomy and public proof policy.
4. Remediate coach FAQ and trust-pack language.
5. Add named coach proof asset block.
6. Add named executive and partner case evidence block.
7. Add capability-vs-roadmap disclosure pattern on partner pages.
8. Add artifact ownership and refresh cadence register.

## Epic 2: Coach and Counselor Operating System Completion

Goal:
Finish the highest-value workflow gaps for coaches and counselor-led delivery.

Outcomes:

1. Coaches get a first-class pre-session preparation artifact.
2. Weekly review becomes a more explicit operating ritual.
3. Firms can see portfolio and cohort-level health, not just single-client slices.
4. Counselor-led workflows become more visible and less implied.

Stories and tasks:

1. Ship first-class coach pre-session snapshot.
2. Redesign weekly review around decision, risk, narrative shift, and next action.
3. Add confidence, momentum, and narrative-drift fields.
4. Add firm-admin and cohort-admin coach views.
5. Add counselor-facing prep and intervention view or demo layer.
6. Add coach ROI calculator by practice model.
7. Add coach workflow proof instrumentation and dashboards.

## Epic 3: Executive Lifecycle Expansion

Goal:
Expand the executive product from active-search infrastructure into a more complete transition lifecycle system.

Outcomes:

1. Optionality mode becomes a first-class executive state.
2. Post-landing support becomes visible and structured.
3. Board and governance pursuit gains a dedicated workflow.
4. Executives gain decision-quality artifacts, not just activity infrastructure.

Stories and tasks:

1. Launch optionality mode for in-role executives.
2. Add accomplishment record and relationship-warmth memory loop.
3. Add executive decision cockpit for active tradeoffs.
4. Launch post-landing 30/60/90 mode.
5. Launch board and governance workflow pack.
6. Add confidential in-role transition route and messaging.
7. Add lifecycle templates: optionality, active campaign, post-landing.

Acceptance criteria:

1. Persona variants are first-class in Epic 3 artifacts, with differentiated workflows for in-role optionality, active transition, post-landing transition, and board-track executives.
2. Confidential and subtle external positioning guidance is embedded for in-role users, including signal-level controls for profile updates and outreach posture.
3. Executive branding profile is captured and maintained as a reusable artifact with narrative thesis, leadership proof points, and audience-specific message variants.
4. "What matters" decision profile is captured as weighted criteria (for example: location, family impact, base and total compensation, industry fit, brand impact, lifestyle, timing), supports user-defined custom factors, and can be adjusted over time.
5. Target-company and offer evaluation views use weighted "what matters" criteria to calculate fit score, risk score, and tradeoff notes used during search and offer decisions.
6. Advisor workflow supports readiness without panic through archetype mapping, weekly priority stack, and stakeholder-specific rapid-prep flows.

Executive Risk and Constraints addendum:

1. Hard constraints are captured separately from weighted preferences, with explicit must-have gates that can disqualify targets and offers before weighted scoring.
2. Weighted preferences remain adjustable and include default plus user-defined custom factors for ongoing tradeoff decisions.
3. Pre-interview and post-interview thought capture is required, including perceived role-fit deltas, confidence deltas, and objection notes by stakeholder audience.
4. Target companies are ranked both overall and by specific criteria, with visible rank-change history used for weekly prioritization and offer decisions.

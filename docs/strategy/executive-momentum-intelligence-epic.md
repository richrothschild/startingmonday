# Epic: Executive Momentum Intelligence (EMI) Category Launch

Status: Complete (Execution package delivered)
Owner: Founder Office + Product + GTM
Start date: 2026-05-27
Target end date: 2026-08-26
Execution close date: 2026-05-25

## Epic Goal

Operationalize the new EMI strategy into shippable assets and product behavior loops that increase:

1. B2B acquisition and retention (coaches, outplacement firms, partner channels)
2. B2C acquisition and retention (executives in transition, optionality, and board-track modes)

This epic explicitly covers:

1. Member-by-member objections mapped to deliverables
2. One KPI per objection
3. A 90-day sprint plan (6 sprints, 2 weeks each)

## Success Criteria

1. EMI category language appears in >= 85 percent of top-funnel assets.
2. EMI Assessment v1 launched and producing >= 40 percent completion from starts.
3. Daily Momentum Loop v1 shipped with >= 55 percent day-7 return for activated users.
4. Benchmark proof system publishes at least 3 denominator-backed proof artifacts.
5. B2B pilot conversion from qualified calls reaches >= 25 percent.

## Scope

In scope:

1. Category narrative and objection-handling infrastructure
2. EMI assessment and score output system
3. Daily behavior loop and recovery mechanics
4. Evidence pipeline and benchmark publication discipline
5. Partner and procurement trust artifacts

Out of scope:

1. Full rebrand of all site surfaces
2. New pricing tier launches beyond test packaging
3. Enterprise procurement automation beyond pilot documentation

## Part 3: 90-Day Sprint Plan (6 Sprints)

Cadence: 2-week sprints, each with a hard exit gate.

### Sprint 1 (Days 1-14): Category and Objection Infrastructure

Goal: Lock narrative, objections, and trust baseline.

Stories:

1. EMI positioning brief finalized (alternative, ICP, category claim).
2. Objection library v1 integrated into sales, partner, and content scripts.
3. Trust center starter pack (claims policy, data boundaries, KPI definitions).

Exit gate:

1. 100 percent of outbound and content templates use approved category language.

### Sprint 2 (Days 15-28): EMI Assessment v1 Launch

Goal: Ship first measurable top-of-funnel and partner asset.

Stories:

1. EMI Assessment v1 shipped with segmented outcomes.
2. Score output page with persona-specific recommendations.
3. Assessment analytics dashboard and event instrumentation live.

Exit gate:

1. Assessment completion rate >= 35 percent and first-value handoff <= 24 hours.

### Sprint 3 (Days 29-42): Daily Momentum Loop v1

Goal: Convert concept into daily behavior product.

Stories:

1. One-screen daily plan with max-3 priorities.
2. 72-hour recovery protocol for displaced segment.
3. Optionality mode for in-role executives.

Exit gate:

1. Day-7 return >= 50 percent in activated cohort.

### Sprint 4 (Days 43-56): Coach and Outplacement Enablement

Goal: Make B2B channels operationally buyable and repeatable.

Stories:

1. Coach shared-view and progress digest shipped.
2. Outplacement pilot runbook and ROI calculator shipped.
3. Counselor command view and low-admin prep summary shipped.

Exit gate:

1. At least 3 pilot-ready B2B opportunities created with complete enablement packs.

### Sprint 5 (Days 57-70): Benchmark and Proof System

Goal: Move from claims to evidence-backed authority.

Stories:

1. Benchmark pipeline v1 with denominator and confidence metadata.
2. First three proof artifacts published.
3. Proof stack integrated into GTM journey and follow-up.

Exit gate:

1. 100 percent of tier-1 claims in active assets are denominator-backed.

### Sprint 6 (Days 71-84): Scale, Tune, and Lock Operating Rhythm

Goal: Tune conversion, retention, and reliability before broader rollout.

Stories:

1. KPI tuning sprint against objection register (top 10 objections only).
2. SLOs and observability complete for EMI core flows.
3. Q4 operating plan and ownership map finalized.

Exit gate:

1. 4 of 5 epic success criteria met, and remaining gap has dated remediation plan.

## Epic Dependency List

## Risks and Mitigations

1. Risk: strategy remains narrative-heavy and product-light
- Mitigation: sprint gates require shipped product surfaces, not only documents

2. Risk: KPI sprawl reduces focus
- Mitigation: one primary KPI per objection and one sprint-level north-star

3. Risk: legal risk from over-claiming benchmark insights
- Mitigation: confidence labels, denominator rule, and claims checklist

4. Risk: B2B pilots fail due to operational complexity
- Mitigation: runbook-first design and founder-independent ops path

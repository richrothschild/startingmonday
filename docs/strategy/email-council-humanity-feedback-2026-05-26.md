# Email Council Humanity Feedback - 2026-05-26

Scope:

- Review current channel templates for human tone, readability, and authenticity.
- Focus on the coach sample and repeated Trigger this week opening style.

Decision:

- Council consensus: drafts are clearer than older versions, but still over-structured and too analytical.
- Trigger this week should be removed from body openers.

## Council Findings

### Severity 1: Mechanical opener pattern harms trust

Issue:

- Opening with Trigger this week sounds system-generated and adds formality overhead.

Why it hurts:

- Reduces warmth.
- Signals automation before relevance.
- Adds a parsing step for the reader.

Council support:

- Josh Braun: reads like a framework label, not a person.
- Kim Scott: sounds detached and procedural.
- Anne Applebaum: weakens credibility by sounding performative.

Required fix:

- Keep trigger content, remove the trigger label.
- Open with a natural sentence tied to recipient context.

### Severity 2: Choice framing is useful but still abstract

Issue:

- Choice lines are short but still phrased as model logic.

Why it hurts:

- Reader sees structure but not lived consequence.

Council support:

- Annie Duke: decision should feel real and reversible, not theoretical.
- Octavia Zahrt: coach emails should name practical coaching impact.

Required fix:

- Remove Choice labels.
- Tie the decision to one practical cost or gain.

### Severity 3: Proof line is still report-like

Issue:

- Proof sentence is accurate but dense and citation-like.

Why it hurts:

- Feels inserted rather than conversational.

Council support:

- Adam Grant: keep caveat, reduce density.
- Robert Cialdini: proof should support flow, not interrupt it.

Required fix:

- Keep one number and one caveat.
- Reduce clause count.

## Rewrite Rules (Humanity Pass)

1. Remove Trigger this week labels from all templates.
2. Remove Choice labels from all templates.
3. Open with one natural sentence tied to recipient context.
4. Keep one decision sentence in plain language.
5. Keep one short proof sentence with caveat.
6. Keep yes or pass CTA, but soften to conversational cadence.

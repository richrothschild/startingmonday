Review cadence: after each new proof asset collected

## Purpose

The three most critical missing trust items identified in Sprint ITS-1 audits are named proof assets for:
1. A coach (before/after workflow outcomes)
2. An executive (transition milestone or search outcome)
3. A partner/outplacement organization (cohort-level outcome)

This document defines what we need to collect, how to collect it, and what format to publish it in.

---

## Why Named Proof Is Required

Anonymous pilot data (n=27, directional cohort metrics) is sufficient to establish that the product works at a basic level. It is not sufficient to:
- Convert premium coaches who want peer-authority proof before recommending to clients.
- Satisfy enterprise partner procurement who need named case evidence.
- Support the $2,000–$8,000/month coach engagement price point where proof is a buying requirement.

The audit confirmed:
- Current named proof count: 0
- Current anonymous/directional proof count: 3 (two cohort references, one pilot summary)
- Target for Sprint ITS-2 completion: at least 1 named coach proof asset live

---

## Proof Asset Targets

### Asset Type 1: Named Coach Proof

What we need:
- A real coach willing to be named (full name or first name + practice description) in a brief case.
- Before/after framing on at least one of these: session prep time, context rebuild time, strategy-time ratio, client adoption rate.
- A short quote (1–2 sentences) the coach approves for use.

Format to collect:
- 15-minute interview (structured questions below).
- Coach approves written version before publication.
- Case appears on /for-coaches page or /case-studies page.

Interview questions:
1. Before you used Starting Monday, how much session time went to context rebuild and catching up?
2. What changed after the first two weeks?
3. What do your clients say when you introduce the prep brief?
4. Would you recommend it to a peer coach? Why?
5. Is there anything we can use publicly with your name on it?

Minimum viable proof block:
> "[Name], [title/practice description]: 'Before this, 20 minutes of every session went to catching up. Now I walk in knowing exactly where we left off. My clients feel it immediately.'"

### Asset Type 2: Named Executive Proof

What we need:
- A real executive willing to be named (or first name + role level + function).
- At least one measurable milestone: first interview timeline, number of target companies engaged, prep brief used before a key conversation.
- A short quote (1–2 sentences).

Format to collect:
- Brief email exchange or async written response (lower barrier than interview).
- Executive approves written version.
- Case appears on /case-studies or embedded in executive-facing pages.

Interview/async questions:
1. What were you trying to accomplish when you started the search?
2. What milestone are you most proud of in your first 30 days?
3. Did the prep brief or signal alerts change how you approached a specific conversation?
4. Can we use your name or initials and role level in a brief case study?

Minimum viable proof block:
> "[First name], [CxO-level], [industry]: 'I had a first interview within 12 days of setting up my pipeline. The prep brief made it feel like I had done the homework already.'"

---

### Asset Type 3: Named Partner/Outplacement Proof

What we need:
- A real outplacement firm, CHRO, or practice leader willing to be identified (organization name, or "a [size] outplacement firm in [region]").
- At least one cohort-level metric or counselor efficiency observation.
- A brief quote or case summary the org approves.

Format to collect:
- Structured written summary provided by program lead, reviewed and approved by partner.
- Published as case study with methodology disclosure.

Minimum viable proof block:
> "[Firm name or description]: 'Our counselors spend more time on strategy and less time catching up. The scorecard gives us visibility we didn't have in previous programs.'"

## Publication Checklist (per asset)

Before any named proof asset goes live:
- [ ] Subject has reviewed and approved exact wording in writing.
- [ ] Claim tier assigned (Tier 1 if quantified, Tier 2 if qualitative).
- [ ] Sample size or context disclosed inline.
- [ ] Date window noted.
- [ ] Asset linked from artifact register (docs/trust/artifact-ownership-register-2026-06-16.md).
- [ ] Content Lead and Product Lead signed off.

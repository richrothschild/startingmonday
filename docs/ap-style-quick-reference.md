# AP Style Quick Reference for Outreach and Guides

This summary covers the most relevant AP style rules for executive coach outreach, guides, and public-facing docs. For full details, consult your licensed AP Stylebook.

## General Principles
- Use concise, clear, and active language.
- Avoid jargon and overly technical terms.
- Prefer short paragraphs and sentences.
- Use the Oxford comma only when necessary for clarity.

## Capitalization
- Capitalize proper nouns, brand names, and formal titles when used before a name (e.g., President John Smith).
- Do not capitalize job titles after a name or when used alone (e.g., John Smith, president).
- Do not capitalize "internet," "website," or "email."

## Numbers
- Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.
- Always use numerals for ages, dates, times, addresses, and percentages.
- Use "percent" (not "%") in text.

## Dates and Times
- Use figures for dates and years (e.g., May 20, 2026).
- Do not use "st," "nd," "rd," or "th" with dates.
- Use a.m. and p.m. (lowercase, with periods).
- Write times as "9 a.m.," "10:30 p.m.," not "10:30pm."

## Punctuation
- Use a single space after a period.
- Do not use the Oxford comma unless needed for clarity.
- Use em dashes (—) without spaces for abrupt changes or emphasis.
- Use hyphens for compound adjectives before a noun (e.g., well-known coach).

## Titles and Headlines
- Capitalize only the first word and proper nouns in headlines.
- Do not use quotation marks for book, software, or report titles; use italics if possible.

## Abbreviations and Acronyms
- Spell out on first reference, then use the acronym (e.g., Chief Executive Officer, then CEO).
- Do not use periods in acronyms (e.g., CEO, not C.E.O.).

## Email and Digital
- Use "email" (not "e-mail").
- Use "website," "homepage," "internet."
- Use "login" (noun), "log in" (verb).

## Miscellaneous
- Use "more than" with numerals, not "over" (e.g., more than 20 coaches).
- Use "because" for causation, "since" for time.
- Avoid exclamation points in professional communication.

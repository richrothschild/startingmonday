## The Gap We See

Traditional outplacement is valuable. Experienced coaches, professional networks, interview preparation, and positioning guidance are things that senior executives benefit from and that human beings deliver better than software.

But traditional outplacement has structural limitations that no amount of coaching hours resolves:

**Intelligence latency**: The roles that matter at VP and C-suite level are filled before they are posted. By the time a career page listing is visible on LinkedIn or in a weekly digest, the preferred candidates are already in conversation. Traditional outplacement has no mechanism for early detection.

**Generic preparation**: A career consultant preparing a CIO client for an interview at a specific company produces a brief based on public information and experience. Starting Monday produces a brief built from the intersection of that specific company's current signals, the candidate's actual career record, and any documents attached — in 60 seconds, at a depth that would take a human researcher hours to replicate.

**Campaign collapse**: Executives in transition are disciplined professionals who quickly fall behind on the operational requirements of a sustained search — tracking companies, managing relationships, maintaining follow-up cadence. The spreadsheet approach fails at the 6-week mark for most candidates.

**Ongoing visibility**: Once a candidate is out of your formal program, you lose visibility into their progress. The relationship ends at contract close, not at offer acceptance.

Starting Monday addresses all four.

## What Starting Monday Does

**Automated company intelligence**: Career pages at up to 25 target companies (or unlimited at higher tiers) are scanned three times a week — before roles appear publicly. Company signals (funding, executive hires and departures, acquisitions, expansions) are monitored and classified by AI, with early alerts delivered each morning.

**60-second prep brief**: When a client schedules a call, they open the company workspace and generate a brief. It is built from the company's recent scans, intelligence signals, attached documents (job description, annual report, press), and the client's own career record. Not a generic summary — a brief that knows both sides of the conversation.

**Full campaign infrastructure**: Pipeline tracking, contact management, follow-up reminders, and a daily briefing ensure nothing falls through. The system runs in the background whether the client is actively working it or not.

**AI advisor with full context**: The assistant knows the client's resume, pipeline, and recent signals. It can help position for a specific role, draft outreach, tailor a resume version, and take operational actions directly.

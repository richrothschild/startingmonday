## The Gap We See

Traditional outplacement is valuable. Experienced coaches, professional networks, interview preparation, and positioning guidance are things that senior executives benefit from and that human beings deliver better than software.

But traditional outplacement has structural limitations that no amount of coaching hours resolves. The first is timing. The roles that matter at VP and C-suite level are filled before they are posted. By the time a career page listing appears on LinkedIn or in a weekly digest, the preferred candidates are already in conversation. Traditional outplacement has no mechanism for early detection.

The second is specificity. A career consultant preparing a CIO for an interview at a specific company produces a brief based on public information and experience. Starting Monday produces a brief built from the intersection of that company's current signals, the candidate's actual career record, and any documents attached, in sixty seconds, at a depth that would take a human researcher four hours to replicate.

The third is sustainability. Executives in transition are disciplined professionals who fall behind on the operational requirements of a sustained search faster than they expect. The tracking falls behind. The follow-up does not go out. The spreadsheet approach fails at the six-week mark for most candidates.

The fourth is visibility. Once a candidate exits your formal program, you lose sight of their progress. The relationship ends at contract close, not at offer acceptance. Starting Monday keeps the campaign running.

## What Starting Monday Does

Starting Monday watches target companies, prepares clients for every conversation, and runs the campaign infrastructure that most candidates cannot sustain manually.

Career pages at up to twenty-five target companies are scanned three times a week, before roles appear publicly. Company signals — funding rounds, executive hires and departures, acquisitions, expansions — are monitored and classified by AI, with early alerts delivered each morning. When a client schedules a call, they open the company workspace and generate a brief. It is built from the company's recent scans, intelligence signals, attached documents, and the client's own career record. Pipeline tracking, contact management, follow-up reminders, and a daily briefing ensure nothing falls through. The system runs whether the client is actively working it or not.

The AI advisor knows the client's resume, pipeline, and recent signals. It helps position for a specific role, draft outreach, tailor a resume version, and takes operational actions directly in conversation.

## The Client Experience

A VP of Finance joins your program. In the first week, she imports her LinkedIn profile, uploads her resume, and adds twelve target companies to her Starting Monday pipeline. The system begins monitoring those companies immediately.

Two weeks later, the system detects an open Controller role at one of her target companies, a role that has not yet appeared on LinkedIn. She gets a morning briefing with the scan result and a signal: the company raised a Series B three weeks ago.

She books a call with a contact at the company. Before the call, she opens Starting Monday, generates a prep brief, and reads a four-page document covering the company's recent growth trajectory, likely interview themes, her strongest positioning angle for this specific role, and suggested answers to the questions most likely to come up given her background.

A human researcher would have taken four hours to produce what she read in ten minutes. She walks in knowing more than the other candidates.

That is the experience every client can have, from day one of your program.

# Starting Monday — Per-Transition Pricing for VC and PE Firms

For operating partners and portfolio talent teams | May 2026

---

## The Situation

A portfolio company has a leadership transition. A new CIO, CTO, or VP Technology is going in. They have sixty to ninety days to build credibility, assess the stack, and start delivering.

That executive needs intelligence on their new company before day one. They need prep for every board meeting, every vendor negotiation, and every conversation with a leadership team that is watching closely. They need the operational infrastructure to run a high-stakes onboarding campaign without falling behind.

Starting Monday provides that infrastructure.

---

## What They Get

Starting Monday monitors the portfolio company and peer organizations for signals: executive movements, product announcements, funding activity, acquisitions, and structural changes. It generates prep briefs for every scheduled conversation using the executive's background documents and the company's current intelligence. It tracks contacts, follow-ups, and milestones so nothing falls through during the most important ninety days of the assignment.

The incoming executive walks in knowing more than the outgoing one did.

The setup fee covers onboarding, profile configuration, and company monitoring initialization. The operating partner or portfolio team can be involved in setup or leave it to the incoming executive.

Active is right for most transition placements. Executive adds daily scanning, a second scan at 20:00 UTC, and salary intelligence for executives managing compensation benchmarking in their first year.

There is no annual commitment. Billing continues month to month after the minimum, and cancels cleanly when the transition is complete.

---

## How It Works in Practice

An operating partner places a new CTO at a mid-market SaaS company. The day the offer is accepted, Starting Monday is set up: the incoming executive's background is loaded, the portfolio company is added alongside five peer companies, and monitoring begins.

For the next ninety days, the executive gets a morning briefing on company signals, a prep brief before every board meeting and key call, and an AI advisor that knows the full context of the role. The operating partner has visibility if needed. At day ninety, the transition is complete. The subscription cancels or continues at the executive's own discretion.

The portfolio company gets an executive who arrived prepared. The operating partner gets one fewer transition that goes sideways in the first ninety days.

---

## Why This Structure

Subscription pricing does not fit the transition use case. The value is concentrated in the first three months. After that the executive either has the relationships and intelligence infrastructure they need, or they are in trouble. Billing monthly with a three-month minimum reflects that.

The setup fee covers real work. Every placement has a different company, a different context, and a different executive background. Configuration is not automated.

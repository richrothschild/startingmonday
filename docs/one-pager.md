## What It Does

Starting Monday watches your target companies, prepares you for every conversation, and runs the campaign you cannot sustain manually.

It scans career pages at your target organizations three times a week, before roles appear on LinkedIn or job boards, and monitors company intelligence signals — funding rounds, executive hires and departures, acquisitions, expansions — the moment they surface. When a call is scheduled, you open the company workspace and generate a prep brief in sixty seconds. That brief is built from their recent signals, your resume, and any documents you have attached. It knows both sides of the conversation. Pipeline tracking, contact management, follow-up reminders, and a daily morning briefing replace the spreadsheet every executive builds and immediately falls behind on.

The AI advisor has full context: your resume, your pipeline, recent signals. It helps you think through positioning, draft outreach, tailor your resume for a specific role, and takes actions directly in conversation.

## Who It Is For

Senior professionals running a targeted search: VP, SVP, CIO, CTO, COO, CFO. Executives who are actively searching, quietly monitoring, or running a confidential campaign while still employed. The search Starting Monday is built for has fifteen to thirty target organizations, a small set of relationships that matter, and a first call where preparation is everything.

## Why Now

Executive search infrastructure has not changed in thirty years. Outplacement firms charge $10,000 to $50,000 and take weeks to produce a generic brief. Job boards index roles that have already been filled. The spreadsheet approach depends entirely on who you know and whether you remembered to follow up.

Starting Monday is what the process should have been: automated intelligence, precise preparation, and a campaign infrastructure that runs whether you are actively working it or not.

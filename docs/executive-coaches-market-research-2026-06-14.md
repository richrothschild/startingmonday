## 2) Market Context and Anchors

### Coaching Industry Backdrop

- International Coach Federation (ICF) estimates ~70,000+ credentialed coaches globally, ~25,000+ in U.S.
- Coaching market heavily skewed toward executive/leadership coaching (vs. life/wellness coaching)
- Average executive coach engagement: 6-12 months, 1-2 sessions/month
- Average cost: $2,000-$5,000/month ($150-$350/hour blended across engagements)
- Market is highly fragmented: ~80% solo/small-firm practitioners; ~20% large coaching firms

### Relationship to Starting Monday's Executive Job-Search Audience

Executives hiring coaches fall into two primary buckets:
1. **In-seat optimization** (80%): employed executives improving performance, leadership, board readiness
2. **Transition support** (20%): executives in active search, negotiating transitions, or post-placement onboarding

Starting Monday is most relevant to the **transition support** bucket and the **board-readiness** subset of in-seat work.

## 3) Executive Coaching Buyer Personas

### 3.1 The High-Potential Executive (In-Seat)

**Profile:**
- C-suite or senior VP track
- High performer, scope growing (or expected to grow)
- Employer or peer sponsorship for coaching
- Budget: often expense-managed, sometimes exec self-pays

**Why They Hire Coaches:**
- Board role preparation
- First-time CxO transition
- Executive presence/communication gaps
- Stakeholder alignment and influence
- Post-promotion onboarding
- Cross-functional leadership challenges

**Coach Hiring Trigger:**
- Promotion to new level
- Specific challenge (e.g., "board expectations mismatch", "CEO relationship tension")
- Peer recommendation ("my coach helped me with this exact thing")
- Board/board chair recommendation
- Executive coach outreach based on LinkedIn profile or industry visibility

**Decision Timeline:**
- Often fast: 1-2 weeks from trigger to first session
- Coach selection criteria: reputation, specific expertise, reference checks, rapport in first call

**Engagement Depth:**
- Coach often becomes trusted advisor and sounding board
- Coaches see executives 1-2x/month for 6-12 months
- High trust and confidentiality expectation
- Coaches often know about executive search activity before it's public

### 3.2 The Transitioning Executive (Active Search/Between Roles)

**Profile:**
- Currently searching or recently separated
- May be self-paying or using severance/search budget
- Often willing to invest $3,000-$8,000 for intensive 3-6 month engagement

**Why They Hire Coaches:**
- Interview preparation and narrative refinement
- Board fluency and director prep
- Personal brand and LinkedIn strategy
- Relationship warm-up campaigns
- Negotiation strategy
- Post-acceptance onboarding (80-day plan)

**Coach Hiring Trigger:**
- Severance package coach benefit (employer-funded, often negotiated)
- Realization that search is taking longer than expected
- Narrative confidence gap
- First rejection or round-advance miss
- Transition moment (leaving current role)

**Decision Timeline:**
- Often compressed: 1 week from trigger to engagement
- Coach selection based on search-specific expertise or industry/function familiarity
- Many transitioning execs will work with 1-2 coaches in parallel (career coach + search coach)

**Engagement Depth:**
- More intensive than in-seat: 2-4 sessions/month, higher urgency
- Coaches directly involved in interview prep, role-fit assessment, offer evaluation
- Short horizon (3-6 months) creates accountability and focus

### 3.3 The Agency Coach (Coaches Other Coaches)

**Profile:**
- Established individual practice (10-30 clients, 80-90% capacity)
- Often has waiting list and referral-based business
- May specialize in executive search, board prep, or transitions
- Typically not tech-enabled; relies on word-of-mouth and LinkedIn

**Why They Consider Partnerships:**
- Overflow referral management (too many inbound requests)
- Adding new tools to client diagnostic/prep toolkit
- Differentiation (offering "data-driven mandate evaluation" to search clients)
- Revenue share for referrals to specific tools/platforms

**Coach Hiring Trigger (for a partner platform):**
- Coaches see recurring search-related questions from clients
- Coaches want to add "market intelligence" layer to coaching relationships
- Coaches interested in tools that reduce their prep time (pre-work, diagnostics, intelligence)

**Engagement Depth:**
- Coaches are unlikely to be "users" of Starting Monday themselves
- But coaches recognize Starting Monday could be valuable input for their clients
- Open to revenue-share model if it strengthens client outcomes

## 4) Executive Coaching Market Sizing (U.S.)

### Model Approach

Using JOLTS separations data as demand signal for coaching engagement:

$$
\text{Monthly separations (execs)} = \text{Total nonfarm separations} \times \frac{\text{exec share}}{100}
$$

From JOLTS Apr 2026: 4.978M separations, ~7.8% are management/executive = ~389,000 annual.

Assumptions:
- Coaching adoption rate among displaced/transitioning execs: 15-35%
- Additional in-seat executives engaging coaches: ~2-3x the transitioning pool
- Coach engagements per year (new starts): 60,000-120,000

### Estimated Coaching Market Size (New Engagements Per Year)

**Low scenario:**
- Transitioning execs: 389,000 × 0.15 = 58,350
- In-seat execs: 58,350 × 2.0 = 116,700
- **Total: ~175,000 new coaching engagements/year**

**Base scenario:**
- Transitioning execs: 389,000 × 0.25 = 97,250
- In-seat execs: 97,250 × 2.5 = 243,125
- **Total: ~340,000 new coaching engagements/year**

**High scenario:**
- Transitioning execs: 389,000 × 0.35 = 136,150
- In-seat execs: 136,150 × 3.0 = 408,450
- **Total: ~544,000 new coaching engagements/year**

### Active Coaching Pool (Now)

Assuming average engagement length of 6-9 months:

- Low: ~88,000 active coaching relationships
- Base: ~170,000 active coaching relationships
- High: ~272,000 active coaching relationships

**Planning range: 80,000-250,000 executives actively in coaching right now**

## 17) Key Takeaway

Executive coaches represent a high-intent, fragmented partnership channel with 7.5K-21K annual addressable users. The coach channel is differentiated by coaches' direct access to executives in transition, their trusted advisor status, and their need for market intelligence to improve coaching outcomes. Revenue-share model with 50-100 partner coaches can yield $50K-$100K/month in new users by Q3 2026, with lower CAC and higher LTV than average channels due to coaches' existing client relationships.

# First Mile WBS: Starting Monday

Date: 2026-07-04
Source plan: [docs/first-visit-experience-review-2026-07-04.md](docs/first-visit-experience-review-2026-07-04.md)

## Objective

Make the first 1-3 visits feel obvious, luxurious, and immediately valuable. The user should quickly understand what to do, see the value of the site early, and feel like the product is already helping them build relationships and surface roles before they are posted.

## Workstreams

| Sprint | Epic | Goal | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sprint 1 | Measurement and Standards | Make the funnel measurable and align the canonical standards to the first-mile experience | Baseline funnel events exist; standards contracts approved; current claims are instrumentable |
| Sprint 2 | First Mile Onboarding | Turn setup into a value experience, not a form sequence | Resume/LinkedIn reflection, company suggestions, and step payoffs are visible in-flow |
| Sprint 3 | Signal-to-Role Discovery | Make the flagship claim visible through real signals and honest role hypotheses | A new user can see why a company is suggested and what role is likely forming |
| Sprint 4 | Relationships and Communications | Make relationship setup feel immediate and actionable | Suggested decision-makers and outreach drafts appear during week 1 |
| Sprint 5 | First-Run Arrival | Replace empty-dashboard friction with a guided ritual | First dashboard shows one clear next action and the brief the user just earned |
| Sprint 6 | Visual System and Proof | Add luxury-grade art direction and optional proof media | Public pages feel editorial, images are intentional, and proof is easy to discover |

## Epic Breakdown

### Epic 1: Measurement and Standards

Purpose: establish the baseline before changing the experience.

Tickets:
- Instrument landing, signup, onboarding, activation, and briefing events.
- Define first-mile KPIs and weekly reporting targets.
- Update landing standards with choice curation, arrival, value-moment, claim-integrity, imagery, and video contracts.

Success criteria:
- Every critical first-mile step has a measurable event.
- Baseline numbers exist for visit survival, onboarding completion, activation, and return.
- The standards document explicitly covers the first-mile experience.

### Epic 2: First Mile Onboarding

Purpose: show value during setup instead of after setup.

Tickets:
- Synthesize resume and LinkedIn uploads into a short positioning reflection.
- Replace static company seeds with resume-informed suggestions and a one-line why for each.
- Turn every onboarding step into a numbered value milestone with a payoff screen.
- Reduce choice load on homepage, signup, and role selection.

Success criteria:
- Suggested companies feel personally relevant.
- The user sees what the system understood before they finish setup.
- Every step ends with a payoff, not a blank submission state.

### Epic 3: Signal-to-Role Discovery

Purpose: make "roles before they are posted" visible and credible.

Tickets:
- Show live signal snapshots immediately after a company is accepted.
- Add a Roles Forming surface that translates company signals into honest role hypotheses.
- Trigger career-page scanning as soon as a company is accepted.
- Add clear confidence language so the product does not overclaim.

Success criteria:
- A new user can see why a role might be forming before any public post exists.
- The flagship promise is represented by real surfaces, not only marketing copy.

### Epic 4: Relationships and Communications

Purpose: make the user feel the system helping them build relationships, not only track companies.

Tickets:
- Surface Apollo-enriched decision-makers for the first company.
- Show scanner-suggested people from company signals.
- Add a first-note draft and follow-up sequence at the point of first value.

Success criteria:
- Week-1 users can identify who to contact and what to say next.
- Outreach is visible as a guided action, not hidden behind a separate prep area.

### Epic 5: First-Run Arrival

Purpose: replace empty-state confusion with a clear concierge moment.

Tickets:
- Replace empty dashboard charts with an itinerary-style first-run view.
- Show the first brief, the first companies, and one suggested action.
- Add a return ritual and better onboarding nudges.

Success criteria:
- The user lands on a page that tells them what to do next.
- The dashboard shows already-earned value rather than only empty containers.

### Epic 6: Visual System and Proof

Purpose: make the site feel like a luxury editorial product.

Tickets:
- Define and source a small photography system for the public pages.
- Decide where a short optional proof film is appropriate, if at all.
- Promote the live demo as proof at the first skepticism moment.

Success criteria:
- Public pages look intentionally editorial, not generic SaaS.
- Visuals support the narrative instead of competing with it.
- Any video shipped is supplementary, captioned, and campaign-grade.

## Ticket Success Criteria Template

Use this template for every Jira ticket created from this WBS:

- Visible proof: what the user sees on screen.
- Metric: the leading measure that should improve.
- Guardrail: what must not regress.
- Exit test: how we know the ticket is done.

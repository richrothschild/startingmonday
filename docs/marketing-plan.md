# Starting Monday — Marketing Plan

**startingmonday.app** | Last updated: May 2026

---

## Strategic Position

Starting Monday does not market to people looking for jobs. It markets to senior professionals running a search campaign — a meaningfully different audience with a meaningfully different psychology.

The language is campaign language, not job-seeker language. The tone is operational, not motivational. The product does not celebrate effort; it produces outcomes. Every marketing touchpoint should reinforce that Starting Monday is infrastructure for a serious search, not a tool to apply faster.

**One-sentence positioning**: Starting Monday is the operating system for a senior professional's job search — intelligence before roles are posted, precise relationship management, and preparation that wins the first conversation.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Active VP/C-Suite Candidates

- Title range: VP, SVP, CIO, CTO, COO, CFO, Chief [function] Officer, General Manager
- Compensation: $250K–$1M+ total comp
- Search status: actively searching or open to the right opportunity
- Pain points: manual tracking, lack of early intelligence, generic interview prep, feeling outpaced by candidates with better networks
- Where they are: LinkedIn (passive scroll, not active), referral networks, professional associations, alumni networks, outplacement programs

### Secondary: Passive Monitors

- Same title range, not actively searching
- Willing to pay $49/month to stay informed and keep relationships warm
- Convert to Active at the moment they decide to commit

### Tertiary: Level-Up Candidates (Director → VP, VP → CIO)

- Ready for the promotion that requires changing companies
- More uncertain about positioning; higher need for the strategy brief and AI chat

## Go-to-Market: Phase 1 (Current — Alpha/Beta)

**Objective**: Land 25 paying customers who represent the core persona. Understand what creates the "this is exactly what I needed" moment. Collect stories.

**Channels**:
1. **Direct personal network** (primary): The founder is the ideal user. Every conversation with a peer who says "I'm in search" is a direct conversion opportunity. This is not a referral program — it is the founder selling personally to people they know and respect.

2. **LinkedIn personal brand** (building): The founder posts about the realities of senior search — not tips, not job advice, but observations that resonate with executives who have been through it. The product is mentioned in context, not promoted.

3. **Targeted outreach to outplacement alumni**: Former executives who have been through Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, or similar programs know the gap — expensive, slow, generic. A direct, honest message about what Starting Monday does differently converts well.

4. **University alumni networks** (near-term): Alumni career offices at top business schools facilitate introductions for job seekers. A partnership or featured listing gets Starting Monday in front of active candidates at the right level. Initial targets: Wake Forest, Stanford, Wharton, Harvard Business School alumni career programs.

## Go-to-Market: Phase 2 (Months 3–6)

**Objective**: Establish a repeatable acquisition channel. 100 paying customers, meaningful word-of-mouth.

### Content Strategy

**Positioning**: Starting Monday is a credible voice on executive search — not career tips, but the process, the intelligence, and the preparation.

**Content types**:
- **LinkedIn articles**: "What I learned tracking 40 companies for 6 months" / "The prep brief that changed how I walked into interviews" / "Why the role you want isn't on LinkedIn" — written in first person by the founder or early users
- **Case studies**: Anonymous case studies of users who converted a cold signal into a first call, or who walked into a prep brief and got the offer. The story is always the same: preparation + timing + positioning.
- **Teardowns**: "Here's what Starting Monday saw at [company] three weeks before the role was posted" — demonstrates the product's intelligence advantage in concrete terms

**SEO targets**: "executive job search tools," "CIO job search," "VP job search preparation," "interview prep for executives," "how to track job opportunities" — long-tail, low-competition, high-intent queries.

### Referral Activation

Every successful user who gets an offer is a referral opportunity. The ask: "Who else do you know who is in search or thinking about it?" One introduction from a satisfied CIO is worth more than 10 LinkedIn posts.

Structure a formal (but light) referral program: one free month for the referrer, one free month added to the referred user's trial.

## Go-to-Market: Phase 3 (Months 6–12)

**Objective**: Establish Starting Monday as the default platform for serious executive search. 500 customers. Establish the Executive and Campaign waitlists as meaningful pipelines.

### Partnership: Manager Tools / Career Tools Ecosystem

Mark Horstman and Michael Auzenne (Manager Tools) have built the largest audience of managers and executives focused on professional effectiveness. Their audience is Starting Monday's audience — people who think systemically about their careers and take professional development seriously.

**Proposed approach**: A direct conversation with Mark Horstman about whether Starting Monday fits the Manager Tools audience. Not a sponsorship pitch — a conversation between professionals about whether the product is advice-defensible. Manager Tools does not endorse anything they haven't vetted; that rigor is what makes their recommendations credible.

If it fits, the options are: podcast episode, newsletter mention, affiliate arrangement. The key is that the recommendation is genuine — Manager Tools listeners will convert because they trust Mark's judgment, not because of an ad.

**What needs to be true first**: The product needs to be stable, the user experience needs to be excellent, and there need to be enough user success stories to anchor the conversation.

### Outplacement Partnerships

Enterprise outplacement firms (Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, Challenger Gray & Christmas) provide services to companies transitioning executives. They are increasingly looking for technology to modernize their offering. A white-label or partner arrangement gives Starting Monday access to large cohorts of job-seeking executives at the moment of peak motivation.

**Structure**: Revenue share on conversions, or a flat per-user fee for the outplacement partner's client base.

### Executive Association Sponsorships

Professional associations for CIOs, CTOs, and other C-suite functions have newsletters, events, and member communications. Sponsorship of the right association newsletter ($2,000–$5,000/month) reaches a highly targeted audience at significantly lower CPL than broad digital advertising.

**Targets**: EVANTA (CIO/CISO network), The CIO Network, SIM (Society for Information Management), CIO.com newsletter.

## Pricing Strategy

**Current pricing is below market**. For a professional earning $400K+, $199/month is less than one hour of their time. The framing should never be "is this affordable?" — it should be "is this worth it?"

**Anchor on the downside**: A mis-positioned search at the executive level adds 3–6 months to the timeline. At $400K annual comp, that is $100K–$200K in foregone earnings. Starting Monday at $199/month for 6 months is $1,200.

**Do not discount**. Offering discounts to the target audience signals that the product is not confident in its value. The free trial is the conversion mechanism — not price reduction.

**Quarterly billing** (currently at $132/$537) reduces churn and rewards commitment. Promote quarterly as the default for engaged users.

**Executive and Campaign waitlists** serve a dual purpose: they signal that the product is in demand at those tiers, and they create a pipeline of self-identified high-value customers who are ready to convert when the tier launches.

## Messaging Framework

### What We Say

- "Your next role isn't on a job board."
- "At this level, the search is a campaign, not an application."
- "Know before anyone else."
- "Walk in prepared."
- "The best CIO mandates are created, not posted."
- "30-day pilot. No credit card."

### What We Do Not Say

- "Land your dream job" — generic, doesn't fit the audience
- "Apply to hundreds of jobs faster" — volume is the wrong frame
- "AI-powered resume builder" — undersells the product, wrong category
- "Career coaching" — implies dependence on someone else's judgment
- "Job board" — never

### Tone

Spare. Direct. Confident. The copy should read like it was written by someone who has run a search at this level and knows exactly what the audience is dealing with. No exclamation points. No "excited to announce." No emoji in marketing materials.

## The Founder's Unfair Advantage

The founder is an active CIO in search — the exact target user. This is not a theoretical product built for an assumed persona. It is a tool built by and for someone running the exact process the product is designed to support.

That authenticity is the product's most powerful marketing asset. Every conversation, post, and case study should draw on that credibility. The marketing is not "we built a tool for executives in search." The marketing is "I was in search, this is what I built to run it, and it works."

That story is rare. Use it.

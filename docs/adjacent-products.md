# Starting Monday — Adjacent Product Opportunities

Internal document | May 2026

---

Three ideas generated during the stickiness strategy sessions belong in a separate product rather than as Starting Monday features. Each is strong enough to build. Each would dilute Starting Monday's core positioning if built inside it. And each is uniquely positioned to be built by Starting Monday because of the data, the relationships, and the user base the core product accumulates.

This document fleshes each one out enough for the right meetings.

---

## Product 1: Executive Market Intelligence Subscription

### The Problem

A CIO who accepts an offer does not stop needing intelligence. They need to know what their competitors are doing, which executives are moving in their industry, which companies are making acquisitions that affect their market, and which PE firms are building positions in their sector. Right now, they piece this together from Google alerts, industry newsletters, and LinkedIn — all of which are generic, noisy, and not calibrated to their specific situation.

There is no market intelligence product built for the individual senior executive. Bloomberg Terminal is built for financial professionals in institutional contexts. CB Insights and PitchBook are built for investors. Gartner and Forrester are built for budget decisions. Nothing exists for the CIO who wants to stay ahead of their market without spending 3 hours a week assembling a picture that is already out of date.

### The Buyer

An employed senior executive — CIO, CTO, CFO, COO — earning $250,000 or more, currently in a role, who wants to be the most informed person in the room about their market and their competitive context. They are not searching. They are managing.

This is a completely different buyer psychology from Starting Monday's active search user. The urgency is lower, the use case is ongoing, and the value is cumulative rather than acute. Willingness to pay: $99 to $149 per month. The buyer does not need a discount — they need a credible reason to believe the intelligence is curated for their specific situation rather than being a generic feed.

### How It Differs from Starting Monday

Starting Monday is built around the search. The pipeline, the contacts, the prep brief, the strategy — everything is oriented toward finding and winning a role. The market intelligence product is built around the role you already have. The monitoring is not of target companies for potential employers — it is of the competitive landscape of your current industry and function.

The data inputs overlap significantly (company signals, executive movements, funding events) but the framing, the delivery mechanism, and the interpretation layer are different. Where Starting Monday's signal says "this company may have a need for someone like you," the market intelligence product says "this development in your market is worth knowing about because of what it means for your organization."

### Market Size

Approximately 2 million senior executives in the US at VP and above hold roles where ongoing market intelligence is genuinely useful and where $99 to $149 per month is a rounding error in their professional budget. The segment willing to pay for curated, role-specific intelligence — rather than assembling it themselves — is probably 3 to 5 percent of that, or 60,000 to 100,000 potential subscribers. At $1,200 per year average, that is $72M to $120M in addressable revenue.

### Starting Monday's Distribution Advantage

Every executive who completes a search on Starting Monday is a warm lead for the market intelligence product. They already know the product, they have already experienced the intelligence quality, and they have just accepted a role where staying informed matters. The post-completion email sequence is the entry point. "You are employed. The intelligence does not stop being useful." Alumni mode, if built, is the bridge. The market intelligence subscription is the destination.

### Build Trigger

Do not build this before Starting Monday has 500 paying users and at least 100 alumni (offer accepted) in the system. The distribution advantage requires a meaningful alumni base. The intelligence infrastructure (signal classification, company monitoring) already exists — the product work is in building the employed-user framing, the delivery format, and the pricing page. Estimated build time once triggered: one sprint for the concept, two to three sprints for the first version.

## Product 2: Board Positioning Platform

### The Problem

The path from CIO or CFO to a board seat is not a search — it is a multi-year cultivation process with no public listings, no standard process, and almost no infrastructure to support the candidate. Board vacancies are filled through relationships, governance committee referrals, and executive search firms who specialize in board placement. The average time from "I want a board seat" to first board seat, for a qualified candidate, is three to seven years.

The executive pursuing a board seat has two problems. First, they need to know when board composition is changing at the companies they are targeting — new vacancies, governance shifts, PE portfolio transitions that require independent directors. This intelligence is scattered across proxy filings, governance databases, and news sources that require significant time to monitor. Second, they need to build and maintain relationships with the people who make board recommendations — sitting directors, PE operating partners, governance committee chairs — over a time horizon that defeats the attention span of any manual tracking system.

### The Buyer

A CIO, CFO, or COO with 15 or more years of executive experience who is either actively pursuing a first board seat or building toward one in the next three to five years. This is a smaller, older, and wealthier audience than Starting Monday's core search persona. They are not looking for a job — they are managing a multi-year strategic positioning effort. Willingness to pay: $199 to $499 per month. The buyer is motivated by career legacy and status as much as by compensation. The product must reflect that.

### How It Differs from Starting Monday

Board search is a different category of professional pursuit. The timeline is years, not months. The "roles" are governance positions, not employment positions. The intelligence needed (proxy filings, board composition, governance committee structure, PE portfolio company board formation) is different in kind from the career page scans and executive departure signals that power Starting Monday. The relationship network is different — sitting directors, institutional investors, governance search specialists — not the HR leaders and PE operating partners of executive search.

Building this as a feature of Starting Monday would create a product that is confusing to position, expensive to build for a minority of users, and unlikely to be priced correctly within the existing tier structure. Built as a standalone product, it can be priced and positioned for its specific buyer with full clarity.

### Market Size

Approximately 50,000 to 80,000 senior US executives are actively pursuing or positioning for board seats at any given time. At $300 per month average, that is $180M to $288M in addressable annual revenue. The market is small but the willingness to pay is high — executive coaching for board positioning runs $500 to $1,000 per hour, and governance search firms charge $50,000 to $150,000 per placement.

### Starting Monday's Distribution Advantage

The Transformation CIO and Campaign candidate personas are the exact audience for this product. Starting Monday's intelligence infrastructure (company monitoring, signal classification, executive departure tracking) covers roughly 70 percent of what the board positioning product needs. The PE firm and board composition intelligence planned for the Executive tier is essentially the foundation of this product. The board positioning platform is what the Executive tier becomes if it is built as a standalone product rather than a premium feature.

### Build Trigger

Do not build this before the Executive tier is live and validated. If the Executive tier's board and PE intelligence features are the most-cited reason users choose that tier, that is the signal that a standalone board positioning product has a market. The first version could be the Executive tier re-skinned with a longer time horizon and a different onboarding — "I am not searching, I am positioning." Estimated build time once triggered: two to three sprints for the first version if the Executive tier infrastructure exists.

## Product 3: Confidential Executive Network

### The Problem

Senior executives in search or between mandates are isolated by design. The confidential nature of the search means they cannot be publicly visible as candidates. They cannot compare notes with peers without risking exposure. They cannot get warm introductions to other candidates watching the same companies without a trusted intermediary. They are running identical searches in parallel, making the same mistakes, watching the same companies, and competing for the same roles — with no mechanism to know any of this.

The executive who is watching Acme Corp and knows that two other senior technology leaders are also watching Acme Corp — one of whom is her former colleague's former boss — has fundamentally different intelligence than the one who is watching in isolation. The introduction that happens because both are in the Starting Monday network is a relationship that would not exist otherwise.

### The Buyer

The same person as Starting Monday's core user, but during the search rather than before or after it. The value proposition is specifically for the active search phase: warm, confidential peer connections around shared target companies, industries, and functions. No public profile. No LinkedIn-style feed. No visibility unless both parties explicitly agree to an introduction.

The buyer does not pay extra for this — it is a retention and engagement feature of the Active plan that makes Starting Monday more valuable than any individual alternative. The network effect means the more users in the system, the more valuable each individual subscription becomes.

### How It Differs from Starting Monday

This is a social product built on top of a search infrastructure product. Social products have fundamentally different engineering requirements (real-time notifications, privacy architecture, moderation, trust systems), different growth mechanics (network effects require critical mass before value exists), and different failure modes (a single confidentiality breach in this community is catastrophic).

Building this correctly requires treating it as a product-within-a-product: separate design review, separate trust architecture, separate launch criteria. It cannot be an afterthought feature. If the peer network is built badly — if a user's confidential search is inadvertently visible to someone in their professional network — the damage to Starting Monday's core brand is severe and fast-spreading in a small community.

### Market Size

The network effect makes market size less relevant than critical mass. A confidential network with 50 nodes has no value. A confidential network with 500 nodes in similar industries and functions has genuine value for every member. The threshold for value is approximately 100 active users in enough proximity (industry, function, geography) to produce meaningful matches. Starting Monday needs 500 to 1,000 active users before this product is worth building.

### Starting Monday's Distribution Advantage

This is the only adjacent product that does not require a separate acquisition strategy. Every Starting Monday user is a potential network node. The opt-in mechanism is simple — "Would you like to be connected with other Starting Monday users watching similar companies?" — and the matching logic runs on data that already exists in the system. No new acquisition is required to launch the network; only a sufficient density of existing users.

### Build Trigger

500 active users minimum. The trust architecture must be designed before a single user opts in — not retrofitted after. The design question that must be answered first: what does each user actually see about another user, and under what conditions? The answer determines the entire architecture. Do not build before that question has a written, reviewed answer. Estimated build time once triggered: three to four sprints for a minimal version with proper trust design.

## What to Say in Meetings

When a prospective investor, partner, or recruiter asks about long-term ambition: "Starting Monday is the search infrastructure product. We see three adjacent opportunities — a market intelligence subscription for employed executives, a board positioning platform for the governance track, and a confidential peer network — each of which leverages the data and user base we are building now. We are not building any of them yet. We are building the infrastructure that makes all three possible."

When a user asks if Starting Monday can help them pursue a board seat: "We track board composition changes and PE portfolio transitions in the Executive tier, which is the intelligence foundation. The full board positioning product is on our roadmap. If this is important to you, get on the waitlist."

When someone asks about the network: "It requires critical mass to be useful. We will build it when the user base supports it. Right now, the most powerful network feature we offer is the intelligence that gets you into conversations before anyone else knows they're happening."

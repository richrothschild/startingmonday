# Roadmap Vision Memo - John McMahon

Council: Sales and Marketing
Date: 2026-05-18
Lens summary: Urgency tied to economic pain drives action.

## Executive View

Starting Monday should prioritize roadmap investments that increase customer trust, shorten time-to-value, and improve sustained usage outcomes for both individual executives and partner channels. From this lens, the core principle is straightforward: roadmap quality is measured by customer progress, not feature count. The roadmap should preserve strategic focus while creating compounding advantage in product quality, go-to-market relevance, and retention economics.

The immediate objective is to raise conversion quality without creating operational fragility. That means protecting reliability baselines, reducing friction in activation and sign-in paths, and tightening narrative clarity so the right users self-select quickly. The broader objective is to build a growth system where B2C activation and B2B distribution reinforce each other over time.

## Why This Matters for Acquisition

Acquisition performance improves most when positioning, product onboarding, and channel strategy are aligned. The roadmap should therefore concentrate on initiatives that create clear buyer intent and low-friction first value. For B2C, this requires reducing initial confusion and helping users complete the first high-value actions quickly. For B2B, this requires channel-specific proof, repeatable partner workflows, and better signal-to-conversation relevance.

From this member's perspective, the most important acquisition bets are: Pain-led qualification, urgency copy, buyer alignment. These should be converted into testable programs with explicit baseline metrics and cycle-time targets so decisions can be made quickly and objectively.

## Why This Matters for Retention

Retention is a function of repeated value realization. Users stay when the product consistently improves decision confidence and execution quality. The roadmap should prioritize mechanisms that make value recurring and cumulative: intelligent prompts, better defaults, reliability discipline, and deeper context-aware outputs. For B2B channels, retention should be tracked through seat activation quality, partner expansion, and recurring referral behavior.

The retention constraint to manage is: Informative but non-converting content. Any initiative that increases this risk should be redesigned before broad rollout.

## 12-Month Roadmap Recommendations

### 1) First 90 days

- Strengthen activation and first-session guidance for high-value actions.
- Remove login and identity friction through provider-first paths and recovery options.
- Keep reliability guardrails and synthetic coverage strict on P0 journeys.

### 2) Months 3 to 6

- Operationalize channel-specific B2B partner playbooks.
- Expand trigger-based outbound and partner attribution measurement.
- Implement weekly decision dashboards for acquisition and retention cohorts.

### 3) Months 6 to 12

- Deepen product quality loops tied to measurable retention deltas.
- Expand premium workflows where outcome improvement is provable.
- Harden partner operating model to support scale without trust erosion.

## Decision Criteria and Governance

Every initiative should include owner, KPI, expected value, review date, and kill criteria. Use monthly probability updates and disconfirming evidence to rebalance the portfolio. Reversible bets should be tested early; irreversible bets should require stronger proof and a pre-mortem.

## Success Metrics

- B2C: visitor-to-trial, six-action completion in 7 days, D30 and D90 retention.
- B2B: partner-sourced opportunities, pilot-to-paid conversion, 90-day seat retention.
- Economics: channel payback, gross margin by segment, net revenue retention.
- Trust: critical-path pass rate, incident rates, auth recovery success.

## Final Recommendation

Adopt a focused roadmap that compounds customer trust and distribution leverage before broad expansion. The best execution path is to align product quality, channel strategy, and decision governance around measurable customer progress. For this lens, the strategic emphasis remains: Urgency tied to economic pain drives action.

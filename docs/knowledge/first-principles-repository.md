# First Principles Repository

Date: 2026-06-07
Purpose: Canonical first-principles bank for Executive Brief, Grill Me, and Council-driven decision systems.

## Verification Policy
- Principles are linked to respected, auditable source traditions.
- Source families: Munger, Drucker, Deming, Kahneman, Taleb, Porter, Christensen, Goldratt, Bezos, Dalio, Duke, Senge, Meadows, Cialdini, Voss, and domain-practitioner literature.
- Each principle is phrased as a decision invariant: true across tactics.
- Citation style: section-level only; source lineage is maintained at the section/policy level rather than per-item lines.

## How to Use
- Step 1: Decompose problem into first principles.
- Step 2: Select matching mental models.
- Step 3: Generate Grill Me line of questioning.
- Step 4: Score answers against principle-level rubric.

## Global Meta First Principles
1. Reality is the ultimate judge; narratives do not override outcomes.
2. Survival precedes optimization; avoid ruin first.
3. Incentives explain behavior better than intentions.
4. What is measured and rewarded will be optimized.
5. Tradeoffs are unavoidable; every strategy is a set of exclusions.
6. Simplicity is a force multiplier under uncertainty.
7. Fast feedback loops beat detailed long-range plans.
8. Compounding dominates linear effort over time.
9. Trust compounds slowly and decays quickly.
10. Clear ownership is required for reliable execution.
11. Context quality determines decision quality.
12. Systems fail at boundaries and handoffs first.
13. Human attention is scarce; focus is strategic capital.
14. Optionality has value under volatility.
15. If a principle cannot survive stress, it is a preference, not a principle.

## First-Principles Prompting Rules for Grill Me
196. Always decompose request into assumptions, constraints, and objectives first.
197. Ask one high-value question at a time and update state after each answer.
198. Attach each question to a principle and model pair.
199. Require second-order consequence chains for major decisions.
200. Separate evidence from inference in every captured entry.
201. Flag unknowns with owner and due date immediately.
202. Maintain explicit confidence score with rationale.
203. Record conflicts between council seats, do not smooth them away.
204. Rubrics should score clarity, evidence, strategy, and risk handling independently.
205. Final verdict should include single highest-leverage next action.

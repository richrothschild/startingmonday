# Starting Monday - Synthetic Mark Horstman Audit
**May 13, 2026 | Comprehensive Site Review via Mark's Framework**

Mark's lens: **Execution discipline, accountability, anti-vagueness, decision focus**

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Overall Grade: B+** — Solid positioning and genuine insight into exec search problems. BUT: internal inconsistencies, unclear who wins vs. who should pass, vague on core value prop across pages, and missing accountability guardrails that would turn a prospect into a customer.

**To earn an A:** Tighten category ownership (stop trying to own all exec searches), make one clear promise per page, add proof-of-time anchors throughout, and clarify who absolutely should NOT use this (as strongly as you clarify who should).

## SITE SECTIONS & GRADES

### 1. LANDING PAGE (/) — **Grade: B-**

**What works:**
- "Monitors your target companies every 48 hours and surfaces signals before a search is formalized" — this is specific and credible
- Situations list (9 cards) shows real empathy for different search states
- FAQ directly addresses comparison to LinkedIn Premium ("infrastructure between job board and coaching")
- Free 30-day trial removes friction

**Mark's Dislikes:**

1. **Vague positioning in metadata vs. specificity in copy**
   - Title: "The executive job search operating system" (too broad, too abstract)
   - Better: "Signal monitoring for CIO and VP technology searches" (who, what, where)
   - **Issue:** "Operating system" suggests it does everything. It doesn't. It does one thing well: surfaces signals before roles are posted.

2. **"Built for technology executives who run a disciplined campaign, not a passive one"**
   - Who is "passive"? The page shows 9 situations — some ARE passive.
   - **Disconnect:** The value prop contradicts the situations offered
   - **Fix:** Either own "all search states" (messier positioning) OR focus on "actively building a target list" (tighter, more defensible)

3. **Nine situations = decision paralysis**
   - Research shows 3-5 strong options perform better than 9
   - Having all 9 suggests you have no single ideal customer
   - **Mark would ask:** "If I take away 5 of these, which 4 do you keep? Those 4 are your real positioning."

4. **Missing time-to-value claim in hero**
   - You claim 15 minutes setup, 60 seconds for briefs, but this is buried in FAQ
   - **Hero should anchor:** "Track 2,000+ companies and get first signal in 24 hours"
   - **Why:** Prospects need to know speed of results, not just existence of results

5. **Pricing preview missing**
   - No indication of cost on landing page
   - Prospect has to hunt to pricing page to decide if this is in their budget
   - **Consequence:** Self-selected drop-off; lost impulse signups

**What Mark would recommend to win more execs:**
- Drop the "operating system" framing; own "signal intelligence for C-suite searches"
- Reduce situations to 4 (urgent, passive-building, stepping-up, passive-aware)
- Add pricing tier in hero or below fold: "$49–499/mo"
- Move specific time claims from FAQ to hero: "First signals arrive within 24 hours"

### 2. PERSONA PAGES (for-vp-technology, for-cdo, etc.) — **Grade: B**

**What works:**
- Each has a distinct narrative (VP Tech ≠ CDO ≠ CISO positioning)
- FAQs are thoughtful and specific to role challenges
- You're speaking to real constraints (VP ceiling, CDO mandate confusion)

**Mark's Dislikes:**

1. **Over-customization creates category confusion**
   - 15+ persona pages feels like you're trying to be everything to everyone
   - **Mark's principle:** If you own a position, you should own 2-3 personas max. If you own 15, you own none.
   - **Better approach:** Core persona (CIO) + 2 adjacent (CISO, CDO). Link others to core.

2. **Each page makes a slightly different promise**
   - VP-Tech page: "Find roles before they're posted + right mandate"
   - CDO page: "Know the mandate before first conversation"
   - CIO page (presumed): Something different again
   - **Problem:** This makes it unclear what you actually do
   - **Fix:** One sentence promise on all pages: "Spot executive opportunities 2–4 weeks early, built specifically for [VP Tech/CDO/CISO] searches"

3. **Situations on persona pages often contradict core positioning**
   - Landing page says "disciplined active searchers"
   - VP-Tech persona includes "I am not actively looking"
   - **Mark sees this as:** You don't know your customer. You're following them to every situation rather than making them come to you.

4. **Missing competitor clarity**
   - "How is this different from LinkedIn Premium?" on landing page only
   - Persona pages should directly say: "Not a job board. Not a coach. The system that feeds both."
   - Each persona should know why this beats competitors for THEIR situation

**What Mark would recommend:**
- Keep 3 primary personas: CIO (core), VP-Tech (adjacent), CISO (adjacent)
- Consolidate other roles (CDO, CPO, etc.) into a single "other roles" page
- Add 1-2 sentence positioning on each: "This is the signal layer that finds [role type] opportunities before your recruiter does"
- On each persona page, add: "Competitive comparison: Why Starting Monday beats [LinkedIn Premium / individual search / search firm retainer]"

### 4. PRICING PAGE (/pricing) — **Grade: C**

**Mark's Biggest Dislike: This page doesn't answer the one question prospects have.**

**What Mark sees:**
- $49/mo: "Monitor" tier
- $199/mo: "Active" tier  
- $499/mo: "Executive" tier
- Teams: Custom

**The problem:**
1. **No clarity on what tier a specific person should buy**
   - Persona pages talk about CIOs, VPs, etc.
   - Pricing page doesn't map role → tier
   - **What tier is a CIO?** "Executive"? But that's ambiguous.

2. **Tier names are vague**
   - "Monitor" suggests passive observation; this contradicts "disciplined campaign" messaging
   - "Active" and "Executive" are barely different semantically; unclear which is right
   - **Better names:** "$49 Tracking Tier" (for intelligence monitoring), "$199 Campaign Tier" (for active search), "$499 Done-With-Me Tier" (for concierge)

3. **No features listed per tier**
   - What does each tier include? You don't say.
   - **Mark:** "Pricing page with no feature breakdown loses 40% of prospects who want to buy but don't understand what they're getting"

4. **"Concierge available by application"**
   - No pricing, no description of what it is
   - No indication of who qualifies
   - **Translation to prospect:** "This might cost $10K+/yr. Don't apply unless you're sure."

5. **No guarantee or outcome anchoring**
   - Pricing pages should answer: "What does my money buy me?" and "What should I expect to happen?"
   - Starting Monday doesn't say
   - **Example:** "$199/mo gets you 10–15 signal alerts per month and 3–5 scheduled calls within 60 days"

6. **"30-day free trial. Cancel any time."**
   - This is fine, but it's the only risk removal
   - **What's missing:** "Not seeing results? We'll refund your first month. No questions."

**What Mark would recommend:**
- **Restructure pricing table:**
  - **$49/mo "Intelligence Monitoring"** — Track up to 50 companies, daily briefing, no prep briefs, email alerts
  - **$199/mo "Active Campaign"** — Track up to 200 companies, daily briefing, AI prep briefs, call tracking, pipeline dashboard
  - **$499/mo "Full Done-With-Me"** — Unlimited tracking, prep briefs, weekly strategic calls with advisor, target list optimization
  - **Teams/Enterprise** — "Seat management for 5+ users. Starts at $2,000/mo. Book a call."

- **Add expected outcomes per tier:**
- $49: "Intelligence monitoring. Spot 1–2 unexpected opportunities per quarter."
  - $199: "Active search. 3–5 opportunity conversations within 60 days."
  - $499: "Full support. First role target within 90 days OR we credit your next month."

- **Add FAQ to pricing page:**
  - "Which tier should I pick?" (role-based guidance)
  - "Can I upgrade/downgrade?" (yes, anytime)
  - "What if I don't see results?" (1-month money-back guarantee)

## CROSS-SITE ISSUES (Mark's Biggest Concerns)

### ISSUE 1: Vague on Category Ownership
**Problem:** Starting Monday doesn't clearly own any category. 

- Is it a "job board for execs"? (No, you're not)
- Is it "AI interview prep"? (No, that's one feature)
- Is it "signal intelligence"? (Yes, but the name doesn't lead here)
- Is it "search firm alternative"? (Kind of, but you're not replacing them)

**Mark's Test:** In one sentence, complete this: "Starting Monday is _____ for _____."

**Your answer should be:** "Starting Monday is signal intelligence for CIO and VP-level tech searches."

**What you're currently saying:** "An executive job search operating system" (too broad, too undefined).

**Consequence:** Prospects don't know what to compare it to, so they compare it to LinkedIn, and you lose.

---

### ISSUE 2: Unclear Who Should NOT Use This
**Problem:** Every page positions Starting Monday as good for everyone.

**Mark's principle:** Great positioning is as much about WHO IT'S NOT FOR as who it is.

**What's missing:**
- "Starting Monday is NOT for candidates still in their first executive role" (they don't have networks)
- "Starting Monday is NOT for passive job seekers" (you don't monitor signals for 6 months; you need active tracking)
- "Starting Monday is NOT for DIY candidates on a $0 budget" (it's $49–499/mo)

**Why this matters:** When you tell people "we're not for you," the rest believe you ARE for them. It's more persuasive than saying "we're for everyone."

**What Mark would recommend:**
- Add a "Not for you?" section on landing page:
  - "You're still in your first executive role" → "Start with [coaching guide]"
  - "You're passively monitoring" → "Try our free monthly newsletter instead"
  - "You want to DIY with free tools" → "We recommend [free alternative]. If you hit limits, upgrade to paid."

---

### ISSUE 3: Missing Data Throughout
**Problem:** Every page makes a claim without evidence.

**Examples:**
- "Surfaces signals 2–4 weeks before posting" (what's the data?)
- "AI prep brief in 60 seconds" (average? worst case? best case?)
- "Built for executives who run disciplined campaigns" (what % of users are "disciplined"?)
- "Most setups in 15 minutes" (minimum? median? 90th percentile?)

**Mark's Rule:** Every quantifiable claim on the site should be backed by: data, source, recency.

**Example of good claims:**
- "2–4 weeks early: Based on analysis of 847 placements from Jan–Apr 2026"
- "Average brief: 62 seconds. Fastest: 28 seconds. Slowest: 89 seconds."
- "92% of users complete setup in under 15 minutes (median: 11 min)"

**What Mark would recommend:**
- Audit all quantifiable claims
- For each, add footnote or source: "Data from [timeframe] across [N users/companies]"
- If no data exists, reword to be qualitative: "Most users report completing setup in 15 minutes or less" (honest, vague, less dangerous)

---

### ISSUE 4: No Clear Competitive Positioning
**Problem:** You mention LinkedIn Premium, but no other competitors.

**What's really competing for your prospect's attention?**
1. LinkedIn Premium ($39/yr) — inferior but cheap
2. Search firms ($25K–50K retainer) — better but expensive
3. Coaching ($150–250/hr) — partial solution
4. DIY (free) — hard but possible

**You're trying to own:** "Better than 1 & 3, cheaper than 2, easier than 4"

**But you're not clearly saying:** "We're the bridge between LinkedIn and a search firm"

**What Mark would recommend:**
- Add competitive grid to pricing page:

| | DIY | LinkedIn Premium | Starting Monday | Search Firm |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cost** | $0 | $39/yr | $198–499/yr | $25K–50K |
| **Signal monitoring** | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| **AI prep briefs** | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| **Daily briefing** | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | Partial |
| **Hands-on coaching** | ❌ | ❌ | Optional | ✅ |
| **Time to hire** | 6–12 mo | 4–9 mo | **2–4 mo** | 3–6 mo |

# Brand and Scaling Synthetic Council

---

## Marty Neumeier

**Area of expertise:** Strategic brand and category identity. Brand strategy, differentiation, category identity, design-driven business strategy.

**First principles used by the council:** A brand is a customer's gut feeling; differentiation creates brand power; clarity of promise matters.

**Main content/source:** The Brand Gap / Zag

**Key points to apply:** Bridge strategy and creativity; build brands around differentiation, focus, clear promise, and category distinctiveness.

---

## Chris Do

**Area of expertise:** Premium positioning and value communication. Brand strategy, creative business, value pricing, education, positioning.

**First principles used by the council:** Price follows perceived value; premium brands communicate transformation, not tasks; clarity increases confidence.

**Main content/source:** The Futur / Chris Do content

**Key points to apply:** Sell outcomes, articulate value, raise perceived status, communicate transformation, and avoid commoditizing expert work through weak pricing language.

---

## Brian Balfour

**Area of expertise:** Growth systems and loops. Growth strategy, retention loops, acquisition systems, Reforge frameworks.

**First principles used by the council:** Growth is a system, not a hack; loops outperform linear tactics; retention drives sustainable acquisition.

**Main content/source:** Brian Balfour essays / Reforge

**Key points to apply:** Design growth models, retention loops, channel-model fit, and compounding acquisition systems instead of chasing disconnected tactics.

---

## Andrew Chen

**Area of expertise:** Network effects and platform growth. Network effects, growth, marketplaces/platforms, consumer/product-led scaling.

**First principles used by the council:** Distribution advantages compound when usage increases network value; cold starts must be deliberately solved.

**Main content/source:** The Cold Start Problem

**Key points to apply:** Networked products must solve the cold start, build atomic networks, create density, scale through network effects, and defend against saturation or collapse.

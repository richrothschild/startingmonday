## Method

The synthetic council used the existing 15-lens framework, but answered a narrower question:

What would a credible executive coach actually pay out of pocket for this product if they believed the page, understood the use case, and expected immediate practical utility?

Assumptions:

1. Buyer is an independent executive coach or small-firm coach lead.
1. Purchase is self-serve and one-time, not a custom engagement.
1. Product is delivered instantly and used within the same week.
1. Buyer judges value on time saved, client quality protection, and confidence in hard conversations.

## Executive summary

Council view: all three products are buyable at current prices. Two are probably priced below what the strongest-fit buyer would tolerate.

Consensus willingness-to-pay by product:

- Coach Session Anti-Drift Kit: most likely buy zone is $79-$109.
- Coach Search Readiness Scorecard: most likely buy zone is $39-$59.
- Shadow Search Governance Pack: most likely buy zone is $99-$149.

Council conclusion: your current price floor is safe. If you want maximum conversion volume, keep current pricing. If you want stronger revenue per buyer without materially hurting trust, test modest increases on the scorecard and governance pack first.

## Product-by-product pricing readout

### 1. Coach Session Anti-Drift Kit

Current price: $79

Council willingness-to-pay estimate:

- Low-friction buy point: $69
- Most likely buy point: $89
- Strong-fit ceiling before noticeable resistance: $109

What the council said:

- This solves a recurring operating pain, not a nice-to-have formatting problem.
- Coaches with more than a handful of active clients will map this directly to recovered prep time and better follow-through.
- The product feels reusable across multiple clients, which raises perceived value above a single-session worksheet.
- Resistance starts when the page reads like templates rather than a real operating system.

Recommendation:

- Keep at $79 if the goal is faster first-wave volume.
- Test $89 once you add proof or usage screenshots.

Council verdict:

- Would pay now: yes
- Pricing posture: fair, slightly conservative

### 2. Coach Search Readiness Scorecard

Current price: $39

Council willingness-to-pay estimate:

- Low-friction buy point: $39
- Most likely buy point: $49
- Strong-fit ceiling before noticeable resistance: $59

What the council said:

- The diagnostic framing is strong because it helps coaches intervene faster.
- The low ticket makes it easy to buy without committee thinking.
- The risk is that low price can signal lightweight value unless the scoring method looks rigorous.
- Shared client-facing usage increases value because it becomes both a tool and a communication artifact.

Recommendation:

- $39 is a strong entry point for adoption.
- The council would support testing $49 after adding one worked example or sample scored output.

Council verdict:

- Would pay now: yes
- Pricing posture: meaningfully conservative

### 3. Shadow Search Governance Pack

Current price: $99

Council willingness-to-pay estimate:

- Low-friction buy point: $89
- Most likely buy point: $119
- Strong-fit ceiling before noticeable resistance: $149

What the council said:

- This has the highest perceived stakes because it touches confidentiality, board dynamics, and trust risk.
- Buyers will pay more for language that helps them avoid a bad conversation or governance mistake.
- The offer feels more specialized and less commoditized than the other two products.
- Ceiling depends on whether the page proves this is field-ready language rather than abstract governance advice.

Recommendation:

- $99 is safe and likely under market for the highest-fit buyer.
- If you want one early price test, test $119 here before changing the other products.

Council verdict:

- Would pay now: yes
- Pricing posture: underpriced for best-fit buyer

## Cross-council synthesis

If the council had to choose only one pricing structure to launch today, it would choose:

- Coach Session Anti-Drift Kit: $79
- Coach Search Readiness Scorecard: $39
- Shadow Search Governance Pack: $119

Why:

1. The anti-drift kit already sits in a credible impulse-buy range.
1. The scorecard works best as the easiest first purchase.
1. The governance pack carries the highest downside-protection value and can support a higher price now.

Bundle logic:

## What would increase willingness to pay fast

1. Add one real sample artifact or screenshot per product.
1. Show exactly when in the coaching cycle each product gets used.
1. Add one sentence of buyer proof, even if it is a pilot outcome rather than a polished testimonial.
1. Make the governance pack feel like language that can be used today, not guidance to interpret later.
1. Add a bundle anchor on every product page.

## Bottom line

The council would buy all three at current prices.

The strongest pricing view is:

- Anti-Drift Kit is correctly priced.
- Search Readiness Scorecard is intentionally cheap and can likely move to $49.
- Shadow Search Governance Pack is the clearest candidate for a price increase and can likely hold at $119.

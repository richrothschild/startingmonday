# Synthetic Council Site Review — May 2026

**Date:** May 11, 2026  
**Subject:** startingmonday.app — landing page, pricing page, public-facing site  
**Format:** Current grade + specific feedback + what it takes to earn an A

---

## How to Read This Document

Each council member reviewed the site as it stands on May 11, 2026. For every member:

- **Current grade** — their honest assessment today
- **What they said** — the specific things they liked, flagged, and required
- **To earn an A** — the precise change that moves their grade

The cross-council summary at the bottom names the three changes that every council converges on.

## Sales and Marketing Council

### Dave Gerhardt — B+

**What he said:** The hero is strong. "The role was never posted. You found it anyway." is a genuine story-first hook that reads like someone who lived the problem. The before/after section is a real point of view worth arguing with. Below the hero, the page loses the founder voice entirely and slides into feature-list mode. Testimonials are anonymous, which builds less trust than a real name at this audience level.

**To earn an A:** Add a founder moment between the hero and the before/after section — one short paragraph in first person that explains why this exists. "I ran this search. I had the relationships. I still lost track of companies, missed signals, and walked into interviews underprepared. I built this because I needed it." Named. That section becomes the most shared part of the page.

All 8 situation cards route to the same /signup, losing a major differentiation opportunity.

**To earn an A:** Put the category name above the fold in a visible H2 or subhead near the hero. "This is the executive job search operating system. Not a job board. Not a coaching platform. ## What the Full Council Agrees On

These three changes appear in four or more councils independently. They are the highest-leverage improvements on the site.

**1. Add one product screenshot.**  
Stanley, Norman, Majors, Biddle, Kozyrkov, Skok all arrive at the same gap from different directions. The landing page has zero product visuals. A single screenshot of a pattern alert card or prep brief excerpt does more trust work than three feature sections.

**2. Give the Concierge tier its own page.**  
Buffett, Campbell, Chris Do, Skok, O'Carroll — every economics and pricing lens flags the same thing. $1,299/mo buried in a footnote band is a capital allocation mistake. It needs a standalone page, dedicated acquisition copy, and at minimum an above-fold mention on the landing page.

**3. Do something with the situation card selection.**  
Norman, Dunford, McMahon, Bourgoin, Barrows, Kahneman, Sutherland — the emotional self-identification in the situation section is the best conversion mechanic on the page and it currently does nothing downstream. Every user who clicks a card tells you who they are. Use it.

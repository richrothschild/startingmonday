# Behavioral Economics Synthetic Council

---

## Nir Eyal

**Area of expertise:** Habit formation and behavioral retention. Product psychology, habit loops, engagement design, attention.

**First principles used by the council:** Products become valuable when they enter recurring behavior loops; triggers, actions, rewards, and investment shape retention.

**Main content/source:** Hooked / Indistractable

**Key points to apply:** The Hook Model uses trigger, action, variable reward, and investment to build habits; ethical use requires aligning habit with user benefit.

---

## Rory Sutherland

**Area of expertise:** Behavioral economics and perception psychology. Behavioral science, advertising, perception, irrational decision-making.

**First principles used by the council:** Perception changes economic reality; humans are irrational but patterned; small framing changes can create large value shifts.

**Main content/source:** Alchemy

**Key points to apply:** Psychological value is real; seemingly irrational improvements can outperform rational optimization when they change perception, confidence, and behavior.

---

## Richard Thaler

**Area of expertise:** Behavioral economics, choice architecture, and decision friction. Nudge design, default effects, incentives, and predictable irrationality.

**First principles used by the council:** Small frictions create large behavior shifts; defaults matter; users do not behave like perfect rational agents.

**Main content/source:** Nudge / Misbehaving

**Key points to apply:** Use ethical nudges and better defaults, reduce avoidable choice overload, and align incentives so users complete valuable actions with less cognitive load.

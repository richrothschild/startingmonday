# Synthetic Council Structure

## How To Use The Council Without Creating Persona Theater

- Treat each named person as a reasoning lens, not as a voice imitation.
- Force disagreement between valid perspectives before synthesizing recommendations.
- Use decision weighting rather than consensus: domain believability, evidence quality, ROI, reversibility, opportunity cost, political risk, trust impact, moat impact, and time horizon.
- Create decision objects with expected outcome, kill criteria, review date, and success metric.
- Review whether prior decisions were good decisions, not merely whether outcomes were lucky or unlucky.
- Use synthetic users to test trust, willingness to pay, retention, executive-grade perception, and action confidence.
- Treat the prompt as a prototype of the reasoning system; the moat is the structured data model, feedback loops, benchmarks, and outcome history.

---

## Recommended Operating Loop

**1. Ingest evidence:** Product analytics, signal-scanner quality, customer feedback, revenue, retention, engineering health, support, sales notes, and founder/team notes.

**2. Generate council review:** Apply expert cognitive models and synthetic user realism.

**3. Surface tensions:** Identify where valid recommendations conflict.

**4. Create decision objects:** Turn recommendations into decisions with owners, evidence, expected ROI, opportunity cost, reversibility, and kill criteria.

**5. Track bets:** Record success metrics, review date, assumptions, and disconfirming evidence.

**6. Review outcomes:** Separate good decisions from lucky outcomes and bad decisions from unlucky outcomes.

**7. Update principles:** Convert repeated lessons into operating principles.

**8. Compound advantage:** Improve signal quality, trust, retention, product judgment, and durable profit over time.

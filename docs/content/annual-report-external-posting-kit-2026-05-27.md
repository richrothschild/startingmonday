## 1) Two-Line Lead-In Options (Use One)

### Option A (Direct Contrarian)

Most teams using AI in executive search are moving faster and making worse decisions.
Speed is easy. Decision quality under confidentiality pressure is the real test.

### Option B (Coach-Centric)

Executive coaching outcomes do not fail from lack of effort.
They fail when AI accelerates prep but weakens judgment discipline.

### Option C (Board-Risk Framing)

The fastest way to lose trust in executive search is not one giant mistake.
It is repeated small confidentiality misses that everyone notices too late.

## 2) Caption Body Skeleton (Copy, Then Personalize)

I rewrote our 2026 executive search + AI + confidentiality report with one goal: behavior change, not policy theater.

- where AI is helping

- a mini-case showing what changed in 4 weeks

If you lead VP-to-CXO or board-track coaching, this should map directly to your weekly operating rhythm.

## 3) Required End Prompt (Constrained)

Which fails first in your process: judgment quality or confidentiality discipline?

Reply with one word: judgment or confidentiality.

## 4) Seeded Practitioner Comments (Post in First Hour)

Post these as your own follow-up comments or through aligned team members.

### Seed Comment 1 (Binary Reinforcement)

If you chose judgment, what is your first failing signal: weak assumptions, weak context, or weak ownership?

### Seed Comment 2 (Operational Friction)

What is one control your team says is "too much process" that actually prevents rework?

### Seed Comment 3 (Coaching Specificity)

For VP-to-CXO clients, where does narrative drift show up first: role fit, market fit, or mandate fit?

## 5) Pinned Comment (Binary Pattern)

Quick pulse check:

A = judgment quality fails first
B = confidentiality discipline fails first

Drop A or B and one line why.

## 7) Reply Handling Rules

1. Ask follow-up questions, do not over-explain.
2. Keep replies under 3 lines where possible.
3. Quote the other person's exact phrase once to show listening.
4. If someone posts a strong counterpoint, thank and invite one concrete example.

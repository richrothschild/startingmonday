# Email Synthetic Council

## Pod Purpose

The Email Council exists to make every outbound email:

1. Relevant to a specific recipient and moment.
2. Trust-preserving under commercial pressure.
3. Decision-oriented instead of feature-oriented.
4. Evidence-backed and legally safe.
5. Easy to reply to with a low-friction next step.

This sub-council is the default review body for:

- Executive outreach emails.
- Coach outreach emails.
- Search-firm outreach emails.
- Outplacement outreach emails.
- Follow-up and close-loop sequences.

Quality standard:

- The council standard is excellent, not merely passable.
- Send-ready means high-confidence quality with strong relevance, legal-safe proof, and low-friction conversion design.
- Anything technically acceptable but not clearly high quality must be rewritten.

---

## Governance and Decision Rights

### Review Roles

- Draft owner: prepares copy and required context.
- Core reviewer pod: scores every email.
- Specialist reviewer pod: engaged when channel or risk requires deeper review.
- Final approver: confirms send gate is met and logs decision.

### Decision Rights

- Any core reviewer can block send if hard-fail criteria are triggered.
- Specialist reviewers can block only in their domain (legal, coach workflow, economics).
- Tie-break authority sits with final approver after documented rationale.

### Review Modes and SLAs

- Fast pass (single pod): low-risk follow-ups and minor edits.
- Full pass (core plus specialist): new templates, major rewrites, and high-risk claims.
- SLA targets: first review within 24 hours, rewrite round within 48 hours, final decision within 72 hours.

## Rubric: What Matters Most

Scoring: 0 to 5 per category. Weighted score out of 100.

Scoring anchors:

- 5: excellent, specific, and ready with no meaningful caveats.
- 4: strong, minor polish only.
- 3: acceptable but not high quality; rewrite required.
- 2: weak and likely to underperform.
- 1: materially flawed.
- 0: missing or harmful.

Principle:

- Scores of 3 are not send-ready. The target is sustained 4s and 5s.

### 1) Relevance and Trigger Fit (15)

Checks:

- Recipient-specific trigger present in first 2 lines.
- Message could not be sent unchanged to a different persona.
- Economic or operational consequence of inaction is clear.

### 2) Audience and Positioning Clarity (10)

Checks:

- Clear who this is for.
- Implied alternative framed correctly.

### 3) Emotional-State Resonance (10)

Checks:

- Opening line reflects recipient state (panic, optionality, burnout, board-track).
- Tone matches context without dramatization.

### 4) Decision Framing Quality (10)

Checks:

- Email clarifies the decision recipient is making.
- Outcome of saying yes or pass is explicit.

### 5) Objection Safety and Trust Tone (10)

Checks:

- Safe-to-say-no language included.
- Objections handled without defensive pressure.

### 6) Proof Quality and Legality (15)

Checks:

- Denominator present when claims are made.
- Caveat present (results vary or directional evidence).
- No implied guarantee language.
- Proof claim is proportionate to evidence quality.

### 7) CTA Quality (10)

Checks:

- Binary yes/pass structure.
- One asset, one action, low friction.

### 8) Voice and Readability (10)

Checks:

- Natural human language.
- Minimal jargon and no over-structuring.

### 9) Channel-Specific Strength (10)

Checks:

- Search-firm copy includes mandate economics.
- Outplacement copy includes operating model and consistency.
- Coach copy emphasizes session amplification.

## Score Bands

- 95 to 100: excellent and send-ready.
- 90 to 94: strong but requires one quality lift before send.
- 80 to 89: not ready; targeted rewrite required.
- 70 to 79: major rewrite required.
- Below 70: do not send.

---

## Hard-Fail Criteria (Auto-Block)

Any one of the following triggers an automatic block, regardless of total score:

1. No clear recipient trigger in the first 2 lines.
2. Outcomes claim without denominator or caveat.
3. CTA is not explicit yes or pass.
4. Message includes pressure language that removes safe-to-say-no posture.
5. Message can be sent unchanged across multiple unrelated personas.
6. Legal-sensitive claim language implies certainty or guarantee.

---

## Required Send Gate

An email is approved only if:

1. Total score is at least 95.
2. Proof Quality and Legality is at least 4 out of 5.
3. CTA Quality is at least 4 out of 5.
4. Relevance and Trigger Fit is at least 4 out of 5.
5. Voice and Readability is at least 4 out of 5.
6. No hard-fail criteria are triggered.

Quality floor:

- Passable is not acceptable.
- Any category score of 3 must be revised before approval.

## Fast Review Workflow

- Assign primary reviewers by section.

- Subject and opening: Gerhardt, Dunford, Bourgoin.
- Objections and tone: Braun, Voss, Kim Scott.
- Proof and claims: Cialdini, Adam Grant, Mary O'Carroll.
- CTA and sequence action: Thaler, James Clear, Barrows.

- Score with rubric.
- Apply only changes tied to low-score categories and hard-fail triggers.
- Re-score and ship when send gate is met.
- Record rationale for any category below 5.

---

## Common Failure Modes

1. Framework-sounding language instead of human voice.
2. Generic proof inflation without bounded evidence.
3. CTA clutter with multiple asks.
4. Audience blending in a single template.
5. Trigger buried below paragraph two.

Correction policy:

- Each failed category requires one concrete rewrite action and one owner.

---

## Outcome Learning Loop

Every send batch must log:

1. Expected metric movement before send.
2. Actual open rate.
3. Actual reply rate.
4. Positive reply rate.
5. Yes versus pass ratio.
6. Top objection patterns by channel.

Monthly governance:

- Recalibrate rubric thresholds and phrase bank based on observed performance.
- Archive low-performing templates and preserve winning variants with revision IDs.

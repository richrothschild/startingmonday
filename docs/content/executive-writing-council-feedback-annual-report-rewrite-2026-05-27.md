## Brutally Honest Red-Team Notes

1. This now reads like a smart operator wrote it, not a policy committee.
2. You still have not weaponized disagreement strongly enough for social distribution.
3. The humor helps flow, but the piece is still optimized for agreement, not replies.
4. The checklist is good, but too safe. It should force uncomfortable self-audit.
5. If this is posted verbatim, opens improve, but replies will likely underperform your target.

## Required Edits Before Publish Push

   Most teams with AI are moving faster and making worse executive decisions.
2. Add one constrained discussion prompt at the end:
   "Which fails first in your process: judgment quality or confidentiality discipline?"
3. Add one mini-case example (5-6 lines) with before/after behavior change.
4. Add role-specific callouts for:
   VP-to-CXO transition coaching and board-track coaching.

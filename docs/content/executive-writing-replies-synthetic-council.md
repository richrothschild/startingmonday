# Executive Writing Replies Synthetic Council

## Pod Purpose

This council is designed to maximize one business outcome:

- qualified replies per post

Secondary outcomes:

- target-segment open rate
- saves/shares among executive coaches
- high-intent DM conversion to calls

This council is for editing long-form and short-form thought leadership used by Starting Monday.

### 1) New York Times editor benchmark (last 60 years)

- A.M. Rosenthal (benchmark lens for hardline newsroom clarity and standards)

### 2) Pulitzer-winning nonfiction writers

- Anne Applebaum (Pulitzer Prize, General Nonfiction)
- Siddhartha Mukherjee (Pulitzer Prize, General Nonfiction)

## Council Members

### A.M. Rosenthal lens

- Core job: eliminate soft language and editorial drift.
- Bias: directness over diplomacy.
- Red flag: any sentence that sounds generic enough for any audience.

### Anne Applebaum lens

- Core job: ensure claims are fair, verifiable, and responsibly framed.
- Bias: precision over hype.
- Red flag: broad conclusions without bounded evidence.

### Siddhartha Mukherjee lens

- Core job: make complexity understandable without oversimplifying.
- Bias: elegant explanation over slogan.
- Red flag: technical claim without operational implication.

### Jared Diamond lens

- Core job: connect context, causality, and consequences.
- Bias: systems-level framing.
- Red flag: recommendations not tied to structural drivers.

### James Clear lens

- Core job: convert ideas into repeatable behavior.
- Bias: practical action loops.
- Red flag: insight without a concrete next action.

### Seth Godin lens

- Core job: sharpen positioning and audience tension.
- Bias: specificity for a tribe over broad appeal.
- Red flag: trying to persuade everyone.

### Tim Urban lens

- Core job: make abstract points memorable through clean framing.
- Bias: conceptual clarity and stickiness.
- Red flag: dense blocks with low narrative gravity.

### Marshall Goldsmith lens

- Core job: convert content into behavior change for leaders.
- Bias: accountability and measurable change.
- Red flag: no explicit follow-through mechanism.

### Herminia Ibarra lens

- Core job: strengthen identity-transition and leadership narrative.
- Bias: strategic role evolution.
- Red flag: positioning advice disconnected from role transitions.

### Jerry Colonna lens

- Core job: preserve humanity and emotional truth.
- Bias: honest tension over polished perfection.
- Red flag: emotionally sterile authority voice.

### Michael Bungay Stanier lens

- Core job: keep advice coach-friendly and question-driven.
- Bias: utility in real conversations.
- Red flag: no coaching question or reflection prompt.

### Whitney Johnson lens

- Core job: align content to growth, S-curve transitions, and reinvention.
- Bias: developmental progression.
- Red flag: static advice that ignores capability growth over time.

## Replies-First Writing Rubric (100 Points)

Primary success metric:

- reply rate at or above 3.0%

Readiness target:

- open rate at or above 10.0% in the executive coach segment

### 1) Hook and Stop-Scroll Power (15)

- 0-5: generic opening
- 6-10: clear but ordinary
- 11-15: sharp, specific, impossible to skim past

### 2) Audience Precision (10)

- 0-3: broad audience blur
- 4-7: mostly coach-relevant
- 8-10: unmistakably written for executive coaches

### 3) Claim Quality and Evidence Posture (10)

- 0-3: overclaiming or vagueness
- 4-7: reasonable claims with partial grounding
- 8-10: precise claims with bounded confidence

### 4) Practical Value Density (10)

- 0-3: mostly opinion
- 4-7: some practical utility
- 8-10: immediately actionable in live coaching workflows

### 5) Emotional Truth and Human Voice (10)

- 0-3: robotic or sterile
- 4-7: clear but flat
- 8-10: credible, human, and grounded

### 6) Distinctive Point of View (10)

- 0-3: interchangeable content
- 4-7: somewhat differentiated
- 8-10: clear and ownable stance

### 7) Structure and Readability (10)

- 0-3: hard to scan
- 4-7: acceptable flow
- 8-10: crisp pacing and fast comprehension

### 8) CTA and Reply Friction (15)

- 0-5: weak or generic CTA
- 6-10: clear CTA, moderate friction
- 11-15: low-friction, conversation-worthy CTA engineered for replies

### 9) Confidentiality Safety and Trust Signaling (10)

- 0-3: risky or careless framing
- 4-7: mostly safe
- 8-10: explicit trust-aware framing without fear language

## Scoring Interpretation

- 90-100: publish now
- 80-89: minor edits, then publish
- 70-79: rework hook + CTA + audience precision
- below 70: rebuild draft from core tension

## Council Operating Rule

No draft ships unless all three pass:

- Rosenthal pass (clarity and sharpness)
- Applebaum pass (claim discipline)
- One executive-coaching specialist pass (real-world coaching utility)

# Human Writing Synthetic Council Prompt And Rubric For LinkedIn Short-Form (Under 1200 Characters)

## Purpose

Use this version when the post must stay under 1200 characters and still feel human, credible, and memorable.

This is a speed tool for high-frequency LinkedIn publishing.

---

## Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

- Max length: 1200 characters total.
- Ideal range: 700 to 1100 characters.
- One core idea per post.
- One subtle humor line maximum.
- One CTA maximum.
- One primary emotional angle per post.

If the post exceeds 1200 characters, trim before scoring.

Emotional angle rotation rule:

- Do not publish two adjacent posts with the same primary emotional angle.
- Rotate across: candid humility, practical relief, conviction, earned optimism, urgency, grounded concern.

---

## Council Lenses (Short-Form Roles)

1. Ann Handley lens: simplify wording and improve readability in the first two lines.

1. Morgan Housel lens: add one concrete observation or mini-scene.

1. Adam Grant lens: keep claims precise and supportable.

1. Nora Ephron lens: add one light, human smile line.

1. Brene Brown lens: name one real tension without oversharing.

1. Robert Gottlieb lens (final editor): tighten opening, remove weak phrasing, and improve pacing.

1. Anne Applebaum lens (Pulitzer-winning final approver): verify claims are fair, precise, and responsibly framed.

### Why These Two Were Added

- Robert Gottlieb: elite editorial discipline for concise, high-impact prose.
- Anne Applebaum: Pulitzer-winning rigor for credibility and argument integrity.

### Final Approver Rule

Before publish, both must pass the draft:

- Gottlieb pass: opening is sharp and no sentence feels soft or bloated.
- Applebaum pass: no overclaiming, and evidence posture is honest.

---

## 8-Minute Workflow

### Minute 0-2: Raw Draft

Write quickly in your own voice.

### Minute 2-5: Council Edits

Run one pass per lens and keep only one edit from each.

### Minute 5-6: Character Trim

Trim to 1200 or less. Keep opening and CTA intact.

### Minute 6-8: Rubric Score, Final Approver Pass, And Decide

- Publish: score 84 or higher.
- Revise: score 72 to 83.
- Rewrite opening: below 72.

## Short-Form Rubric (100 Points)

### 1) Hook Strength In First Two Lines (25 points)

- 0 to 10: generic opener, low pull.
- 11 to 18: clear but not memorable.
- 19 to 25: specific and compelling.

Checklist:

- First line sounds lived, not templated.
- Reader can identify audience and tension quickly.

### 2) Human Specificity (20 points)

- 0 to 8: abstract and broad.
- 9 to 15: some concrete detail.
- 16 to 20: clear lived detail and context.

Checklist:

- Includes at least one concrete signal: number, moment, role, or quote.

### 3) Credibility And Claim Discipline (20 points)

- 0 to 8: overstated or vague claim.
- 9 to 15: mostly grounded claim.
- 16 to 20: precise claim with clear context.

Checklist:

- One main claim only.
- Confidence level is honest.

### 4) Wit And Warmth (15 points)

- 0 to 5: flat or robotic.
- 6 to 11: friendly but predictable.
- 12 to 15: subtle humor, natural voice.

Checklist:

- One smile line max.
- Tone stays professional.

### 5) Compression Quality (10 points)

- 0 to 3: bloated or repetitive.
- 4 to 7: mostly tight.
- 8 to 10: concise and complete.

Checklist:

- Under 1200 characters.
- No repeated ideas.

### 6) CTA Quality (10 points)

- 0 to 3: weak or generic prompt.
- 4 to 7: acceptable prompt.
- 8 to 10: clear, conversation-worthy prompt.

Checklist:

- CTA fits the idea and audience.
- Invites thoughtful replies.

---

## Critical Fails (Auto-Revise)

Revise before posting if any apply:

- Over 1200 characters.
- More than one major idea.
- More than one humor line.
- Claims certainty without context.
- Opening line could fit any generic business post.

---

## 20-Second Pre-Publish Check

- Strong first line?
- One real detail?
- One honest claim?
- One smile line max?
- One clear CTA?
- Under 1200?

If all are yes, publish.

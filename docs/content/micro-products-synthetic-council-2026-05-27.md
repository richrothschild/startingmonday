# Micro Products Synthetic Council

Date: 2026-05-27
Purpose: govern the design, pricing, positioning, and launch quality of Starting Monday micro products.

## Council mission

Maximize three outcomes without sacrificing trust:

1. buyer conversion
1. practical user outcomes
1. credibility lift for the core brand

## Council composition

## 1) Product strategy and market framing

- April Dunford lens: positioning clarity and category fit
- Rory Sutherland lens: behavioral leverage and perceived value
- Seth Godin lens: audience tension and tribe relevance

## 2) Offer and pricing economics

- Alex Hormozi lens: offer strength and value equation
- Patrick Campbell lens: pricing and packaging discipline
- Bernadette Jiwa lens: customer meaning and buying triggers

## 3) Execution and behavior change

- Marshall Goldsmith lens: measurable behavior change and accountability
- James Clear lens: repeatable action design and implementation
- Michael Bungay Stanier lens: coaching utility and real-world usability

## 4) Trust, evidence, and editorial quality

- Anne Applebaum lens: claim discipline and evidence posture
- A.M. Rosenthal lens: sharp clarity and anti-fluff standards
- Herminia Ibarra lens: executive identity-transition relevance

## 5) Distribution and growth performance

- Katelyn Bourgoin lens: audience pain articulation and demand testing
- Brian Balfour lens: channel-model fit and growth loops
- Andrew Chen lens: social distribution mechanics and demand resonance

## Council operating rules

No micro product ships unless all three pass:

1. Offer pass: Hormozi or Campbell lens confirms clear value-to-price logic.
1. Utility pass: Goldsmith or Clear lens confirms implementation quality.
1. Trust pass: Applebaum or Rosenthal lens confirms claims and framing integrity.

## Micro-product readiness rubric (100 points)

## 1) Pain and audience precision (15)

- Is the product solving one specific painful decision for one specific buyer?

## 2) Offer clarity and value density (15)

- Is promise clear, tangible, and outcome-linked?

## 3) Pricing and packaging logic (10)

- Is price coherent with perceived and delivered value?

## 4) Actionability and implementation quality (15)

- Can user realize value quickly with low friction?

## 5) Credibility and trust signaling (10)

- Are claims bounded, practical, and evidence-aware?

## 6) Distinctiveness and positioning (10)

- Is product non-generic and strategically differentiated?

## 7) Distribution fit and demand potential (10)

- Is there a credible channel path to buyers?

## 8) Upsell pathway into core offer (10)

- Does product naturally transition buyer into next-value offer?

## 9) Operational viability and margin health (5)

- Can this run at high margin without service overload?

Scoring interpretation:

- 90-100: ship now
- 80-89: ship with minor fixes
- 70-79: revise offer, pricing, or utility before launch
- below 70: no launch, rebuild from audience pain

## Risk radar checklist

Required red-team checks before launch:

1. Generic template risk
1. Over-claim or under-evidence risk
1. Low-margin support burden risk
1. Weak upsell path risk
1. Brand mismatch risk

## Reusable council prompt


# Automated Posting Plan: Funny + Practical Executive Search Series

Date: 2026-05-27
Owner: Starting Monday
Primary source: annual report public edition

## Goal

Turn the annual report into a 4-week posting engine that creates:

1. high-quality comments from practitioners,
2. profile visits from target buyers,
3. repeatable article assets for executive coaches, executives, and outplacement teams.

## Voice Recipe (Dave Barry x Marshall Goldsmith)

Use this formula in every post and article:

1. Start with a sharp, slightly absurd observation from real work.
2. Name the expensive behavior clearly.
3. Give a practical operating fix in 2 to 4 bullet points.
4. End with one specific question that invites practitioner disagreement.

Keep this balance:

- 25% humor and pattern satire
- 75% practical operating guidance

## Audience Lanes and Pain to Expose

### Executive coaches

Pain to expose:

- clients want transformation with no behavior change,
- prep notes look polished but decisions are still weak,
- coaching sessions get consumed by context reconstruction.

### Executives

Pain to expose:

- they are told to be strategic but measured on speed,
- they confuse activity with signal quality,
- they optimize for optics while weakening options.

### Outplacement leaders

Pain to expose:

- pressure to show momentum leads to low-quality outreach,
- teams over-template and under-diagnose,
- career narratives drift because nobody owns positioning quality.

## Article and Post Bank (12 Pieces)

## 1) Shadow search satire

Hook:

- "If your first governance document is a recruiter call, congratulations, you have invented expensive avoidance."

Practical fix:

- require written board expectations,
- require direct CEO feedback date,
- require time-bound remediation window.

Prompt:

- "What is your hard gate before a search is legitimate?"

## 2) The KPI theater post

Hook:

- "When your dashboard is green and your judgment is red, the dashboard is lying by omission."

Practical fix:

- add one decision quality metric,
- score assumption quality before activity volume,
- run a weekly decision postmortem.

Prompt:

- "Which metric do you track that you secretly know is vanity?"

## 3) Confidentiality controls for humans

Hook:

- "The leak is rarely one villain; it is usually twelve tiny shortcuts in a trench coat."

Practical fix:

- role-based access boundaries,
- language discipline in summaries,
- documented escalation path.

Prompt:

- "Which control feels annoying but saves you most often?"

## 4) Why your urgent search is six months late

Hook:

- "Most urgent executive searches are just delayed decisions wearing a sprint outfit."

Practical fix:

- quarterly succession review,
- weak-signal capture,
- pre-brief role hypotheses.

Prompt:

- "Where does delay usually begin in your process?"

## 5) Executive prep as fiction

Hook:

- "Some prep docs are bestsellers: confident plot, weak evidence, unhappy ending."

Practical fix:

- evidence tags for every claim,
- confidence labeling,
- contradiction check before interviews.

Prompt:

- "What is the most common unsupported claim in executive prep?"

## 6) Job search dashboard delusion

Hook:

- "Twelve tabs open is not strategy. It is a cry for operational design."

Practical fix:

- define one target narrative per quarter,
- cap active tracks,
- add weekly kill criteria.

Prompt:

- "What do you now stop tracking because it never changed outcomes?"

## 7) Outplacement speed trap

Hook:

- "If every candidate gets the same 'personalized' message, you built a blender, not a strategy."

Practical fix:

- persona-level narrative lanes,
- outreach quality rubric,
- manager review on first 10 sends.

Prompt:

- "Where do you trade quality for speed most often?"

## 8) Coach session time leak

Hook:

- "If half the session is context recovery, your operating system is billing itself to death."

Practical fix:

- pre-session brief template,
- shared assumptions log,
- action ownership at close.

Prompt:

- "What percentage of your sessions gets lost to context rebuild?"

## 9) Networking myths that waste quarters

Hook:

- "'Just network more' is advice the way 'just breathe underwater' is swim coaching."

Practical fix:

- define target node types,
- ask for one specific transfer each outreach,
- close loop with value return.

Prompt:

- "What outreach pattern actually converts to real conversations for you?"

## 10) Board communication debt

Hook:

- "Boards do not get surprised by CEOs; they get surprised by their own delayed candor."

Practical fix:

- monthly written expectation resets,
- explicit risk register,
- communication audit at quarter close.

Prompt:

- "What conversation does your board delay too long?"

## 11) Narrative architecture for transitions

Hook:

- "Most executive stories fail for one reason: they describe jobs, not value transfer."

Practical fix:

- arc in three moves: context, intervention, measurable shift,
- remove biography clutter,
- pressure-test with a skeptical operator.

Prompt:

- "What one line in your narrative pulls the strongest response?"

## 12) What not to automate in career strategy

Hook:

- "AI can draft your message. It cannot decide what you should not say yet."

Practical fix:

- automate formatting and synthesis,
- keep judgment calls human,
- force a final human contradiction review.

Prompt:

- "Where have you seen automation quietly damage trust?"

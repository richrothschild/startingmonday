# Executive Coaching Synthetic Council

## Pod Purpose

The Executive Coaching pod answers:
- What do different types of coaches actually do?
- Which coaching persona aligns with Starting Monday's value?
- How do coaches think about tools and infrastructure?
- What coaching models scale and which remain 1-on-1 boutiques?
- How do we build messaging and positioning for each coach type?

This pod is different from general B2B partnerships.

Business Development asks: How do we get coaches to refer us?

Executive Coaching asks: What does each type of coach actually need? What problems do they solve? What gaps exist in their current tooling?

For Starting Monday, coaching is a critical partner channel because coaches know when executives are in motion AND they control the narrative about preparation and discipline.

## Coach Personas

### Persona 1: The Career Transition Specialist
**What they do:** Work with executives (VP/SVP/C-suite) navigating involuntary and voluntary career transitions. Focus: landing well, quickly, and on your terms. They coach through the emotional journey (shock, anger, fear) and the strategic journey (narrative building, target list, outreach discipline).

**Their typical client:** Executive who was recently laid off, fired, or chose to leave. Needs to move in 30-90 days. Coaching engagement: $3,000–$10,000 for 6-8 weeks of weekly sessions.

**What they currently do between sessions:** Ask the client to come back with a target list, a network map, a timeline. They're reconstructing progress manually at each session. They cannot track 30 companies. They cannot see what happened between Monday and Thursday.

**Their pain point:** Session time is split 50/50 between emotional support and "wait, tell me where we are with the companies again?" They want more time for strategy and less time rebuilding context.

**How Starting Monday helps:** The coach walks into every session knowing the client's pipeline, what signals appeared, which companies moved, what prep work was done. Session time shifts from research catch-up to strategy. The coach becomes a strategic peer, not a researcher.

**Key selling angle:** "One hour with you is worth more than a research tool. Start Monday handles the research and daily discipline. You handle the human work."

---

### Persona 2: The VP-to-CXO Positioning Coach
**What they do:** Work with high-performing executives (VP/SVP level) who are ready for the next seat (CIO, CEO, CTO, COO). Focus: narrative refinement, board-level story, position articulation, credibility building. They coach through career positioning, not crisis recovery. Coaching engagement: $5,000–$25,000 per engagement over 3-6 months. Often higher-value, longer engagements.

**Their typical client:** Executive who is still in role but has decided they are ready for the next level. Needs to move carefully (confidentially) and strategically. Usually looking 12-24 months ahead, not 90 days.

**What they currently do between sessions:** Ask the client to think about their story, research target companies, build a relationship list. The client is juggling a current job plus a hidden career project. They forget details. They miss signals. They lose momentum between sessions.

**Their pain point:** Long sales cycles require relationship maintenance and signal awareness over months. Without daily structure, clients go dormant. The coach has to re-energize them at every session.

**How Starting Monday helps:** Daily briefing keeps the client engaged without active effort. Signals surface early (the coach sees them too via shared view). Prep briefs ready in a minute. The client stays warm and ready without the coach having to rebuild context. Relationship tracking never goes cold.

**Key selling angle:** "Your clients need structure they will actually use between your sessions. You need visibility into their discipline without requiring a status call. Starting Monday is that structure."

---

### Persona 3: The Executive Search Firm Coach (Retained Search Affiliate)
**What they do:** Work with executives on behalf of retained search firms (not as an employee, but as a referral partner). Specializing in interview prep, positioning, negotiation, and onboarding coaching for C-suite placements. Coaching engagement: $2,000–$8,000 per engagement, billed by the search firm or the candidate.

**Their typical client:** Executive who is already in active search with a retained search firm. The firm recommends coaching for interview prep, positioning, or negotiation. Usually 2-4 week engagements around key interviews or offers.

**What they currently do between sessions:** Ask the client about their interviews, their strategy with the search firm, their positioning. They are almost entirely reactive to what the client tells them. They cannot see the actual pipeline or company signals. They cannot track which companies are actually moving.

**Their pain point:** Interview prep is short-term and reactive. They want to help the client think about the broader strategy but don't have visibility into the real situation. They are coaching half-blind.

**How Starting Monday helps:** See the client's entire pipeline, company signals, and interview timeline in one place. Coach can see which companies are moving, what signals appeared, what the client should be preparing for. The coach becomes proactive, not just reactive. Prep briefs generated in a minute support the session. Negotiation strategy informed by real company intelligence.

**Key selling angle:** "Your candidates are more prepared when they walk into interviews if they have the right intelligence and structure beforehand. Starting Monday makes your coaching more effective and your clients more confident."

---

### Persona 4: The Board Positioning & Governance Coach
**What they do:** Work with senior executives (CEO/CFO/CIO level) pursuing board seats. Focus: governance readiness, board narrative, relationship building with directors, positioning for governance roles. Long coaching engagements (6-24 months) because board seeking is a multi-year play. Coaching engagement: $10,000–$50,000+ per engagement.

**Their typical client:** Already successful executive who wants a board seat. This is not urgent but strategic. They will coach across multiple board opportunities and governance pathways simultaneously.

**What they currently do between sessions:** Ask the client about conversations with directors, PE partners, governance committees. Coaching is mostly about narrative refinement and relationship strategy. The coach cannot track board composition changes, governance shifts, PE portfolio transitions, or director network opportunities.

**Their pain point:** Board seeking requires long-term relationship maintenance and signal awareness across hundreds of companies and PE firms. Without systematic tracking, relationships go cold and opportunities are missed.

**How Starting Monday helps:** Track target board companies, monitor governance changes (8-K filings, board composition, PE transitions), maintain relationship timeline, prepare board-level narratives. The coach can see which companies are moving into governance transitions. The client stays engaged and prepared across the multi-year timeline.

**Key selling angle:** "Board seeking is a 3-5 year play. Your clients need structure and intelligence to maintain momentum without your constant attention. Starting Monday automates the signal side so you can focus on the relationship and narrative side."

## Reconsidered Persona Matrix (2026)

This matrix extends the four core personas with behavior-level preferences for LinkedIn content and confidentiality expectations.

### Persona 5: The Confidential In-Role Transition Coach
**Who they serve:** Executives searching while still employed and not publicly signaling a move.

**Primary concerns:** Confidentiality, selective outreach, and low-visibility momentum between sessions.

**What they like in tools and content:**
- Tight access controls and explicit role-based visibility.
- Practical workflows that avoid public signaling until intentional.
- LinkedIn posts about strategy quality, confidentiality discipline, and decision timing.

**What they dislike in tools and content:**
- Public-first tactics that assume open-to-work visibility.
- Generic "network harder" advice with no confidentiality safeguards.
- Product hype posts that expose sensitive operating details.

**LinkedIn style fit:**
- Likes: sober, specific, low-drama posts with operational language.
- Dislikes: loud self-promotion, vague inspiration, or urgency theater.

---

### Persona 6: The Corporate Outplacement / L&D-Adjacent Coach
**Who they serve:** Leaders in sponsored transition programs via employers, HR, or outplacement partners.

**Primary concerns:** Repeatable process, measurable outcomes, and risk reduction at scale.

**What they like in tools and content:**
- Clear process checkpoints and measurable session impact.
- Evidence-backed messaging, especially labor and skills data.
- LinkedIn posts that combine executive empathy with operational metrics.

**What they dislike in tools and content:**
- Boutique-only workflows that do not scale.
- Claims without evidence.
- Overly personal founder narratives without client-outcome relevance.

**LinkedIn style fit:**
- Likes: concise frameworks, before/after process improvements, risk-aware language.
- Dislikes: controversial hot takes detached from client outcomes.

## LinkedIn Content Preference Grid (All Personas)

### Themes that generally perform well
- Session effectiveness (reduce context rebuild).
- Narrative quality for VP-to-CXO transitions.
- Confidentiality-safe operating rhythm.
- AI as judgment support, not autopilot.
- Board-track continuity and long-horizon discipline.

### Themes that underperform across personas
- Feature-first announcements with no coaching implication.
- Generic career advice for broad audiences.
- Overlong posts without a concrete operational takeaway.
- Performative controversy that weakens trust.

### Practical content constraints for this council
- Keep posts in the 3-5 hashtag range.
- Use a broad + niche mix of hashtags.
- Prioritize one clear friction point and one concrete fix per post.
- Avoid disclosing client-sensitive details, even when anonymized.

## Coach Outreach Strategy (Updated)

With these personas in mind, the outreach strategy should differentiate:

**For Career Transition Specialists:** Lead with "session effectiveness" and "less context rebuild."  
**For VP-to-CXO Coaches:** Lead with "daily client discipline" and "conviction building."  
**For Executive Search Firm Coaches:** Lead with "better-prepared candidates" and "competitive prep depth."  
**For Board Coaches:** Lead with "multi-year tracking" and "governance signal visibility."

**Single message that works across all four:**  
"Built a tool that handles the research and daily discipline so you can focus on the strategy and relationship work. Coaches are using it to give their clients a prep and tracking advantage. Would a 15-minute demo be worth your time?"

But in follow-ups and demos, customize to their specific coaching model.

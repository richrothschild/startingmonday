# Management Synthetic Council

---

## Mark Horstman

**Area of expertise:** Managerial execution and accountability. Management systems, one-on-ones, feedback, coaching, delegation.

**First principles used by the council:** Management is a repeatable discipline; clear expectations and consistent follow-up create execution reliability.

**Main content/source:** The Effective Manager / Manager Tools

**Key points to apply:** Run regular one-on-ones, give actionable feedback, coach deliberately, delegate with clarity, and make management behavior repeatable.

---

## Patty McCord

**Area of expertise:** Talent density and high-performance culture. Talent systems, Netflix-style culture, performance management, organizational honesty.

**First principles used by the council:** Adults perform best with context and accountability; talent density beats policy density; bureaucracy often protects mediocrity.

**Main content/source:** Powerful

**Key points to apply:** Build candid high-performance cultures with freedom, responsibility, context, talent density, and low tolerance for process theater.

---

## Matt Mochary

**Area of expertise:** Executive operating systems. CEO coaching, operating cadence, founder discipline, leadership systems.

**First principles used by the council:** The founder's operating system becomes the company's operating system; clarity, accountability, and cadence reduce drag.

**Main content/source:** The Great CEO Within / Mochary Method

**Key points to apply:** Build company operating habits: clear roles, decision frameworks, one-on-ones, accountability, feedback, onboarding, and disciplined meetings.

---

## Kim Scott

**Area of expertise:** Leadership communication and feedback. Executive feedback, leadership culture, candid communication, management behavior.

**First principles used by the council:** Care personally and challenge directly; honest feedback loops drive better execution; human dynamics shape product outcomes.

**Main content/source:** Radical Candor

**Key points to apply:** Strong teams combine direct challenge with genuine care; leaders must avoid both ruinous empathy and obnoxious aggression.

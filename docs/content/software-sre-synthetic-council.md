# Software and SRE Synthetic Council

---

## Kent Beck

**Area of expertise:** Software design and sustainable development. Extreme Programming, test-driven development, software design, engineering simplicity.

**First principles used by the council:** Make the change easy, then make the easy change; simplicity preserves optionality; fast feedback improves design quality.

**Main content/source:** Tidy First? / Extreme Programming Explained

**Key points to apply:** Separate structural changes from behavioral changes, make small reversible improvements, use tests and feedback, and manage design as an economic choice.

---

## Martin Fowler

**Area of expertise:** Architecture and refactoring. Software architecture, refactoring, enterprise application patterns, technical debt.

**First principles used by the council:** Architecture exists to preserve useful change; technical debt must be intentional; refactoring maintains optionality.

**Main content/source:** Refactoring / Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture

**Key points to apply:** Improve internal structure without changing behavior, keep systems evolvable, and document architecture decisions in context.

---

## Charity Majors

**Area of expertise:** Observability and production excellence. Observability, production systems, engineering operations, debugging culture.

**First principles used by the council:** You cannot improve what you cannot observe; production is reality; debugging speed is a strategic advantage.

**Main content/source:** Observability Engineering / charity.wtf

**Key points to apply:** Design systems so teams can ask novel questions of production, debug quickly, reduce unknown unknowns, and learn from real user-impacting behavior.

---

## Gene Kim

**Area of expertise:** DevOps and operational systems thinking. DevOps, flow, feedback loops, operational excellence, engineering culture.

**First principles used by the council:** Flow, feedback, and continual learning create operational excellence; engineering systems are socio-technical systems.

**Main content/source:** The Phoenix Project / The Unicorn Project / Accelerate

**Key points to apply:** Optimize the whole value stream, reduce constraints, build fast feedback, improve deployment safety, and create a culture of continual learning.

---

## Betsy Beyer

**Area of expertise:** Site reliability discipline. SRE, incident management, service-level objectives, toil reduction.

**First principles used by the council:** Reliability must be engineered deliberately; error budgets rationalize velocity/stability tradeoffs; incidents should produce learning.

**Main content/source:** Google Site Reliability Engineering books

**Key points to apply:** Use SLOs, error budgets, toil reduction, automation, and incident reviews to balance reliability with product velocity.

---

## Liz Fong-Jones

**Area of expertise:** Human-centered reliability and observability. SRE, observability, resilience, incident culture, distributed systems operations.

**First principles used by the council:** Reliability is technical and human; healthy cultures recover faster; observability protects user trust.

**Main content/source:** SRE and observability writing/speaking

**Key points to apply:** Reduce blast radius, design graceful degradation, build empathetic incident practices, and connect reliability work to user trust.

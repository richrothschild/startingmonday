# Trial and Acquisition Strategy + Synthetic Council

Date: 2026-05-22
Owner: Founder Office (Rich)
Purpose: Maximize qualified customer trials and paid acquisition before and during Sprint 1

## Objective

Increase high-fit trial starts and improve trial-to-paid conversion without diluting brand trust.

Primary business outcomes for the next 12 weeks:

1. Increase qualified trial starts from target circles by 2x.
2. Reach trial-to-paid conversion of at least 30 percent for qualified trials.
3. Reduce time to first value in trial to under 48 hours.
4. Build repeatable acquisition loops through coaches, search-adjacent advisors, and operator communities.

## Strategy Thesis

Starting Monday should win with trust-first founder-led demand plus high-precision conversion paths.

Acquisition model:

1. Create demand with founder authority and evidence-backed insights.
2. Capture demand with role-specific trial offers and low-friction onboarding.
3. Convert demand with fast proof of value in week 1.
4. Compound demand with referral and partner loops tied to real outcomes.

## Target Segments and Offer Mapping

### Segment A: Senior executives in active transition

Pain: lack of signal visibility, inconsistent prep, stalled pipeline momentum.

Offer:

1. 30-day structured trial with clear week-1 outcomes.
2. First-session prep brief and signal action checklist.

### Segment B: Executive coaches with transition clients

Pain: paid session time lost to context rebuild and weak between-session accountability.

Offer:

1. Coach preview with two to three live client seats.
2. Pass-fail scorecard at day 30 with coaching-specific success metrics.

### Segment C: Search-adjacent advisors and retained-search affiliates

Pain: candidate readiness variance and reactive interview preparation.

Offer:

1. Readiness framework briefing plus trial path for selected candidates.
2. Evidence pack showing interview-readiness process and outcomes.

## Funnel Design (Trust to Trial to Paid)

### Top of funnel (authority and relevance)

1. Founder insight series (3 posts per week) anchored in executive-search readiness.
2. One weekly long-form memo with proof block (denominator, timeframe, confidence).
3. Strategic distribution in target circles through high-value comments and direct peer conversations.

### Middle of funnel (intent and qualification)

1. Role-specific landing paths for executives, coaches, and advisors.
2. Single-page trial decision brief with expected outcomes by day 7 and day 30.
3. Fast qualification questions to route users into correct onboarding flow.

### Bottom of funnel (activation and conversion)

1. Day-1 setup outcome: first signal action completed.
2. Day-3 outcome: first prep brief reviewed before a real conversation.
3. Day-7 outcome: one momentum diagnosis and next-step plan.
4. Day-30 outcome: pass-fail decision with recommended paid tier.

## Channel Plan

Priority channels:

1. LinkedIn founder channel for demand creation.
2. Email and direct outreach to targeted high-fit nodes.
3. Podcasts, webinars, and guest articles for borrowed trust.
4. Partner channels through executive coaches and advisor networks.

Channel rule:

Do not add a new channel until current primary channels are Green for two consecutive weeks.

## Visibility and Domain Authority Program

Goal: increase discoverability, trust, and branded demand so trials grow from both direct and organic channels.

### Domain authority workstream

1. Publish one high-authority, citation-worthy asset per month (research report, benchmark, or methodology paper).
2. Execute digital PR outreach to secure backlinks from credible business, career, leadership, and technology publications.
3. Build partner co-marketing pages with coach and advisor partners that include editorial links.
4. Strengthen internal linking from high-traffic pages to trial-conversion pages.
5. Maintain a quarterly keyword map focused on executive transition and readiness intent terms.

### Brand visibility workstream

1. Increase branded-search demand with consistent founder narrative and repeated category language.
2. Build a repeatable media pipeline for podcasts, guest bylines, and quote opportunities.
3. Establish recurring public artifacts that drive recall: quarterly report, monthly market memo, weekly insight post.
4. Use executive-grade proof blocks on every major public page to increase trust conversion.

## Conferences, Webinars, and Speaking Strategy

### Conferences

1. Prioritize 8 to 12 conferences where executive coaches, CHRO leaders, search-adjacent advisors, and senior operators gather.
2. Target one of three outcomes per event: speaking slot, panel participation, or curated roundtable host role.
3. Use event-specific landing pages and QR trial offers to track attribution.
4. Ship post-event follow-up sequence within 72 hours with one proof asset and one call to action.

### Webinars and virtual briefings

1. Run one webinar or private briefing per month focused on readiness, not product feature tours.
2. Co-host with credible partners (coach leaders, search advisors, industry operators) to borrow trust.
3. Require one practical takeaway artifact (checklist, scorecard, framework sheet) for every session.
4. Route registrants to segmented trial journeys based on role.

## LinkedIn Sales and Social Presence Strategy

2. Run a weekly outreach cadence: 12 high-context touches with asset-led value.
3. Use role-specific message tracks tied to one pain and one proof point.

3. Maintain daily engagement in target conversations through thoughtful comments.
4. Use social listening for objection discovery and content roadmap updates.
5. Avoid vanity posting. Every post must map to one funnel stage.

## Operating Cadence

Weekly:

1. Review funnel metrics against Green/Yellow/Red thresholds.
2. Run one council review on highest-impact acquisition asset.
3. Ship one controlled experiment with clear success metric.
4. Review LinkedIn sales funnel stages and unblock stalled high-fit leads.
5. Track media, event, and backlink pipeline progress.

Biweekly:

1. Prioritize next sprint experiments by expected revenue impact and reversibility.
2. Remove one low-performing motion and reallocate effort.
3. Review conference and webinar calendar pipeline for the next 60 days.

Monthly:

1. Publish acquisition review memo with results, misses, and next bets.
2. Update council assumptions based on observed buyer behavior.
3. Publish one authority report or insight brief designed to earn references and backlinks.
4. Audit domain-authority movement and branded-demand growth against targets.

This strategy is successful when high-fit prospects enter trial faster, activate in week 1, and convert with clear evidence of value.

# Human Writing Synthetic Council Prompt And Rubric

## Purpose

Use this council to make every post feel professional, witty, lightly humorous, and unmistakably human in under 10 minutes.

This is not a style-imitation exercise. Use each writer as a lens for critique, not a voice clone.

---

## Council Setup

### Council Members And Jobs

1. Ann Handley (clarity + warmth): remove jargon, tighten language, keep the reader in the room.

1. Morgan Housel (story + insight): turn abstract claims into a concrete scene or observation.

1. Adam Grant (credibility + nuance): strengthen claims with evidence and confidence-level discipline.

1. Nora Ephron (wit + humanity): add one light line of humor or self-awareness without becoming performative.

1. Brene Brown (vulnerability + courage): add emotional honesty and real stakes without oversharing.

1. Robert Gottlieb (final editor): enforce ruthless clarity, pacing, and line-level precision; cut anything indulgent or soft.

1. Anne Applebaum (Pulitzer-winning final approver): enforce credibility, intellectual honesty, and consequence-level framing for serious claims.

### Why These Two Were Added

- Robert Gottlieb: one of the most respected modern editors, known for sharp structural judgment and precise line editing across major nonfiction.
- Anne Applebaum: Pulitzer-winning writer with a disciplined evidence culture and clear, high-trust explanatory voice under complex stakes.

### Final Approver Rule

No post is publish-ready until both final approvers pass it:

- Gottlieb pass: wording is tight, opening is strong, no dead sentences.
- Applebaum pass: claims are fair, evidence-aware, and not overstated.

### Voice Guardrails (Always On)

- Short sentences beat clever complexity.
- One smile line is enough. No stand-up routine.
- Replace abstractions with specifics.
- Use contractions and natural rhythm.
- Keep one line that only you could have written.

### Emotional Angle Rotation (Required)

Assign one primary emotional angle before drafting and rotate it across adjacent posts.

Approved angles:

- candid humility
- practical relief
- conviction
- earned optimism
- urgency
- grounded concern

Rotation rule:

- Do not publish two adjacent posts with the same primary emotional angle.
- If two drafts feel tonally similar, rewrite the opening and middle tension line before final approval.

---

## 10-Minute Workflow

### Minute 0-2: Draft

Write the post fast in your own voice. Do not edit while drafting.

### Minute 2-6: Five Fast Council Passes

Run one pass per member. Keep only one action from each pass.

### Minute 6-8: Rubric Score

Score quickly using the table below.

### Minute 8-10: Final Approver Pass And Publish Decision

Apply top 2 to 3 changes only, then run the two final approver checks.

Decision rule:

- Publish if score is 82 or higher and no critical fails.
- Revise if score is 70 to 81.
- Rewrite opening if score is below 70.

- Do not imitate any person directly.
- Use each council member only as a critique lens.
- Keep the post concise and readable.

- Ensure the emotional angle differs from the most recent published post.

Council lenses:
1) Ann Handley lens: clarity, warmth, readability.
2) Morgan Housel lens: concrete story/scene and insight.
3) Adam Grant lens: evidence quality, nuance, credibility.
4) Nora Ephron lens: light wit, self-awareness, humanity.
5) Brene Brown lens: emotional truth, courage, grounded stakes.

Then return:
1) Final revised post
2) Humanity score (0-100)
3) Top 3 reasons for that score
4) One risk if I publish as-is
5) Primary emotional angle used

Post draft:
[PASTE DRAFT]

## Human Quality Scoring Rubric (100 Points)

### 1) Human Signal And Specificity (25 points)

- 0 to 10: Vague, generic, no lived detail.
- 11 to 18: Some specifics, still broad.
- 19 to 25: Concrete moment, clear specificity, feels lived.

Checklist:

- Includes one concrete detail (number, role, date, moment, or quote).
- Contains one line that sounds like a real person, not content software.

### 2) Clarity And Readability (20 points)

- 0 to 8: Dense, corporate, hard to scan.
- 9 to 15: Mostly clear, occasional clutter.
- 16 to 20: Crisp, direct, easy to read in one pass.

Checklist:

- First two lines are clear.
- No heavy jargon.
- Sentence length varies naturally.

### 3) Credibility And Claim Discipline (20 points)

- 0 to 8: Overstated claims, no grounding.
- 9 to 15: Some support, mixed precision.
- 16 to 20: Claims are grounded, nuanced, and trustworthy.

Checklist:

- One primary claim only.
- Evidence or context included when needed.
- Uncertainty is acknowledged where appropriate.

### 4) Wit And Warmth (15 points)

- 0 to 5: Flat or robotic.
- 6 to 11: Friendly but predictable.
- 12 to 15: Light, tasteful humor and human warmth.

Checklist:

- Exactly one subtle smile line.
- Tone is professional, not snarky.

### 5) Emotional Honesty And Stakes (10 points)

- 0 to 3: Detached and impersonal.
- 4 to 7: Some emotion, low stakes.
- 8 to 10: Names real tension, grounded emotional truth.

Checklist:

- Identifies a real tension or tradeoff.
- Feels candid without oversharing.

### 6) CTA Quality And Conversation Potential (10 points)

- 0 to 3: Generic CTA or no invitation.
- 4 to 7: Acceptable prompt.
- 8 to 10: Clear invitation that sparks thoughtful responses.

Checklist:

- Ends with a specific invitation.
- CTA matches audience sophistication.

---

## Critical Fails (Automatic Revise)

Revise before publishing if any of these are true:

- Sounds like it could be posted by anyone with no edits.
- Contains inflated certainty with no evidence.
- Uses multiple humor lines that distract from substance.
- Reads like product marketing instead of lived operator insight.

---

## Fast Pre-Publish Checklist (30 Seconds)

- One real moment?
- One real claim?
- One subtle smile line?
- One honest tension?
- One clear invitation?

If all five are yes, publish.

---

## Optional: Weekly Calibration Loop

Once per week, review your last 3 posts and log:

- Humanity score at publish time.
- Actual performance (saves, thoughtful comments, inbound quality).
- Which rubric category best predicted results.

Use that to tune next week’s drafts.

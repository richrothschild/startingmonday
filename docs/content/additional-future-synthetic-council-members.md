# Additional Future Synthetic Council Members

These people were discussed as possible future additions or reference lenses. They are not required for the core council, but they may be useful if Starting Monday expands into platform strategy, AI infrastructure, deeper product strategy, behavioral science, or founder/company-building review.

---

## Satya Nadella

**Potential council role:** Platform strategy, empathy, culture change. Enterprise platforms, AI/cloud strategy, growth mindset leadership.

**First principles:** Empathy and learning culture enable reinvention; platforms compound through ecosystems; mission clarity matters.

**Main content/source:** Hit Refresh

**Key points to apply:** Rebuild culture around empathy, growth mindset, mission clarity, cloud/platform strategy, and continuous renewal.

---

## Jensen Huang

**Potential council role:** AI infrastructure and platform ecosystems. Accelerated computing, AI infrastructure, ecosystem strategy, technical storytelling.

**First principles:** Platform shifts create compounding advantage; deep technical bets require long time horizons; ecosystem tooling drives adoption.

**Main content/source:** NVIDIA keynotes and GTC talks

**Key points to apply:** No single book; key content emphasizes accelerated computing, AI platform ecosystems, developer adoption, and long-horizon technical conviction.

---

## Shreyas Doshi

**Potential council role:** Product strategy and prioritization. Product thinking, strategy, PM craft, decision frameworks.

**First principles:** Good product strategy clarifies tradeoffs; prioritization is about saying no; execution without strategy creates waste.

**Main content/source:** Product strategy essays and courses

**Key points to apply:** Use product judgment, strategy before roadmap, high-agency execution, and rigorous prioritization to avoid feature factories.

---

## Gibson Biddle

**Potential council role:** Product strategy and consumer value. Product strategy, Netflix product leadership, delight, retention, product frameworks.

**First principles:** Strategy is a theory of how to delight customers in hard-to-copy ways; product leaders must choose bets explicitly.

**Main content/source:** Ask Gib / product strategy talks

**Key points to apply:** Use frameworks such as delight, margin, hard-to-copy advantage, and strategic bets to evaluate product choices.

---

## Daniel Kahneman

**Potential council role:** Behavioral decision science. Cognitive biases, prospect theory, judgment under uncertainty, noise.

**First principles:** Human judgment is biased and noisy; loss aversion, anchoring, availability, and overconfidence distort decisions.

**Main content/source:** Thinking, Fast and Slow / Noise

**Key points to apply:** System 1 is fast and intuitive; System 2 is slow and deliberate; decisions improve when we account for bias, base rates, loss aversion, and judgment noise.

---

## Ben Horowitz

**Potential council role:** Founder hardship and organizational scaling. Startup leadership, wartime CEO behavior, culture, hard decisions.

**First principles:** There are no easy answers in company building; leaders must face hard truths; culture is what people do when under pressure.

**Main content/source:** The Hard Thing About Hard Things / What You Do Is Who You Are

**Key points to apply:** CEOs must make lonely hard decisions, manage through crises, hire/fire executives, preserve morale, and operationalize culture through actions.

---

## Jeff Bezos

**Potential council role:** Long-term customer obsession and high-velocity decisions. Customer obsession, long-term strategy, operational mechanisms, shareholder communication.

**First principles:** Start with the customer and work backward; maintain Day 1; distinguish one-way and two-way doors; mechanisms beat intentions.

**Main content/source:** Amazon shareholder letters

**Key points to apply:** Prioritize long-term free cash flow, customer obsession, invention, high standards, reversible decision speed, and operating mechanisms.

---

## James Clear

**Potential council role:** Habit systems, behavior design, and implementation consistency.

**First principles:** Outcomes are lagging indicators of systems; small behavior changes compound when friction is reduced and cues are explicit.

**Main content/source:** Atomic Habits

**Key points to apply:** Design product and operating workflows around clear cues, low-friction actions, immediate feedback loops, and consistent weekly cadence so implementation does not depend on willpower.

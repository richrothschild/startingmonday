# Business Development Partnerships Synthetic Council

## Pod Purpose

The BD pod answers:
- Who already has trust with our target users?
- Which partners know when executives are entering career transition?
- Which ecosystem can create repeatable referral flow?
- Which strategic partnerships improve credibility?
- Which partner categories could become future revenue channels?

This pod is different from outreach sales.

Sales asks: How do we get this account to buy?

Business development asks: What ecosystem relationships create leverage, distribution, credibility, or embedded demand?

For Starting Monday, this may be more important than pure outbound.

**Potential BD channels:** CIO associations, executive coaches, retained search firms, PE operating partner networks, leadership communities, alumni executive networks, outplacement firms, cybersecurity/IT leadership groups, higher-ed IT associations, digital transformation consultancies, fractional CIO networks.

## Council Members

### Bob Moore
**Area of expertise:** Ecosystem-led growth. Co-founder and CEO of Crossbeam and author of Ecosystem-Led Growth. Focused on using partner ecosystems to generate leads, close deals, and grow companies.

**Starting Monday lens:** Identify where partner data, overlapping audiences, and ecosystem relationships can create qualified demand.

**First principles he brings:**
- Growth can come from ecosystems, not just direct sales.
- Partner overlap reveals hidden demand.
- Co-selling and shared context improve conversion.
- Ecosystems can become a scalable revenue source.

**Key questions he asks:**
- Who already has trust with your target executive?
- Which partners own the moment before the user needs you?
- Which relationships create repeatable referral flow?
- Can partner data improve targeting and conversion?

**Best application to Starting Monday:** Build a partner map around entities that already know when executives are in motion: executive coaches, retained search firms, recruiters, CIO communities, PE talent teams, outplacement firms, leadership development firms.

---

### Jay McBain
**Area of expertise:** Channels, partnerships, alliances, and ecosystems. Chief analyst at Canalys. Widely associated with the shift from traditional channels to ecosystem-based GTM.

**Starting Monday lens:** Think beyond one-off partnerships and design a broader ecosystem strategy around influence, channels, alliances, and partner-led buyer journeys.

**First principles he brings:**
- Modern B2B buying is ecosystem-influenced.
- Buyers are shaped by many trusted parties before vendor contact.
- Partnerships must be treated as GTM infrastructure, not side deals.
- Channels, alliances, communities, and platforms all shape distribution.

**Key questions he asks:**
- Which ecosystem influences the executive before they search?
- What are the key decision moments in the buyer journey?
- Which partner categories influence trust?
- Is BD being treated as a strategic growth system or ad hoc networking?

**Best application to Starting Monday:** Create a partner ecosystem strategy around: credibility partners, lead referral partners, data/signal partners, co-selling partners, content/community partners, and advisory partners.

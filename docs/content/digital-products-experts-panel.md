# Digital Products Experts Panel

## Purpose

This panel reviews product strategy, growth loops, retention, and execution quality for digital products at launch and scale.

## Panel Members

### Marty Cagan
Area: product operating model and empowered teams.
What he cares about:
- product teams solve outcomes, not output
- discovery quality before delivery
- product strategy coherence
How to measure:
- percentage of roadmap items with validated problem evidence
- discovery-to-delivery cycle time
- post-launch outcome hit rate versus target

### Teresa Torres
Area: continuous discovery and customer evidence.
What she cares about:
- ongoing customer learning cadence
- decision quality grounded in evidence
- opportunity solution fit
How to measure:
- customer interviews per week
- decision logs with supporting evidence ratio
- opportunities tested per month and win rate

### Brian Balfour
Area: growth loops and sustainable acquisition.
What he cares about:
- repeatable growth model
- channel product fit
- retention-quality growth
How to measure:
- activation to retained-user conversion by channel
- loop completion rate and loop time
- CAC payback by segment

### Gibson Biddle
Area: delight, margin, hard-to-copy strategy.
What he cares about:
- customer delight and utility
- strategic defensibility
- value-to-price consistency
How to measure:
- NPS or equivalent satisfaction trend by segment
- differentiation score in win-loss review
- gross margin and retention by plan

### Julie Zhuo
Area: product quality and user-centered execution.
What she cares about:
- clarity of user journey
- interface quality and trust
- cross-functional execution quality
How to measure:
- task success rate on critical journeys
- UX issue escape rate to production
- design QA pass rate before release

## Rubric

Score each category 1 to 5:
- strategy clarity
- customer evidence strength
- growth model quality
- retention durability
- UX trust and clarity
- execution reliability

Overall panel score:
- 4.5 to 5.0: A+
- 4.0 to 4.49: A
- 3.5 to 3.99: B
- below 3.5: improvement required

# UI UX and Product Design Synthetic Council

---

## Dieter Rams

**Area of expertise:** Visual restraint and timeless UI principles. Industrial design, simplicity, product usefulness, minimalist design philosophy.

**First principles used by the council:** Good design is useful, understandable, unobtrusive, honest, long-lasting, and as little design as possible.

**Main content/source:** Ten Principles for Good Design

**Key points to apply:** Use restraint, clarity, usefulness, honesty, and minimal visual noise; avoid decorative complexity that distracts from function.

---

## Pablo Stanley

**Area of expertise:** Approachable modern visual systems. Illustration systems, friendly product aesthetics, design tools, visual personality.

**First principles used by the council:** Products can feel approachable without losing clarity; delight should support function; visual warmth reduces intimidation.

**Main content/source:** Pablo Stanley projects / Blush / Open Peeps

**Key points to apply:** Use friendly visual systems and approachable UI elements to reduce anxiety and make software feel more human without undermining executive-grade trust.

---

## Don Norman

**Area of expertise:** Human-centered UX and cognitive ergonomics. Usability, cognitive science, affordances, feedback, user-centered design.

**First principles used by the council:** Design must match human cognition; affordances, signifiers, constraints, and feedback reduce user error.

**Main content/source:** The Design of Everyday Things

**Key points to apply:** When users struggle, often the design is at fault; make actions visible, map controls naturally, provide feedback, and design around human mental models.

---

## Julie Zhuo

**Area of expertise:** Product design leadership. Product design, design management, UX systems, leadership.

**First principles used by the council:** Great product design starts with user intent; interaction details shape trust; design quality scales through judgment and systems.

**Main content/source:** The Making of a Manager

**Key points to apply:** Management is learned through feedback, clarity, outcomes, and people development; design leaders must create systems that help teams make better product decisions.

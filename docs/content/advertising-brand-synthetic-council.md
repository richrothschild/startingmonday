# Advertising & Brand Strategy Synthetic Council

## Pod Purpose

The Advertising pod answers:
- How do we build brand trust with senior executives?
- What messaging frameworks make Starting Monday credible to C-suite buyers?
- Which brand positioning compounds authority over time?
- How do we own a specific narrative that differentiates from competitors?
- Which campaigns create lasting conviction versus transactional demand?

This pod is different from outreach sales and marketing conversion metrics.

Sales asks: How do we get this account to convert?

Advertising asks: What narrative, positioning, and brand equity makes this the obvious choice before we ever talk to them?

For Starting Monday, brand is everything. A CIO in transition will only trust a platform that demonstrates it understands their world, not just their need.

## Council Members

### David Ogilvy
**Area of expertise:** Brand positioning, consumer psychology, and timeless advertising principles. Founder of Ogilvy & Mather. Author of "Confessions of an Advertising Man" and creator of the Marlboro Man, Maxwell House coffee positioning, and dozens of iconic campaigns.

**Starting Monday lens:** Build a brand architecture that targets the psychology and aspirations of senior executives, not just their problems.

**First principles he brings:**
- Brand is the promise made by the founder to the customer.
- The consumer (executive) is not a moron; she is your wife. Speak to her intelligence.
- Good copy is not creative; it is truthful and tested against reality.
- Invest in research and consumer insight before positioning. Spend on big ideas, not high volume.
- The brand name, visual identity, and key message must all work together.
- Repetition and consistency build brand equity over 24+ months.

**Key questions he asks:**
- What is the exact promise we are making to senior executives?
- Does our messaging assume executives are rational or emotional?
- What visual and verbal identity tells that story consistently?
- Are we testing messaging against real buyer psychology or guessing?
- Which media and platforms own the moment when an executive enters transition?
- What would make a CIO reach for Starting Monday before they even search?

**Best application to Starting Monday:**
- Position around readiness and early signal, not desperation or urgency.
- Create a distinctive visual and verbal brand (not generic SaaS language).
- Target the executive's aspiration to run a disciplined search, not the fear of job loss.
- Build a brand narrative about how strong candidates prepare before opportunities are public.
- Use case studies and proof from real executives, not feature lists.
- Create advertising that reaches executives 6-12 months before they enter transition.

---

### Rory Sutherland
**Area of expertise:** Irrational consumer behavior, lateral thinking, and psychological advertising. Vice-Chairman of Ogilvy Group. Specializes in using behavioral economics and psychology to solve advertising and business problems.

**Starting Monday lens:** Use behavioral psychology and contrarian positioning to make Starting Monday the obvious, non-negotiable choice for senior executives.

**First principles he brings:**
- People are irrational in predictable ways. Use this, don't fight it.
- The perceived value is often more important than the real value.
- Lateral thinking and reframing often beat obvious solutions.
- Small changes in framing create massive perception shifts.
- Status, belonging, and narrative matter more than features.
- The best advertising changes how people *think* about a category, not just a product.

**Key questions he asks:**
- What is the status play here? Why would a CIO *want* to be associated with this?
- What narrative makes Starting Monday prestigious, not desperate?
- Where is the contrarian insight that flips the category?
- What small perceptual change makes this feel essential before it feels optional?
- How do we make executives feel smarter, not needier, for using this?
- What behavioral trigger makes someone willing to act before their conscious mind says it's time?

**Best application to Starting Monday:**
- Reframe "job search tool" as "executive intelligence system" — status vs. desperation.
- Use scarcity and exclusivity positioning: "For executives who prepare before the market knows they should."
- Create FOMO for the right audience: "The executives you're competing with are already tracking these companies."
- Position confidence and readiness, not anxiety. "Be ready" beats "Don't get caught off guard."
- Use social proof from high-status executives, not quantity of users.
- Build aspiration around the *process* of being a disciplined executive searcher, not the outcome.
- Create perceived exclusivity: access to intelligence others don't have yet.

## Current Starting Monday Brand Thesis (For Reference)

**Promise:** Starting Monday gives senior executives the intelligence, preparation depth, and relationship discipline to run a better search than competitors run — before opportunities are widely visible.

**Target Psychology:** Executives want to feel in control, prepared, and ahead of the market. They want to avoid reactive scrambling. They want the narrative to be "I was ready when the right opportunity appeared" not "I panicked when my role was eliminated."

**Visual/Verbal Brand:** Clean, direct, no-BS language. Assume the executive is brilliant. Dark backgrounds (not fluffy). Orange accent (energy, readiness, action). Typography is sharp and readable. Tone is peer-to-peer, not coach-to-client.

**Key Differentiator:** Not "help you job search faster" but "give you early signals and prep depth that separate great searches from reactive ones."

**Status Play:** Using Starting Monday signals you are a disciplined, prepared executive. Not using it signals you are relying on luck and conventional wisdom.

# Starting Monday — B2B Market Attack Plan
**Version 1.0 — May 8, 2026**
**Status: Canonical. Five-pass refined before delivery.**

---

## The Core Insight

B2B does not have the timing problem that plagues B2C. A career coach is always in the market for tools that help them scale. A VC/PE operating partner is always responsible for portfolio executive transitions. A university career center director is always looking to differentiate their alumni services. These are permanent problems, not episodic ones. The constraint is **awareness and trust**, not timing.

The second insight: every B2B buyer in this space has a different motivation. Career coaches want to serve more clients without burning out. VC/PE partners want to fulfill a fiduciary duty efficiently. Career center directors want peer-validated, differentiated programs. Outplacement firm leaders want to modernize their offering without overhauling their delivery model. The pitch must lead with **their** problem, not SM's features. The product solves it — but you have to earn the right to show them how.

The third insight: B2B revenue at scale is likely to exceed B2C revenue. At Year 3, the Coach tier alone (50 coaches × 6 clients × $599/month) generates more MRR than 200 Active-tier B2C users. The B2C product is the proof of concept. The B2B product is the margin engine.

## Channel 1 — Career Coaches (Coach Tier, $599/month)

**The fastest B2B path. Closes in days. No procurement process.**

### The coach's problem
An independent career coach with 5 clients earns $150-500/hour × 5 clients × 10 sessions/engagement = $7,500-25,000/engagement. They are bandwidth-constrained. They cannot take more clients without degrading quality — and every experienced coach knows the reputation cost of over-extending. Starting Monday removes the mechanical work (monitoring, briefing delivery, follow-up tracking) so the coach can take 8-10 clients with the same relational depth they currently offer 5.

### The pitch
Not "our platform helps executives." That's a feature pitch to the wrong person.

**Correct pitch:** "You do the strategy and the relationship. Starting Monday handles the monitoring, the daily briefing, the follow-up tracking, and the prep brief delivery. Take on 2 more clients without burning out. Your engagement quality goes up; your capacity doubles."

**The math for the coach:**
- Current: 5 clients × $500/session × 10 sessions = $25,000 in billings
- With SM: 8 clients × $500/session × 10 sessions = $40,000 in billings
- SM cost: $599/month × 5 months (typical engagement) = $2,995
- Net gain per engagement cycle: $15,000 - $2,995 = $12,005
- ROI: 5x. This is an easy yes once the math is visible.

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator Playbook

**Setup (Month 3, Week 1):** Activate LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/month). Create saved search:
- Title contains: "career coach" OR "executive coach" OR "outplacement consultant"
- Location: United States
- Active on LinkedIn: Yes (posted in last 30 days)
- Connection degree: 2nd degree preferred (shared connections increase response rate by 40%)
- Company size: Individual or small firm (avoiding large outplacement firms — they have procurement)

**Volume:**
- 20 connection requests per day (LinkedIn's safe limit)
- 10 InMail messages per day (to 3rd-degree connections)
- Total weekly outreach: 150-200 touches

**The DM (after connection accepted):**
---
"[Name] — saw you coach executives at [director/VP/CIO level]. I built a tool that several coaches are using to handle the monitoring and daily briefing delivery for their clients, so they can take on more engagements without the manual work. Would a 15-minute demo be useful? No obligation — just happy to show you what it does."
---

**What NOT to say:**
- "I'd love to connect and explore synergies"
- "Starting Monday is an AI-powered platform that..."
- Anything with more than 3 sentences in the opener

**Follow-up sequence:**
- Day 3 (if no response): Brief follow-up: "Happy to share a brief demo video if easier."
- Day 7 (if no response): "No worries if not relevant — just let me know."
- No further follow-up. Move on. Coaches who don't respond in 2 follow-ups are not going to convert.

**Demo call structure (15 minutes):**
1. "Tell me about a typical search you run for an executive client." (2 min — let them talk)
2. "Here's what SM does for that client automatically." (5 min — live demo of daily briefing, company scan, outreach draft)
3. "Here's your coach dashboard — what you see across all clients." (3 min)
4. "Any questions?" (3 min)
5. "30-day free trial to run a real client through it. If it doesn't make your life easier, cancel." (2 min)

## Channel 2 — VC/PE Executive Transition Support

**Highest ROI per deal. Shortest enterprise sales cycle. Completely unaddressed in prior planning.**

### The problem
VC/PE funds manage executive transitions constantly. A portfolio company fires its CIO after a bad quarter. An EVP departs after an acquisition. A COO is restructured out. These transitions happen 10-25 times per year per active fund. The departing executive is a valued relationship — someone the GP or operating partner has worked closely with. The fund has both a fiduciary responsibility and a relationship investment in seeing that person land well.

Current options: a referral to an outplacement firm (takes 6 months, costs $15-50K, the executive often dislikes the process), or nothing. Neither is great. SM is a third option: fast, specific, controlled by the executive, available at 11pm when they're anxious.

### The pitch
Not to the executive. To the operating partner or GP who manages people operations.

**Correct pitch:** "When you have a portfolio executive transition, we can have them set up and running in 20 minutes. They get daily briefings, company monitoring, and AI coaching. You fulfill your duty to them without a 6-month outplacement engagement. Cost: $200-600 per transition depending on duration. Takes 15 minutes to set up."

### The trigger-based outreach approach

Cold outreach to VC/PE partners has a low response rate. Trigger-based outreach — where you reference a specific, visible event — has a 5-10x higher response rate.

**The trigger:** When a VC-backed company announces an executive departure or leadership change, it appears in:
- The company's press release or LinkedIn announcement
- Crunchbase or PitchBook executive change data
- LinkedIn's "new position" signals for the departing executive
- TechCrunch, The Information, or venture beat coverage

**The outreach (within 48 hours of the trigger):**
---
"[Name] — saw [Company] recently made a leadership change at the [CIO/CTO/EVP] level. I built a tool that several operating partners are using to help departing portfolio executives run their next search efficiently — daily briefings, company monitoring, AI-drafted outreach. Takes 20 minutes to set up and costs a fraction of traditional outplacement. Happy to show you in 10 minutes if it's useful for situations like this."
---

This message works because:
1. It references a specific event (not generic)
2. It frames the cost in comparison to outplacement (their current alternative)
3. It's appropriately brief for a GP's attention span

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator Playbook

Saved search:
- Title: "operating partner" OR "talent partner" OR "chief of staff" at VC/PE firms
- Company type: Venture Capital or Private Equity
- Location: US
- Activity: Active in past 30 days

Volume: 10 targeted DMs/week (lower volume, higher personalization)

## Channel 3 — University Career Centers and MBA Programs

**Network effect. One deal becomes five through peer referrals.**

### The problem
University career centers compete on alumni career outcomes. A top MBA program's career center is judged by placement rates, compensation achieved, and time-to-placement. Any tool that demonstrably improves those metrics is worth paying for. The current state: most career centers recommend generic tools (LinkedIn Premium, Teal) that aren't purpose-built for executive search.

### Revenue model
Annual institutional license: $8,000-$25,000 depending on alumni base size.
- Small program (500 annual alumni in search): $8,000/year
- Mid-size program (1,500 alumni): $15,000/year
- Large program (3,000+ alumni): $25,000/year

Covers unlimited use for alumni in active search during the license period. Includes:
- White-labeled daily briefings (school branding)
- Aggregate usage dashboard for the career center director
- Quarterly report: "how your alumni searched and what worked"

### The Wake Forest / Stanford Zoom (May 18) is the foundation

One satisfied career center director is worth more than 20 cold calls. Approach the May 18 Zoom as relationship-building, not selling.

**For the Zoom:**
1. Live demo of the product (show a prep brief for a company relevant to their alumni)
2. "I want your honest reaction to whether this would be useful to your alumni"
3. Offer: 10 free trial codes for alumni who are currently in search. No strings. Just collect feedback.
4. Ask: "Who else in your career network should see this?" (This is the moment that opens 5 more doors if the answer is positive)

**The NACE conference play:**
Submit a session proposal to the NACE annual conference: "AI and the executive alumni search: what career centers are using and what works." A 45-minute session at NACE reaches 2,500+ career center professionals. One conference appearance = 30-50 warm leads. Submit proposal for Year 1 Q4 conference.

**NACE membership:** $400/year. Pays for itself with one institutional lead.

### University acquisition projections

| Period | New institutional licenses | Annual revenue |
|--------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Year 1 Q4 | 1 pilot | $8,000 |
| Year 2 | 4-6 schools | $48,000-$90,000 |
| Year 3 | 10-14 schools | $120,000-$210,000 |

## Channel 4 — Outplacement Firms (Mid-Size First)

**Not Lee Hecht Harrison first. 18-month sales cycle. Start with boutiques.**

### The problem with starting at the top
Lee Hecht Harrison, RiseSmart, Careerminds — these firms have IT security reviews, legal procurement committees, 12-18 month pilots, and enterprise pricing negotiations. They are Year 3 revenue, not Year 1 or Year 2.

### The right starting segment
Independent or boutique outplacement practices: 50-200 executives per year, founded by former HR executives, competing for clients against the Lee Hecht Harrisons by offering more personalized service. These firms make decisions in 4-8 weeks with 1-2 decision-makers.

**Their problem:** They differentiate on white-glove personal service but are limited by bandwidth. They can serve 20 executives well or 50 executives adequately. SM makes them more capable — they can serve 50 executives with the same quality they used to give 20.

**The pitch:**
"Most outplacement firms give executives a career coach and a Zoom call schedule. You give them more. Starting Monday gives you monitoring, daily briefings, and AI prep for every client — without adding headcount. Your top-line differentiation stays; your delivery scales."

**Revenue model:**
- Per-seat bulk pricing: $75/seat/month (below retail, above COGS)
- Annual contract minimum 25 seats: $22,500/year
- Contract covers all executives they place in the system during the year

### Outplacement acquisition projections

| Period | Contracts signed | Seats | Annual revenue |
|--------|-----------------|-------|----------------|
| Year 1 | 0 | 0 | $0 |
| Year 2 | 1-2 boutiques | 25-50 | $22,500-$45,000 |
| Year 3 | 3-5 contracts | 75-150 | $67,500-$135,000 |

**Required before outplacement outreach:**
- Institutional features (admin dashboard, white-labeling, aggregate reporting)
- Reference from at least one career coach who uses SM (warm intro to boutique outplacement firms)
- Per-seat billing model in Stripe (different from individual subscriptions)

## Channel 5 — Retained Search Firm Referral Partnerships

**Zero commercial cost. Builds candidate network relationships.**

### The insight
Spencer Stuart, Korn Ferry, Heidrick & Struggles — every partner at these firms maintains a candidate network. Executives they've placed previously return to them when they're looking again. The search firm has every incentive to help those executives prepare well: better-prepared candidates = faster placements = more fees.

**Not a commercial relationship.** A referral relationship. The pitch to search firm partners:

"When your executive candidates are preparing for a search, this tool helps them monitor their target companies and draft their outreach. No commercial arrangement needed — I'll give you 10 codes for your candidate network. When they mention you recommended it and they land, that's a reference for you too."

**Why this works:**
- No cost to the search firm
- Makes their candidate network better-prepared (better candidates = better placements)
- They become informal ambassadors without a formal partnership
- One search firm partner who mentions SM to their network creates warm referrals with pre-transferred trust

**Approach:**
- Personal outreach through Rich's existing recruiter relationships (he already has them from his own search)
- Start with the 3-5 recruiters he has the warmest relationship with
- Give them 10 trial codes to share directly
- Follow up: "Did anyone find it useful? Who else in your network should see it?"

**Activation:** Month 9 (after 25+ happy paying users to reference)

## LinkedIn B2B Campaign Architecture

The B2C LinkedIn strategy (executive career intelligence content) reaches the wrong audience for B2B. B2B buyers — career coaches, HR leaders, VC/PE partners — read different content and need a separate approach.

### Two-track content strategy (parallel to B2C content)

**Track A (B2C — 3x/week):** Executive career intelligence for job seekers
- Data: what the scans show about hiring patterns
- How-to: outreach structures that work, timing signals
- Behind-the-build: authentic founder perspective

**Track B (B2B — 1-2x/week):** Practitioner intelligence for coaches and HR buyers
- "How to serve twice as many clients without burning out" (coaches)
- "What data-driven outplacement looks like vs. the legacy approach" (outplacement buyers)
- "The 6 weeks after a layoff announcement that determine executive placement outcomes" (VC/PE)
- "What career centers are building to differentiate their alumni services in 2026" (universities)

**Key discipline:** These must feel like genuine insights, not product marketing. If a career coach reads a Track B post and thinks "this person understands my world," they'll follow, engage, and eventually respond to a DM. If they read it and think "this is a vendor pitch," they'll scroll past.

### Association and podcast strategy

| Platform | Audience | Action | Timing |
|----------|----------|--------|--------|
| ICF (Int'l Coaching Federation) | Executive coaches | Webinar/chapter presentation | Month 6 |
| CCMC (Credentialed Career Manager) | Career management practitioners | Member newsletter | Month 6 |
| NACE | Career center directors | Conference presentation submission | Month 9 |
| SHRM | HR leaders, outplacement buyers | Chapter presentation, LinkedIn group | Year 2 |
| Talent10x podcast | HR leadership | Guest appearance | Month 9 |

**Cost:** ICF membership $200/year. CCMC membership $175/year. NACE membership $400/year. Total: $775/year for access to the three primary B2B audiences.

**Expected yield from one podcast appearance:** 3-8 qualified B2B leads with pre-built trust from the host's endorsement.

## The Honest Constraint

B2B requires the founder to wear a sales hat simultaneously with the product builder hat. The LinkedIn Sales Navigator outreach can be batched (2 hours/week, asynchronous), but demo calls cannot be automated. At 2-3 demo calls/week, the time investment is ~3-4 hours/week in Year 1. This is feasible alongside product development if the outreach is efficient (LinkedIn DMs, not cold calls; scheduled demos, not on-demand).

At Month 12-15, when B2B pipeline volume exceeds 10-15 qualified conversations in flight simultaneously, a part-time B2B business development contractor (10-15 hours/week at $2,000-3,000/month) becomes necessary. Budget for this in Month 12 planning.

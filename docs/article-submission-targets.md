# Article Submission Targets: Executive Coach Audience

Purpose: Prioritized list of publications and platforms where executive coaches read, trust, and share content. Mapped to the Starting Monday synthetic council personas.

Council alignment key:
- Goldsmith → behavior change, accountability, C-suite
- Ibarra → career reinvention, identity, transition
- Stanier → coaching conversations, practical habits
- Heynderickx → CIO/technology executive coaching
- Fernández-Aráoz → readiness, depth, preparation
- Solomon → sustained engagement, long-term coaching practice
- Godin → trust, tribes, smallest viable audience
- Cialdini → authority, social proof, trust signals

---

## Tier 1 — Highest Authority, Highest Trust

These are the outlets executive coaches read most and cite as authoritative.
Getting published here substantially increases credibility with coaches and their C-suite clients.

### 1. Harvard Business Review (HBR)
- URL: hbr.org
- Submission: hbr.org/guidelines-for-authors
- Format: 1,500-3,000 words | digital articles, or long-form
- Why it matters: Marshall Goldsmith, Herminia Ibarra, Claudio Fernández-Aráoz all publish here. Coaches use HBR to stay current and recommend to clients. An HBR byline creates instant authority with every persona on the council.
- Best angles for Starting Monday: "Why executive transitions fail: the intelligence gap"; "How the best coaches prepare their clients before every session"; "The between-session layer your clients need"
- Submission type: Submit to hbr.org or pitch editors at ideacast@hbr.org
- AP style: No — HBR has its own style guide. Clear, practitioner-focused prose.

### 2. MIT Sloan Management Review
- URL: sloanreview.mit.edu
- Submission: sloanreview.mit.edu/submit-an-article/
- Format: 3,000-5,000 words | rigorous, data-backed
- Why it matters: Technology executives and CIO coaches rely on Sloan for credible, research-grounded content. Aligns directly with Heynderickx and Fernández-Aráoz council personas.
- Best angles: "Signal intelligence as a leadership competency"; "How technology executives stay market-ready between searches"; "Prep infrastructure as coaching leverage"
- Submission type: Peer-reviewed and editor-reviewed tracks available.

### 3. Forbes (Leadership, Careers, or Technology sections)
- URL: forbes.com/sites/forbescoachescouncil/
- Submission: forbescoachescouncil.com (invitation-based; must apply)
- Format: 500-1,500 words | listicles to essays
- Why it matters: Forbes Coaches Council is read directly by executive coaches. Bylines here spread through LinkedIn coach networks immediately. Social proof and authority play (Cialdini).
- Best angles: "The five signals executive coaches should track for every client"; "Why coaching sessions fail before they start"; "One operating layer your coaching practice is missing"
- Submission type: Apply for Forbes Coaches Council membership. Once accepted, contribute directly.

### 4. Fast Company (Work Life / Leadership)
- URL: fastcompany.com
- Submission: fastcompany.com/contribute
- Format: 800-1,500 words | modern voice, data-driven, accessible
- Why it matters: High coach and VP-to-CXO candidate readership. Godin and Stanier council personas would find this resonant — permission marketing to the coaching community, not a pitch.
- Best angles: "The prep habit that separates effective executives from reactive ones"; "How I help clients stop rebuilding context every session"; "Why your clients are unprepared for their most important conversations"
- Submission type: Pitch form at fastcompany.com/contribute.

### 5. Inc. Magazine (Leadership, Coaching, Careers)
- URL: inc.com
- Submission: inc.com/contribute
- Format: 700-1,500 words | practical, actionable
- Why it matters: Strong readership among coach referral network and scaling professionals. Strong platform for coaches who want to attract next-level clients.
- Best angles: "The between-session layer every great coach knows about"; "How executive coaches add value without adding hours"; "The accountability tool your clients actually want"
- Submission type: Pitch at inc.com/contribute.

## Tier 2 — High Credibility, Niche Authority

These are publications executive coaches actively follow, often share, and use to vet vendors and ideas.

### 6. The Wall Street Journal (Journal Report / Leadership & Management)
- URL: wsj.com
- Submission: wsj.com/articles/submit-a-letter-or-opinion
- Format: 600-1,200 words | WSJ editorial voice; rigorous
- Why it matters: High-status outlet. Coaches who serve senior executives and board clients pay attention. Cialdini authority signal.
- Best angles: "Why executive job searches fail in the first two weeks"; "The intelligence gap companies ignore when hiring senior leaders"
- Submission type: opinion@wsj.com or the WSJ opinion submission portal.

### 7. LinkedIn (Articles and Newsletters)
- URL: linkedin.com
- Submission: Write directly on LinkedIn — articles or newsletter
- Format: 500-1,500 words
- Why it matters: Direct access to the executive coach community. Seth Godin's tribe-building principle at work. Posts and articles here spread through coach networks faster than any other channel. Zero gatekeeping.
- Best angles: All angles from Tiers 1 and 2, repurposed for direct coach audience.
- Submission type: Publish directly. Use a Starting Monday newsletter for recurring reach.

### 8. Coaching at Work (UK-based, international readership)
- URL: coaching-at-work.com
- Submission: coaching-at-work.com/contribute
- Format: 700-1,500 words | practitioner tone
- Why it matters: Read directly by ICF-certified coaches and organizational coaching practitioners. High signal-to-noise ratio for the Stanier and Solomon council personas.
- Best angles: "Infrastructure as a coaching differentiator"; "The unseen bottleneck in coaching transitions"
- Submission type: Email editorial team via the website contact.

### 9. Chief Executive
- URL: chiefexecutive.net
- Submission: chiefexecutive.net/contribute/
- Format: 600-1,200 words | peer-to-peer CEO voice
- Why it matters: Coaches use articles here when talking to board and C-suite clients. Landing a byline here reaches the buyer audience directly.
- Best angles: "The CEO prep discipline that prevents reactive decisions"; "Signal intelligence for executives in motion"
- Submission type: Pitch form on website.

### 10. SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management)
- URL: shrm.org
- Submission: Editorial contributions via shrm.org
- Format: 800-2,000 words | HR and talent leadership tone
- Why it matters: Corporate coaching buyers (HR, CHRO, talent teams) route through SHRM. Useful for the B2B/coaching partnership angle. Aligns with Solomon's sustained engagement focus.
- Best angles: "How executive coaches build lasting client accountability"; "What HR misses when evaluating external coaching tools"
- Submission type: Contact editorial@shrm.org.

## Tier 3 — Targeted Reach, Practitioner Trust

Smaller audiences but directly inside the coaching profession and C-suite talent community.

### 11. International Coaching Federation (ICF) Publications
- URL: coachingfederation.org
- Submission: Via ICF membership and publication requests
- Format: 600-1,500 words | practitioner evidence-based
- Why it matters: ICF-credentialed coaches trust ICF content by default. Strong authority signal for coaches who care about professional standards.
- Best angles: "Tech-enabled coaching: augmenting your practice without replacing your judgment"

### 12. Choice Magazine (The Magazine of Professional Coaching)
- URL: choice-online.com
- Submission: choice-online.com/contribute
- Format: 800-1,800 words | practitioner voice
- Why it matters: Direct coaching profession audience. Smaller but highly engaged. Coaches share Choice articles with peers.

### 13. Psychology Today (Careers/Leadership section)
- URL: psychologytoday.com
- Submission: Apply to blog at psychologytoday.com/us/blog
- Format: 600-1,200 words | accessible, applied
- Why it matters: Coaches recommend Psychology Today content to clients. Strong authority for emotional and identity coaching (Ibarra, Heynderickx alignment).
- Best angles: "The anxiety behind executive career transitions"; "What prepared executives actually do differently"

### 14. Korn Ferry Insights (Guest contribution or PR angle)
- URL: kornferry.com/insights
- Submission: Primarily internal, but PR or co-authorship possible
- Format: 700-1,500 words
- Why it matters: Executive coaches who work alongside Korn Ferry or similar firms read and share these insights. Social proof play.

### 15. Spencer Stuart Perspectives (Guest or PR angle)
- URL: spencerstuart.com/research-and-insight
- Submission: PR or collaborative relationship; not an open submission platform
- Format: Research-backed, 1,000-2,500 words
- Why it matters: C-suite coaches and search firm partners read Spencer Stuart. Getting quoted or co-bylined here carries extraordinary authority.

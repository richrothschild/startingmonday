# Starting Monday — B2C Market Attack Plan
**Version 1.0 — May 8, 2026**
**Status: Canonical. Five-pass refined before delivery.**

---

## The Core Insight

The B2C acquisition problem is a **timing problem**, not an awareness problem. Executive job search is episodic: a person who is happily employed today will be urgently searching tomorrow if the call comes. The window when someone will pay attention to a career tool is approximately 2-3 weeks wide — the moment they know they're looking. Every acquisition channel should be evaluated by one question: *does this reach the executive at or before that window?*

The second insight: the product must convert on **depth of value**, not breadth of features. A CIO evaluating SM in their first 10 minutes will compare it to ChatGPT, LinkedIn Premium, and their existing spreadsheet. The win comes from showing something none of those can do: a prep brief that knows their specific background, a scan match for a role at their target company that just posted, a follow-up draft that sounds like them at their sharpest. That is the conversion. Marketing creates the visit. The product makes the sale.

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (B2C Launch Focus)

**Primary:** Active-search executive (Persona 1 + 5)
- Title: CIO, CTO, VP IT, VP Engineering, VP Technology
- Compensation: $200K-$500K
- Situation: In transition (laid off, packaged out, or quietly looking)
- Geography: US major metros (Bay Area, NYC, Chicago, Austin, Boston, Seattle, Atlanta)
- Search urgency: High. Severance runway is finite. Every week matters.
- WTP: $129-249/month. Easy yes if the product is visibly excellent.

**Secondary:** Passive executive monitor (Persona 4)
- Employed, watching for the right opportunity
- WTP: $49/month
- LTV anchor: stays 12-24 months; low churn; stabilizes MRR as active-searchers cycle

**Defer for Phase 3:** VP/Director candidate (Persona 2) until Company Discovery Engine is live. Mid-career climber (Persona 3) until Executive product is proven and free competition is directly addressed.

## 2. Channel Strategy

Ranked by: (strategic leverage × timing alignment) ÷ (founder time required × cost)

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn Optimizer: The Trigger Moment Tool
**Priority: 1 (live now, optimize continuously)**

The LinkedIn Optimizer at `/optimize` is the most strategically positioned asset in the product. People update their LinkedIn profile at the exact moment they decide to start looking. This is the trigger moment — before they've made any tool decisions, before they've paid for anything, before their search has a structure.

**The conversion path:**
`/optimize` visit → immediate value (specific, ranked critique) → email capture for full PDF report → Day 0 welcome email → Day 3 trial signup CTA → Day 7 urgency follow-up → Trial → Paid

**What "elite" looks like for /optimize:**
- The critique must be specific, not generic. "Your headline is too broad" is worthless. "Your headline says 'Experienced Technology Leader' — a CIO recruiter scanning 40 profiles will skip this. Write it as the role you want: 'CIO | Digital Transformation | Enterprise Technology at Scale.'" That is what converts.
- Add a real-time score for each section (0-100), ranked by impact
- Add a "compare to top CIO profiles" benchmark if technically feasible
- The social share mechanic: "Share your optimization score on LinkedIn" with a simple graphic. Every share is a free acquisition.

**Metrics:**
- Target: 300 monthly visitors to /optimize by Month 3 → 600 by Month 6
- Email capture rate: ≥25% of visitors
- Trial conversion from email: ≥12%
- Effective B2C CAC from this channel: ~$15

### Channel 2 — Founder LinkedIn Content: The Daily Signal
**Priority: 1 (begin immediately, 3 posts/week, non-negotiable)**

The founder is the product at this stage. Rich Rothschild, transformation CIO in active search, built the tool he needed. That story is credible in a way a startup's marketing copy is not. One authentic post about what the data shows about CIO hiring reaches an audience that no ad spend can match at this cost.

**What to post:**

*Data posts (1x/week):*
"We scanned 400 career pages this week. Here's what we found about CIO hiring in Q2 2026." [3-5 findings]. "Want the full scan report? [link to /optimize or /demo]"

*Coaching posts (1x/week):*
"The one email structure that gets a response from an executive recruiter. Here's what works and why." [Specific, structured]. "I tested this in my own search at Cisco, TiVo, and Adobe. Pattern held."

*Behind-the-build posts (1x/week):*
"Built this because I needed it and it didn't exist." [Authentic, founder angle]. "Here's what I learned about the $300K+ job search that no tool was solving."

**What NOT to post:**
- "I'm excited to announce..." (any product announcement phrasing)
- Generic career advice ("networking is important")
- Anything that sounds like a marketing email in post form

**Format rules:**
- First line does the work: "The CIO job market just shifted. Here's what the data shows."
- Short paragraphs (1-2 lines max)
- No hashtag spam (1-2 at most, specific)
- Engage directly with comments within 1 hour of posting (LinkedIn algorithm rewards fast engagement)

**Metrics:**
- Follower target: 1,000 by Month 3; 3,000 by Month 6; 8,000 by Month 12
- Post reach target: avg 3,000-8,000 impressions/post by Month 3
- Trial signups from LinkedIn: ≥3/month by Month 3; ≥8/month by Month 6

### Channel 3 — Live Demo Page: Convert at the Wow Moment
**Priority: 1 (build in Sprint E, deploy Month 3)**

The demo page is the single highest-leverage conversion asset after /optimize. The principle: show the output before asking for the login. A CIO evaluating SM should be able to type "ServiceNow" and "CIO" and receive a streaming, real Claude prep brief — company intelligence, role analysis, likely interview questions, positioning advice — in 60 seconds. No login. No credit card. Just the output.

**Why this converts:**
The gap between "AI career tool" as a concept and "holy sh*t this knows exactly what to ask me in my ServiceNow interview" as an experience is 100% conversion-rate-difference. The demo page makes the product real before signup friction appears.

**Execution requirements:**
- Input: company name (or URL) + target title
- Output: streaming Claude prep brief, rendered in real time
- Quality: must be the best prep brief the user has ever seen. Not "here are some questions you might be asked." "Based on ServiceNow's Q4 2025 earnings call where CEO Bill McDermott emphasized AI copilot integration, and the CIO's role in deploying Now Assist across enterprise clients, you should expect: [specific questions, specific positioning]."
- CTA at end: "Get this for every company in your search. Free 30-day trial — no credit card."
- Email capture before showing full brief (after showing first 30% as a preview)

**Metrics:**
- Visitors from LinkedIn + /optimize referral: target 200/month by Month 4
- Email capture rate: ≥30% (higher intent than /optimize — they came for a specific company)
- Trial conversion from demo email: ≥20%

### Channel 4 — University Career Networks
**Priority: 2 (Wake Forest/Stanford Zoom is the entry point)**

The May 18 Zoom is not a marketing call. It is a product demo to someone who can refer SM to their alumni network. The approach:

**For the May 18 Zoom:**
1. Lead with the demo page (show it live — type their university's name + a relevant role)
2. Frame as "tool I wish I'd had during my search, built on my 30 years of search experience"
3. Ask: "Would you be comfortable recommending this to alumni in active search? Not a partnership — just a recommendation."
4. Leave with: 5 free trial codes for alumni they personally know who are in search
5. Follow up: send personalized notes to those 5 alumni from Rich directly

**Network effect:**
One satisfied career center endorser → NACE conference introduction → 5 more schools. This is how to get to 15 university relationships without a sales team. The first relationship is the only one you have to earn from scratch.

**Metrics:**
- Target from Wake Forest/Stanford: 5 trial users, 2-3 paying by Month 3
- Network expansion: 3-5 additional university contacts by Month 9
- Long-term: institutional license conversations beginning Month 12-15

### Channel 5 — Manager Tools Partnership
**Priority: 1 (strategically) / NOT TOUCHABLE BEFORE MONTH 15 (operationally)**

This is not a channel — it is a step-function event that requires the product to be earned. Mark Horstman is methodical, detail-oriented, and protective of his audience. A substandard product demo ends the opportunity permanently. A great one produces 500-2,000 trial signups in a week.

**What needs to be true before approaching:**
- 25+ paying users with documented positive outcomes
- Executive tier live with salary intelligence and interview prep
- Onboarding wizard complete (no "I didn't finish setting it up" feedback)
- AI output quality good enough to demo live on a podcast without hedging
- Infrastructure that doesn't fall over under 300 concurrent new signups

**Approach when ready:**
Personal connection, not cold pitch. Let the product demo make the case. "Would you be willing to try it for your next search-related content? I built it for executives exactly like your audience and I'd love your honest reaction."

**Treat Month 15 (August 2027) as a hard ship date** — begin assembling the qualifying evidence at Month 3. Collect testimonials, document outcomes, build the case. Do not improvise this.

### Channel 6 — LinkedIn Paid Advertising
**Priority: 3 (not before trial-to-paid is measured)**

Target audience: LinkedIn members with title CIO, CTO, VP IT, VP Technology + "Open to Work" signal OR recent "started new position" without an obvious next step.

**Ad creative direction:**
Lead with a specific, concrete claim — not "AI career tool."
- "We scanned 500 career pages this week. Here's what we found about CIO hiring." (curiosity-driven, sends to /optimize or a blog post)
- "Start monitoring your target companies before the search goes public." (specific, timely)
- "Your career coach is available 9-5. We're available at 11pm when you need that follow-up draft." (emotional + specific)

**Activation gate:**
Do NOT activate LinkedIn ads before trial-to-paid conversion is measured at ≥35%. At $100-200 CAC and 5-month average Active tenure ($645 LTV), the math requires ≥35% trial-to-paid to stay cash-positive per user. Activating at 20% conversion = CAC exceeds LTV within 12 months.

**Budget:** $500-$1,000/month starting Month 6 if conversion is proven; $2,000-$3,000/month at Month 12+

### Channel 7 — SEO: Own the Proprietary Content Space
**Priority: 3 (6-month lead time; start Month 3 for Month 9 results)**

Do not compete for "executive job search tips" or "career coaching advice" — LinkedIn, The Muse, and thousands of coaches dominate those terms. Compete for terms that only the Starting Monday platform can own:

**Target content and terms:**
- "how to monitor company career pages automatically" (no real competition; this is SM's core feature)
- "CIO job search strategy 2026" (timely, specific, low competition)
- "what does a CIO prep brief look like" (product-led; sends to demo page)
- "executive job search daily routine" (high intent, moderate competition)
- "how to track recruiter follow-ups" (specific tool query)

**Content format:** Long-form (1,500-3,000 words), answers the query completely, references SM's approach as the practical implementation. Not promotional — educational and data-driven.

**Proprietary content advantage:**
The only content no one else can publish: "We scanned X career pages across [sector] this quarter and here's what we found about hiring patterns." This is genuine market intelligence that no career blogger can replicate because they don't have the data. One article in this format, SEO-optimized and widely shared on LinkedIn, creates an authoritative backlink and a data advantage that compounds.

**Cadence:** 1 article/week starting Month 3. First 3 articles: data-driven (use scan results). Next 3: how-to content (search strategy). Next 3: tool comparison (honest assessment of SM vs. Teal vs. LinkedIn Premium).

### Channel 8 — Referral Program
**Priority: 2 (activate at 20 paying users)**

Job seekers know other job seekers. When someone lands, the first thing they do is tell their peers who are still searching what helped them. This is the highest-quality lead in B2C because the trust is pre-transferred.

**Mechanics:** Placed user shares their unique link. Referral gets first month free. Referring user gets a free month when referral converts to paid. No points. No tiers. Simple.

**When to promote:** After every milestone in the product — after first interview, after offer, after cancellation. "You just [landed an interview / accepted an offer]. Know someone still in search? Give them a free month and get one yourself."

**Target:** 20% of placed users generate one referral. At 10 placements/month (achievable by Month 12): 2 additional paying users/month from referrals.

## 3. Conversion Funnel Architecture

### Awareness → Trial

| Source | Behavior | Target Conversion |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| LinkedIn content | Click to /optimize or /demo | 2-4% of post viewers |
| /optimize tool | Email capture for report | 25% of visitors |
| /optimize email → trial | 12% of email list | |
| /demo page | Email capture midway through brief | 30% of visitors |
| /demo email → trial | 20% of email list | |
| Word of mouth / referral | Direct trial signup | 45% (high intent) |
| University career center ref. | Direct trial signup | 35% |
| LinkedIn ad → /optimize | 1-2% of impressions | |

### Trial → Paid

**Target: 40% trial-to-paid conversion**

The six activation actions — complete when a user has done all six in their first 7 days:
1. Set their role target and experience level
2. Added at least 3 target companies
3. Uploaded or pasted their resume
4. Generated one prep brief
5. Sent or scheduled a daily briefing
6. Generated or saved one outreach draft

Users who complete all six actions in the first 7 days convert at dramatically higher rates than those who don't. Everything in the onboarding experience should funnel toward these six actions.

**Activation email sequence:**
- Day 0: Welcome + direct link to onboarding wizard
- Day 3: "3 days in — did you add your first company?" (direct link to company add)
- Day 5: "Here's what your search looks like to a recruiter right now." (personalized brief preview)
- Day 7: "7 days left in your trial. Here's what three paying users did in their first week." (social proof)
- Day 10: "Your trial ends in 4 days — here's what stays if you upgrade." (specific, non-generic urgency)
- Day 14: "Trial ends today. Keep your search going." (single CTA, no friction)

**Conversion killers to eliminate:**
- User never completes onboarding (blank dashboard = no value = churn)
- User hits a bug or confusing state in the first session (one bad experience = gone)
- User doesn't receive a daily briefing within 24 hours of completing setup
- Daily briefing arrives with no scan matches and feels generic

### Paid → Retained

**Target: 70% 60-day retention; 55% 90-day retention**

Primary retention driver: daily briefing open rate ≥60%. If they're opening it, they're getting value. If it drops below 50%, something is wrong with the briefing quality or cadence.

Secondary retention drivers:
- Momentum Score drop → same-day nudge email with 3 specific actions
- Weekly progress report (Sunday) — makes search velocity visible even in slow weeks
- In-app smart prompt at Week 3 (the wall period): "Most searches feel slow right now. Here's what the data says works in weeks 4-8."
- Scan match email → direct CTA to draft outreach → pipeline action → reinforces product value loop

**Unavoidable churn:** When users land, they cancel. Design for graceful churn:
- On cancel: offer 3-month pause at half price ("pause your search, not your readiness")
- 6 months post-cancel: reactivation email ("Ready to check the market?")
- 12 months post-cancel: annual reactivation trigger

## 4. Phase Timeline

### Phase 1: Prove Conversion (Months 1-3, now through July 2026)

Goal: Validate trial-to-paid ≥35% before any paid acquisition.

Actions:
- Measure every trial user individually. Interview every non-converter personally (10-minute call, not survey).
- Optimize /optimize content and UX based on email capture data
- Write and launch activation email drip sequence
- Build onboarding wizard — eliminate the blank dashboard conversion killer
- Begin Founder LinkedIn content (3x/week, every week)
- Conduct Wake Forest/Stanford Zoom; follow up with 5 trial users
- Begin LinkedIn Sales Navigator B2B outreach to career coaches (parallel track)

Success criteria:
- 10 paying users by June 1
- Trial-to-paid conversion measured at ≥35%
- Daily briefing open rate ≥50%
- At least 3 users with documented positive outcomes

### Phase 2: Build the Channel (Months 4-9, Aug-Nov 2026)

Goal: Multiple acquisition channels generating 15-20 new users/month.

Actions:
- Executive tier live ($249, sprint D-E) — lifts ARPU, signals executive positioning
- Demo page live — converts the "wow" moment before signup
- LinkedIn content gaining traction; LinkedIn Optimizer SEO beginning to compound
- Referral program live (at 20 users)
- LinkedIn ads testing (if trial-to-paid ≥35% confirmed)
- First 3 SEO articles published
- B2B track running parallel (coaches, VC/PE outreach)

Success criteria:
- 30 paying users by October 2026
- $4,000-$5,000 MRR
- At least one acquisition channel producing ≥5 users/month consistently

### Phase 3: Scale the Engine (Months 10-24, Dec 2026 through May 2028)

Goal: Reach $25K MRR. Manager Tools partnership earned.

Actions:
- LinkedIn ads scaled to $2,000-3,000/month
- SEO compounding (10-15 users/month from organic by Month 18)
- Referral program generating 3-5 users/month
- University career network delivering 3-5 institutional trials
- Manager Tools approach in Month 15 (if qualification criteria met)
- Coach tier live — B2B revenue beginning to meaningfully offset B2C churn
- B2B revenue beginning to exceed B2C as the scaling mechanism

Success criteria:
- 100 paying users by May 2027
- $25K MRR by January 2028 (Month 20)
- NPS ≥50
- Two acquisition channels generating ≥10 users/month each

## 5. Positioning and Messaging

### Brand positioning
*"We get you to Starting Monday."* — The outcome, not the features. The specific Monday when you start the new role.

### Primary headline (landing page)
"The only tool that watches your target companies and tells you when to move — before the search goes public."

### Competitive counter-positioning

| Competitor | Their pitch | SM counter |
|------------|-------------|------------|
| LinkedIn Premium | "More reach" | "LinkedIn tells you what jobs exist. Starting Monday tells you how to get them before they're posted." |
| ChatGPT | "Anything you need" | "ChatGPT forgets everything the moment you close the window. Starting Monday knows your full search — every company, every contact, every follow-up — from day one through offer." |
| Human coach | "Expert guidance" | "Your career coach is available 9-5. We're available at 11pm when you're drafting the follow-up that could change everything." |
| Jack & Jill AI | "Free" | "They wait for an employer to want you. Starting Monday gets you there first. And your search stays yours — no employer network sees your profile until you reach out." |
| Teal / Huntr | "Job tracker" | "Teal tracks what you've applied to. Starting Monday monitors the companies before roles exist and tells you exactly when to move." |

### What to never say
- "AI-powered" as a standalone differentiator (table stakes by 2026)
- "Leverage your network" — the exact phrase the product replaces
- "Streamline your job search" — generic, meaningless
- Any outcome promise ("land faster," "get the job") — sets up liability and kills trust when some users don't

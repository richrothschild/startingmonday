## Important notice
This document is an operational readiness summary and not legal advice. Contract language and legal positions must be approved by authorized counsel.

---

## 1) Purpose and intended use

This summary is designed to shorten first-pass legal review for retained-search buyers evaluating Starting Monday.

Use this packet to:
1. Confirm confidentiality and data-control boundaries.
2. Align on pilot legal structure.
3. Reduce repetitive back-and-forth on standard diligence questions.

---

## 2) Product scope in search-firm context

Starting Monday supports candidate readiness and search workflow execution in retained-search contexts.

Core workflow surfaces:
1. Candidate prep briefs and role context summaries.
2. Pipeline and activity tracking for active transitions.
3. Controlled visibility for authorized partner-side stakeholders.

Not in scope:
1. Autonomous hiring decisions.
2. Employer-side candidate ranking automation used as sole decision basis.
3. Unbounded data sharing across unrelated mandates.

---

## 3) Roles and data responsibility model

Primary roles:
1. Candidate user
2. Search-firm partner or principal
3. Candidate-success owner or operator
4. Starting Monday support and operations users

Control principles:
1. Candidate-controlled sharing is the default for partner visibility.
2. Role-scoped access is enforced to prevent cross-mandate overexposure.
3. Access revocation must be available and auditable.

---

## 4) Data categories and handling boundaries

Typical data categories in pilot:
1. Professional profile and role-history context provided by candidate.
2. Search workflow metadata (actions, milestones, readiness notes).
3. Candidate-generated narrative and prep artifacts.
4. Operational telemetry for product quality and support.

Sensitive handling assumptions:
1. Personal contact details are minimized to required workflow use.
2. Confidential candidate-transition context is treated as restricted business information.
3. Data used for model assistance follows approved usage boundaries and logging controls.

---

## 5) Access controls and confidentiality guardrails

Minimum control expectations for pilot:
1. Authentication required for all role access.
2. Authorization rules scoped to tenant, role, and shared-resource context.
3. Audit records retained for high-risk access and permission changes.
4. Immediate revocation path for candidate-shared views.

Confidentiality safeguards:
1. Need-to-know visibility by mandate context.
2. No broad partner-side visibility unless explicitly granted.
3. Controlled export and share patterns for external artifacts.

---

## 6) Security posture summary (buyer-facing)

Security control areas to provide in diligence responses:
1. Encryption in transit and at rest.
2. Access management and least-privilege controls.
3. Logging and incident response process.
4. Backup and recovery approach.
5. Vulnerability management and patch hygiene.

Operational evidence to include in packet attachment set:
1. Security overview and architecture summary.
2. Incident response contact and notification model.
3. Data retention and deletion policy summary.

---

## 7) AI assistance boundaries

AI features in this context are decision-support tools and not decision-makers.

Required disclosure points:
1. Outputs are assistive and require human review.
2. Generated recommendations are not sole hiring decisions.
3. Confidence and source context should be visible where applicable.

Governance requirements:
1. Claim language on pages must match contractual and policy language.
2. High-risk claims require validation tier labels.
3. Public statements separate directional pilot findings from validated outcomes.

---

## 8) Data retention and deletion posture

Pilot-ready policy expectations:
1. Defined retention periods by data type.
2. Deletion workflow for terminated pilot or revoked access.
3. Export and handoff path for customer-owned artifacts.

Deletion operations should include:
1. Request intake and verification.
2. Processing target SLA.
3. Completion confirmation mechanism.

## 11) Common legal objections and response pattern

1. Objection: Candidate confidentiality risk across mandates.
- Response pattern: role-scoped access, candidate-controlled sharing, auditable revocation.

2. Objection: AI outputs used as hiring decisions.
- Response pattern: explicit decision-support positioning and human-review requirement.

3. Objection: Undefined retention and deletion controls.
- Response pattern: provide retention schedule summary and deletion workflow SLA.

4. Objection: Pilot terms are too broad.
- Response pattern: use narrow pilot order form with explicit scope, roles, and termination terms.

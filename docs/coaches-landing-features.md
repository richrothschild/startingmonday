# Coaches Landing Page Features

Refined through three council passes:
- Practitioner lens: coach-native workflow and low-friction use.
- Practice-owner lens: pricing, ROI, and scaling clarity.
- Trust lens: permission control, privacy, and no recruiter-side sharing.

## Core Features

- Coach-first hero
- Keep the first line outcome-led: coaches stay in strategy while clients arrive prepared.
- State the trust boundary immediately: client-controlled access and private-by-default workflows.
- Use one primary CTA and one secondary route.

- Outcome proof block
- Lead with practical proof that coaching improves, not generic product claims.
- Show pilot signal and the business outcome in the same view.
- Keep the proof short enough to read in one scan.

- At-a-glance operating rhythm
- Show the weekly cadence in coach language.
- Make the signal-to-action-to-prep loop visible.
- Keep the timing clear for solo coaches and firms.

- Persona routing
- Split the journey for independent coaches, boutique firms, and enterprise-sponsored cohorts.
- Send each persona to the most relevant next step.
- Clarify the buying model and support model at the same time.

- Pilot package
- Define the 30-day preview with a small live-client test.
- Make pass/fail criteria visible before signup.
- Keep the risk reversal obvious.

- Pricing clarity
- Show the coach buyer plans on the landing page.
- Keep the referral lane visible as a secondary option.
- Explain pricing in plain coaching economics.

- Trust and privacy
- Surface the permission model near the top of the page.
- Link to the trust pack directly from the landing page.
- Make the no recruiter-side-sharing statement explicit.

- Supporting resources
- Keep the worksheet, FAQ, economics, and micro-products discoverable.
- Treat those pages as support surfaces, not the primary story.
- Use them to deepen confidence after the landing page earns attention.

## 1. Who they are

### Delta-T Executive Career Management

- Fully remote LLC, team across the US, clients worldwide
- Squarespace website, Moxo for client portal login
- Positioning: high-touch, high-end, senior-level exclusivity, human inclusivity
- Client login at delta-t.moxo.com (Moxo = client collaboration SaaS, not proprietary tech)
- LinkedIn company page; active on TikTok
- Founded relatively recently (© 2026 but LinkedIn and site suggest ~2023-2025 vintage)
- Revenue model: one-time sliding-scale fee, no recurring dues, "sliding scale based on individual requirements" (fees undisclosed on call, deferred to a director)
- Sales process: SDR pre-qualifies, then books a 45-min "complimentary private video chat with a director"
- Core sales pitch: private executive network of thousands globally, "rarely advertised opportunities," dedicated director, one-on-one model with limited clients per consultant
- Deliverables: strategic positioning plan, professional narrative, network introductions, high-stakes interview prep, negotiation support
- Technology: Moxo client portal only - no proprietary AI, no signal monitoring, no pipeline tracking

### Exec Upgrades (Greg/Tim Madden company)

- Founder Tim Madden, former US Army Captain, Inc 500 two years running, A+ BBB rating, veteran-owned
- Featured in: Fast Company, Newsweek, Entrepreneur, Business Insider
- Flat fee: $15,000 (payment plans available)
- Guarantee: work until you land; if unhappy within 2 years, redo at no cost
- Average time to placement: 45-90 days (vs. 9-18 months DIY)
- 417-600+ high-level offers placed; 2,476+ six-figure interviews facilitated
- Sales process: Greg (SDR/closer) runs initial call, full pitch via deck, closes same call
- Three-phase model:
  1. Brand foundation (resume, LinkedIn, executive story rebuild) - 2 weeks
  2. Pipeline building (3 methods):
     - Automated LinkedIn outreach via API (12,000 messages/month, targets hiring leaders before roles post)
     - Internal alumni network (~9,500 people, ~1/3 of placements come from here)
     - Human-executed job applications (80-100/day, 2,000/month, done by their team, 162 apps per interview average)
  3. Interview coaching and offer negotiation (mock interviews, email negotiation, avg 27.2% pay increase)
- Communication: Slack/Huddle/Zoom, dedicated project manager, daily application report sent to client
- Technology: specialized LinkedIn API software for outreach, human teams for execution, no AI product for clients

## 4. Positioning comparison

### Delta-T positioning
"Executive Career Management for Changing Times" - high-touch, relationship-driven, exclusivity brand, human-first. Sells hope and access. Defers pricing until a "director" call. Target buyer: senior executive who wants someone else to manage their search.

### Exec Upgrades positioning
Execution-heavy, high volume, data-driven ("162 applications for one interview," "12,000 outreach messages"), results-centric (27.2% pay raise, 45-90 day placement). Military discipline metaphor. Closes on the same call. Target buyer: executive who is frustrated with DIY and wants a managed service.

### Starting Monday positioning
"The operating system for your next career move" - daily execution system, signal intelligence, coach-facing tools, between-session accountability. Target buyer: the executive who wants to stay in control but wants a smarter system, AND the coach who wants to deliver better outcomes at scale.

## 5. Gap analysis: what Starting Monday is missing to compete head-on

### Gap 1: No human services layer
Both competitors sell advisor time. Starting Monday sells software. Executives at the $300K+ level often want someone to DO things for them, not a tool they run. This is a real market gap. The question is whether Starting Monday fills it directly or partners to fill it.

**Closing strategy options:**
- Do not try to be a services business. Instead, make Starting Monday the system that makes coaches and advisors better so they can serve more clients at higher quality.
- Alternatively: offer a "concierge" tier that pairs the platform with a dedicated advisor through a coaching partner network (white-label-compatible).

### Gap 2: No done-for-you application execution
Exec Upgrades' most defensible moat is 80-100 human-executed applications per day. This is labor-intensive and hard to match with software alone. Starting Monday does not do this.

**Closing strategy:** Starting Monday's signal layer could reduce the need for volume applications by improving precision and timing. Executives who get to companies before roles post need fewer applications. This is a genuine competitive positioning claim.

### Gap 3: No executive network
Delta-T sells access to its proprietary network of thousands of executives. This is hard to replicate without time. Starting Monday has no equivalent.

**Closing strategy:** Position network differently. Starting Monday surfaces market signals that others miss. That is a different kind of network edge - not relationship-based, but intelligence-based.

### Gap 4: No outbound outreach execution for clients
Exec Upgrades sends 12,000 LinkedIn messages per month on behalf of clients using API tools. Starting Monday does not do outreach on behalf of clients.

**Closing strategy:** Starting Monday could help clients and coaches build more targeted, timed outreach by surfacing the right signal at the right moment. The client executes; Starting Monday makes the targeting sharper.

### Gap 5: Resume and LinkedIn rebuild services
Neither competitor relies solely on AI for this. Both have human writers who interview the client and rebuild the materials.

**Closing strategy:** Starting Monday's positioning module and narrative tools partially address this. A partnership with a coaching firm or resume service provider could complete it without Starting Monday building a services arm.

## 6. Starting Monday's genuine advantages over both competitors

**Advantage 1: Intelligence over effort**
Both competitors compete on volume and human effort. Starting Monday competes on timing and precision. Getting a client into a conversation before a role is posted is more valuable than 162 applications per interview.

**Advantage 2: Between-session continuity**
Both competitors do point-in-time interventions (resume, interview prep). Starting Monday sustains momentum continuously. This is the gap that causes executive searches to fail - drift between sessions, not poor interview performance.

**Advantage 3: Coach-native tools**
Neither Delta-T nor Exec Upgrades sells to coaches. They sell directly to executives. Starting Monday sells to coaches, who sell to executives. This is a fundamentally different distribution model that these competitors are not designed to attack.

**Advantage 4: Scalability and recurring economics**
$15,000 one-time (Exec Upgrades) or sliding-scale one-time (Delta-T) are high-stakes, hard to sell. Starting Monday at $299-$1,500/month recurring builds a more predictable, lower-friction business with lower client acquisition cost.

**Advantage 5: White-label / OEM**
Neither competitor has a white-label model. A boutique coaching firm that wants to offer technology to its clients cannot use Delta-T or Exec Upgrades - those firms compete with coaches. Starting Monday can partner with them.

## 7. Should we approach Delta-T with a white-label offer?

**Assessment: Yes, but only after understanding their model more deeply.**

The case for:
- Delta-T uses Moxo (off-the-shelf client portal), not proprietary technology. They are buying tools, not building them. They are technology-receptive.
- Their model is high-touch and human-driven. Starting Monday would add signal intelligence and daily client tracking without threatening the advisor relationship.
- They explicitly position clients away from job boards and toward the hidden market. Starting Monday's signal layer directly supports that positioning. It would strengthen their pitch, not contradict it.
- They have scale ambition (global, industry-agnostic). Maintaining quality at scale is hard with a human-only model. Starting Monday solves that.

The case against:
- Delta-T's value proposition is partially the mystery. The "sliding scale" pricing and "director consultation" model are high-friction by design because high friction = high perceived value. Adding transparent software might undercut that positioning.
- If they use Starting Monday behind the scenes and don't white-label it, they get the benefit and you get nothing. You would need a clear contractual model.
- They are new enough that they may not have the client volume to warrant a platform contract yet.

**Recommended approach:**
Do not pitch white-label to Delta-T cold. First, run the same call that Rich ran as a prospect - gather more intelligence on pricing, client base size, advisor capacity, and technology stack. Then position Starting Monday as an enabler that makes their advisors more effective, not as a product they would surface to clients. The white-label angle may come later once you have established a relationship.

2. VISUAL STYLE OF THE SHIPPED SKETCHES (observed)
--------------------------------------------------
- Medium: loose fashion-editorial illustration — confident ink/marker linework over watercolor washes.
- Palette: black/charcoal figure with warm champagne, gold, and stone washes; generous white negative space; soft bokeh light dots.
- Subject: a single poised executive figure, tailored clothing, calm confident expression; environment only gestured (desk edges, skyline strokes), never rendered fully.
- Composition: one focal figure, asymmetric balance, background dissolves into sketch marks and washes at the edges.
- Register: luxury magazine editorial (think fashion-house campaign sketch), NOT corporate stock, NOT SaaS illustration, NOT cartoon.

3. CANONICAL STANDARDS THAT GOVERN THE IMAGERY
--------------------------------------------------
A. docs/landing-page-standard.md (sitewide source of truth)
- "Brand integrity: every route must look intentionally part of one product."
- Visual regression gating with screenshot diffs at route-level breakpoints.

B. docs/strategy/archetypes/luxury-brand-archetype.md (luxury visual rules)
- Restraint over density; one focal visual at a time.
- Editorial image framing, never edge-to-edge clutter.
- Strong negative space around hero and media.
- Accent: muted gold/champagne only for emphasis; avoid neon or saturated marketing hues.
- Pass threshold: 23/25 on visual restraint, editorial composition, material quality feel, color discipline, premium coherence.

C. docs/first-visit-experience-review-2026-07-04.md, Section 5 (art direction spec)
- Small image system: 4-6 images maximum sitewide.
- "Dark, architectural, human-but-anonymous... No stock-photo executives shaking hands."

- One image per scroll band, not galleries; each image earns a full- or half-bleed moment.

D. docs/first-mile-wbs-2026-07-04.md, Sprint 6 "Visual System and Proof"

4. RECONSTRUCTED PROMPT TEMPLATE (not the original — rebuilt from standards + shipped style)
--------------------------------------------------
Use as a starting point to produce new images consistent with the set:

"Luxury fashion-editorial illustration of [SUBJECT: e.g., a poised executive woman standing at a boardroom table / an executive reviewing documents in a refined home office]. Loose confident ink and marker sketch linework over soft watercolor washes. Palette: black and charcoal figure, warm champagne and gold washes, stone neutrals, generous white negative space, subtle golden bokeh light. Environment only gestured with sketch strokes — desk edges, window light, skyline hints — never fully rendered. Single focal figure, asymmetric editorial composition, calm and self-assured expression, tailored clothing. Style register: high-fashion campaign sketch for a premium magazine. Not corporate stock photography, not flat SaaS vector illustration, not cartoon. Wide aspect ratio for hero placement; must sit gracefully next to a very dark (slate-950) page canvas."

Per-subject variants already in the set:
- Leadership presence at a table (homepage hero)
- Woman deciding between two doors (choice/threshold metaphor, /for-individuals)
- Young leader at a laptop (/for-leaders)
- Executive at desk with documents, refined home office (/for-executives)

--------------------------------------------------
5. ACCEPTANCE CHECKLIST FOR ANY NEW SKETCH
--------------------------------------------------
1. Reads as editorial illustration, not stock or SaaS art.
2. One focal human figure, anonymous enough to be aspirational.
3. Champagne/gold/stone washes only; no saturated marketing colors.
4. Generous negative space; background dissolves to sketch marks.
5. Composition works inside a rounded, bordered figure card on a slate-950 page.
6. Scores 23/25+ on the luxury archetype rubric (restraint, composition, material feel, color discipline, coherence).
7. Sitewide image count stays within the 4-6 image budget.

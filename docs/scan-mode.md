## How to Trigger a Scan

Say: **"Scan this JD"** and paste the job description text.

1. Match score (0–100)
2. Recommended resume variant
3. Keywords present in your resume
4. Keywords missing from your resume
5. Specific edits to make before applying
6. Job scorecard score (0–10)
7. Apply / Skip recommendation

## Exclusion Filters

Auto-skip any role that meets one or more of these criteria. Do not score — just flag and move on.

| Filter | Reject Signal |
|---|---|
| Scope language | "support and maintain," "optimize existing," "keep the lights on" — no transformation mandate |
| Budget ownership | No P&L, no budget authority stated, or budget <$5M |
| IC-heavy | Role requires hands-on coding, system administration, or individual contributor delivery |
| Company size | <100 employees — org too small to require a CIO |
| Comp ceiling | Base ceiling stated below $300K for CIO/VP level |
| Deep coding requirement | "Strong Python/Java/Go required," "hands-on engineering expected" |
| Wrong geography | Requires full-time on-site outside CA, NY, or Pacific/Mountain time zones |
| Staffing firm | Posted by Robert Half, Randstad, TEKsystems, Kforce, Apex — hourly rate or contract-to-hire |
| Industry | Health, semiconductor, or financial services — auto-skip unless explicitly overridden |

## Up-Rank Signals

These are green flags that increase probability of interview and offer. A role with 3+ signals should move to the top of the apply queue regardless of posting recency.

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| JD language: "execution recovery," "platform modernization," "delivery discipline," "operating model," "M&A integration," "managed services" | These are your exact problem types — few candidates have all of them |
| Company is under-the-radar with real operational complexity | Less competition from trophy-hunters; your proof points stand out more |
| Role reports to CEO, COO, CFO, or board-facing executive | Access and scope signal; also means the hire matters to the business |
| JD requires credibility across engineering, IT, finance, security, and operations simultaneously | Filters out pure technologists and pure administrators — narrows the field to your profile |
| Company has scaled fast (Series C/D, post-acquisition) and visibly needs systems maturity | Growth-stage chaos is your specialty — and they know they have the problem |
| FedRAMP, SOC 2, NIST, or regulated-adjacent requirements without being in health/finance | Compliance credential is rare at the CIO level in SaaS companies |
| Warm path available: mutual connection, recruiter relationship, or investor overlap | Every step removed from cold application increases offer probability materially |

## Differentiation Narratives

Use these when drafting cover letters, outreach messages, or interview talking points. Match to the role based on what the JD is actually asking for.

---

### A. Infra Operator
**Use when:** JD emphasizes reliability, uptime, scale, infrastructure modernization, or operational complexity.

> "I've run large-scale infrastructure with SLA accountability — $2B global data center strategy at Cisco, platforms processing $200B+ in annual transaction volume at Apple/Ariba, 25M-user streaming integration at Netflix-TiVo. I'm not a strategy CIO. I'm the one who signs the SLA and delivers it."

---

### B. The Fixer
**Use when:** JD uses language like "transformation," "execution recovery," "bring order," "scale the org," or is in a PE-backed turnaround context.

> "I'm brought in when execution breaks. At Cisco WebEx I inherited a $164M engineering org releasing every six months. In 18 months we were releasing every three weeks with zero downtime. KPI attainment went from 62% to 93%. That's the kind of problem I solve."

---

### C. Execution Architect
**Use when:** JD is VP Engineering / Head of TPM / VP Engineering Operations — engineering delivery, program governance, cross-team alignment.

> "I design the systems that make engineering organizations deliver predictably. Roadmap governance, dependency management, release cadence, executive visibility — I build the operating model that lets engineering move fast without losing control. That's what I did at Pandora when we went from 3 to 125 SaaS platforms."

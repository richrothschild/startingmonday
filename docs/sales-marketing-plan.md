# Sales & Marketing Plan — Starting Monday
### Version 1.0 — May 2026

---

## Overview

This document covers acquisition strategy, channel prioritization, messaging architecture, conversion funnel, and retention marketing for Starting Monday. It is a working plan — updated as data replaces assumption.

**Current state:** Alpha cohort live. First paying users in trial. No paid acquisition running. All current growth is founder-driven, direct relationship.

**MRR target:** $25,000 (167 users at ~$150/month blended) within 18–24 months.

**Primary constraint:** Solo founder. Acquisition must be leveraged (one-to-many), not linear (one-to-one). Channels that require constant personal time to generate leads do not scale.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile

### Primary ICP: The Active Executive

- **Title:** CIO, CTO, CHRO, VP of IT, VP of Engineering, VP of Technology, Head of IT
- **Experience:** 15–25 years
- **Compensation:** $200K–$500K
- **Situation:** In transition — laid off, packaged out, or quietly looking while employed
- **Geography:** US, major metro (Bay Area, NYC, Chicago, Austin, Boston, Seattle, Atlanta)
- **Urgency:** High. Active search. Severance runway is finite.
- **Pain:** Missing follow-ups. Inconsistent outreach quality. Can't monitor 20 career pages manually. Has paid $5K–$25K for a human coach with inconsistent ROI.
- **WTP:** $129–$249/month. Easy yes if it saves 5 hours/week.
- **Average duration:** 4–6 months

### Secondary ICP: The VP-Level Candidate

- **Title:** Director or Senior Director targeting VP for the first time
- **Experience:** 12–18 years
- **Situation:** Employed, wants to make the jump, never run a VP-level search
- **Pain:** Doesn't know how to work the hidden job market. Has never negotiated at this level. Imposter syndrome about the title jump.
- **WTP:** $99–$129/month
- **Average duration:** 6–12 months

### Tertiary ICP: The Passive Monitor

- **Situation:** Employed, not actively looking, watching for the right opportunity
- **WTP:** $49/month ("alert me if anything changes at my target companies")
- **Value:** Structural MRR stabilizer — low churn, long duration, converts to Active when search begins

### Not the ICP (for launch)

- Entry-level or early-career professionals — underserved by design; wrong tier
- Users looking for auto-apply volume tools — wrong mental model
- EU/international users — US-only at launch
- Career coaches as primary customers — planned for Sprint 8, not the launch acquisition channel

## 2. Messaging Architecture

### Brand positioning

> "We get you to Starting Monday."

The outcome, not the features. The promise is the job — the specific Monday when you start the new role. Every piece of marketing copy anchors to that outcome.

### Primary value propositions by ICP

**For active executives:**
- "The only tool that watches your target companies and tells you when to move — before the search goes public."
- "Everything a $10,000 career coach gives you, plus the monitoring and intelligence they can't. $129/month."
- "Your search stays yours. No employer network. No matching marketplace. No one sees your profile until you want them to."

**For VP candidates making the level jump:**
- "Built for the VP search you've never run before. Knows the market. Knows your positioning. Available at 11pm when you're drafting that message."

**For passive monitors:**
- "$49/month to know the moment something changes at the companies you're watching. Cancel when you stop watching."

### Competitive counter-messaging

**Against LinkedIn Premium:** "LinkedIn waits for you to apply. Starting Monday watches your companies and tells you when to move."

**Against Jack and Jill AI (free):** "They wait for an employer to want you. Starting Monday gets you there first." And: "JaJ sees your profile. Your search on Starting Monday stays yours."

**Against ChatGPT:** "ChatGPT forgets everything the moment you close the window. Starting Monday knows your full search — every company, every contact, every follow-up — from day one through offer."

**Against human coaches:** "Your career coach is available 9–5 on weekdays. We're available at 11pm when you're drafting the follow-up that could change everything."

### Anti-patterns in messaging

Never use:
- "AI-powered" as a standalone differentiator (table stakes)
- "Leverage your network" (the exact phrase the product exists to replace)
- "Streamline your job search" (generic, meaningless)
- Generic outcome claims ("land your dream job faster") — these are promises the product cannot guarantee and that damage trust

Lead with:
- Specific, concrete behaviors the product performs ("watches your 20 target companies")
- Time savings backed by specificity ("in the time between your 7am coffee and your 9am call")
- The emotional reality of the search ("you have one shot at the right timing — we make sure you don't miss it")

## 3. Acquisition Channels

Ranked by priority. Priority = (strategic leverage × timing) / cost and effort.

---

### Channel 1: LinkedIn Optimizer — Zero-Friction Top-of-Funnel (Live)

**What it is:** Free public tool at startingmonday.app/optimize. No login required. Users paste their LinkedIn headline and About section; get specific, ranked recommendations. No email gate to use it; email gate to receive the full PDF report.

**Why it's first:** People update their LinkedIn profile at the exact moment they begin a job search. The /optimize page captures users at the trigger moment — before they've made any tool decisions.

**Conversion path:** /optimize → email capture for report → trial signup CTA in follow-up email → 30-day trial → paying customer.

**Effort:** Live. Ongoing maintenance: update recommendations quarterly based on what's actually working in the market.

**Target metric:** 200 unique monthly visitors to /optimize → 20% email capture → 5% trial conversion = 2 paying customers/month from this channel.

---

### Channel 2: Founder LinkedIn Content (Starting Now)

**What it is:** Regular founder posts on LinkedIn about executive job search intelligence. Not promotional copy — actual intelligence that demonstrates the product's value.

**Content types:**
- Data from the platform: "We scanned 200 career pages this week. Here's what CIO hiring looks like in Q2 2026."
- Search coaching: "The one email that got a response from a CIO recruiter in 2025. Here's the structure."
- Company intelligence: "Three companies that just hired a new CEO and what that means for IT leadership changes."
- Personal experience: Founder's own search — what worked, what didn't, why he built the product.

**Why it works:** LinkedIn reach is highest among executives. Founder content with authentic insight outperforms branded content 10:1 for engagement. One good post can reach 10,000–50,000 people organically in this audience.

**Conversion path:** Post → profile visit → startingmonday.app → LinkedIn Optimizer or trial signup.

**Cadence:** 3 posts/week. Two shorter (observations, data points), one longer (analysis or how-to).

**Effort:** 2–3 hours/week. Non-negotiable — this is the primary organic channel at current scale.

**Target metric:** 1,000 LinkedIn followers by Month 3 → 5,000 by Month 6 → 2 paying customers/month from LinkedIn content at steady state.

### Channel 3: Wake Forest / Stanford Career Network (May 2026)

**What it is:** Direct conversation with career center leadership at Wake Forest who is connected to Stanford Alumni career network and the broader career coaching network.

**Opportunity:** Career centers at top MBA and undergraduate programs place thousands of executives annually. A referral relationship with one well-connected career center contact can drive dozens of users without any marketing spend.

**Approach for the May 18 meeting:**
- Lead with the product demo — show the daily briefing, company monitoring, and prep brief
- Position SM as a tool career centers can recommend to alumni — not compete with
- Explore whether career centers would provide SM as a resource to alumni in active search
- Leave behind: free trial codes for 10 alumni they personally place into the product; collect feedback

**Conversion path:** Career center endorsement → alumni referral → trial → paying customer.

**Effort:** One Zoom meeting + follow-up. High-leverage if it converts to a referral relationship.

**Target metric:** 5–10 trial users from this channel. 2–3 paying users. Introduction to one additional career network contact.

### Channel 4: Manager Tools Partnership (Q3 2026)

**What it is:** Mark Horstman at Manager Tools has one of the largest and most loyal audiences of professional managers and executives in the world. A Manager Tools endorsement is uniquely valuable because their audience is the exact target customer — high-trust, decision-maker-level professionals who take product recommendations from Mark seriously.

**Requirements before approaching:**
- Product must be polished enough that Mark uses it himself and finds it defensible
- AI output quality must be genuinely excellent — Mark will test the prep briefs and outreach drafts; if they sound generic, the opportunity is gone
- At least 20–30 satisfied paying users with documented outcomes

**Approach:** Personal introduction through the career search context. Not a pitch — a product demo. Let the product make the case.

**Conversion path:** Manager Tools podcast mention or newsletter → startingmonday.app → trial → paying customer.

**Target metric:** One Manager Tools mention could drive 500–2,000 trial signups. At 30% trial-to-paid, that is 150–600 paying customers.

### Channel 5: SEO — Long-Tail Search (Months 3–12)

**What it is:** Organic search traffic for specific, answerable queries that Starting Monday's content can own.

**Target terms (not competitive broad terms):**
- "how to monitor company career pages automatically"
- "CIO job search strategy 2026"
- "executive job search daily routine"
- "how to track recruiter follow-ups"
- "AI tool for executive job search"
- "what to do after getting laid off CIO"
- "how to outreach to a VP without a connection"

**Content format:** Long-form guides (1,500–3,000 words) that answer the question completely and reference Starting Monday's approach as the practical implementation. Not promotional — educational.

**Effort:** 1 high-quality article per week. Takes 3–6 months to rank. Not a near-term channel.

**Target metric:** 500 organic monthly visitors by Month 6 → 2,000 by Month 12 → 5 paying customers/month at steady state.

### Channel 6: Referral Program (Live at 20 Paying Users)

**What it is:** Placed users receive one free month for each referral who becomes a paying customer. Referral link tracked through Stripe/custom attribution.

**Why job search referrals work:** Job seekers know other job seekers. When someone lands, the first thing they do is tell their peers who are still searching what helped them.

**Timing:** Launch when there are 20+ paying users — enough to generate meaningful referral volume.

**Conversion path:** Placed user shares link → referral signs up for trial → converts → placed user gets free month.

**Target metric:** 20% of placed users generate one referral. At 10 placements/month, that is 2 additional customers/month from referrals.

### Channel 7: Career Coach Partnerships (Coach Tier — Sprint 8)

**What it is:** Independent career coaches (1–10 clients) subscribe to the Coach tier ($599/month) and use SM as a delivery platform for their clients.

**Why coaches:** A coach who uses and endorses the platform is the highest-value referral channel. One coach with 5 clients = $599/month recurring + potential direct referrals from clients who want to continue after coaching ends.

**Approach:**
- Find 2–3 early-adopter coaches willing to pilot
- Build features with their feedback
- Use them as case studies for other coaches
- Position as: "Starting Monday makes you 2x more productive with each client — which means you can take on twice as many clients."

**Not competing with coaches — enabling them:** The Coach tier is designed so coaches use SM as infrastructure, not as a replacement for their human judgment.

**Target metric:** 5 Coach tier subscribers by Month 18. Each represents $599/month and access to their client network.

---

### Channel 8: LinkedIn Advertising (Month 6+, Budget-Dependent)

**What it is:** Targeted LinkedIn ads to "open to work" technology executives in major US metros.

**Why LinkedIn ads are not first:** CAC through LinkedIn ads is $50–$200 per paid trial. At $129/month and 5-month average duration ($645 LTV), the math works — but only if trial-to-paid conversion is above 30%. Running ads before conversion rate is proven is burning money.

**When to activate:** After trial-to-paid conversion is measured at 30%+. Budget: $500–$2,000/month.

**Ad creative direction:**
- Lead with a specific, concrete capability — not "AI career tool"
- "We scanned 500 career pages this week. Here's what we found about CIO hiring." — drives curiosity
- "Start monitoring your target companies before the search goes public." — specific and timely
- Retargeting: users who visited /optimize but did not sign up

## 4. Conversion Funnel

### Awareness → Trial

| Touchpoint | Goal | Conversion target |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post | Click to startingmonday.app | 2–5% of views |
| /optimize tool | Email capture | 20% of visitors |
| /optimize follow-up email | Trial signup | 10% of email captures |
| Word of mouth / referral | Trial signup | 40% (intent is already high) |
| Direct LinkedIn DM from founder | Trial signup | 30% |

### Trial → Paid

Target: 40% trial-to-paid conversion.

**What drives conversion:**
- User completes the Six Actions (the activation framework) within the first 7 days
- User receives at least one scan match for a role they care about
- User generates one prep brief or outreach draft that they actually use
- User receives the daily briefing for 5+ consecutive days and opens it

**What kills conversion:**
- User never completes onboarding (no briefing_time set, no companies added)
- User never experiences a "wow" moment — no match, no useful brief, no signal
- User hits a bug or broken feature during the trial window

**Activation sequence (automated):**
- Day 0: Welcome email with setup checklist (the six actions)
- Day 3: "How's your search going?" — direct from founder email address. If they haven't added companies, link to add them now.
- Day 7: "You have 7 days left in your trial." — show what they've done, what they haven't, what they're missing.
- Day 10: Last chance — show one real example of what a paid user experienced this week (anonymized).
- Day 14 (trial end): "Keep your search going — choose your plan."

**Reducing conversion friction:**
- No credit card required during trial (already implemented)
- Cancel anytime, no questions asked (in all messaging, not buried in ToS)
- Offer monthly, not annual, as the default — actively searching users will not commit annually

### Paid → Retained

Target: 70% 60-day retention.

**Retention levers:**
- Daily briefing open rate >= 60% — the briefing is the primary retention driver. If they're opening it, they're getting value.
- Momentum Score drop triggers a nudge email within 24 hours
- Weekly progress report delivered Sunday — makes search velocity visible
- In-product smart prompts after each action ("You just added your first company — want to generate a prep brief?")
- Proactive scan failure notification — if we can't scan their company, tell them before they notice

**Unavoidable churn:** Active searchers who land will cancel. Design for graceful churn:
- On cancel: offer a 3-month pause at half price instead of full cancel
- 6 months after cancel: "Ready to check the market?" reactivation email
- Annual reactivation: triggered automatically on the 1-year anniversary of offer acceptance

## 5. Retention Marketing

### Daily briefing (primary retention driver)

The daily briefing is the highest-engagement touchpoint. Delivered by email at the user's configured time and timezone. Every briefing contains at least one of: new scan match, active signal, overdue follow-up, or suggested action. Nothing goes out that is purely generic.

**Open rate target:** 60%+. Industry average for transactional email is 20–30%. 60% is achievable because the briefing is expected, personalized, and action-oriented.

**Subject line approach:** Specific and situational, not generic.
- "3 actions for your Cisco search this week" (not "Your Daily Briefing")
- "New match at ServiceNow — CIO role, 85/100 relevance" (when a high-score match is present)
- "Your follow-up to Marcus Chen is 2 days overdue" (urgency, specific)

### Weekly progress report (Sunday)

Pipeline snapshot: companies tracked, stage progression, scan highlights, momentum score with week-over-week delta, 3 Claude-suggested actions for next week. Makes the search feel like it is moving forward even in slow weeks.

### Momentum nudge (triggered)

When momentum score drops 15+ points week-over-week, the system triggers a nudge email within 24 hours. Tone: direct, not alarming. "Your search has slowed down this week — here are 3 specific things to do today." The email references their specific pipeline, not generic advice.

### In-app smart prompts

Contextual CTAs surfaced at the right moments:
- After resume upload: "Add your first target company"
- After first company: "Generate a prep brief for [Company Name]"
- After first briefing: "Set your briefing time to get this every morning"
- At week 3 (wall period): "Most searches slow down around now — here's what the data says about what works in weeks 4–8"

## 6. B2B Track

### Career coaches (independent)

**Target:** Independent career coaches with 3–10 active clients, billing $150–$500/hour. These coaches run executive searches from a personal relationship model — they know their clients, they review every outreach draft, they prep every interview. SM makes them more productive without threatening their relationship.

**Pitch:** "Starting Monday handles the monitoring, the draft review, the briefing delivery, and the follow-up tracking. You handle the strategy and the relationship. Take on 2 more clients without burning out."

**Coach tier value:** $599/month for up to 10 client profiles. White-label briefings with coach branding. Aggregate pipeline view.

**Sales cycle:** Personal outreach to coaches who publish on LinkedIn about executive career coaching. Short cycle — coaches are small businesses, not procurement committees. Decision in days, not months.

### Outplacement firms (enterprise)

**Target:** Lee Hecht Harrison, RiseSmart, Careerminds, Challenger Gray, Right Management. These firms provide outplacement services (funded by the employer) when executives are laid off. The candidate cost is zero to the executive — the former employer pays.

**Pitch:** "Add Starting Monday to your outplacement package. Your clients get AI-powered monitoring and coaching for the duration of their search. You differentiate your offering without adding headcount."

**Sales cycle:** 6–18 months. Requires: legal review, IT security review, procurement approval, pilot negotiation. Do not count on this revenue in Year 1. Target Year 2.

**When to pursue:** After 50 paying users and a documented case study from a placed user. Before that, the product has not earned the meeting.

## 9. What Success Looks Like at Each Stage

### Alpha (current — 5–15 users)
- Every user has at least one "wow" moment documented
- At least 3 users would pay $129/month right now if trial ended
- At least 1 user lands an interview from a SM-surfaced opportunity

### Early traction (10–30 users, Month 2–6)
- Trial-to-paid conversion measured above 30%
- At least one user refers a second user unprompted
- Daily briefing open rate above 50%
- No critical bugs in core product loop (scanning, briefing, chat)

### Growth (30–100 users, Month 6–12)
- LinkedIn content generating inbound signups without founder direct outreach
- One partnership relationship (career center or coach) producing users
- MRR growing 15%+ month-over-month
- Net revenue retention above 90% (upgrades offset churn)

### Scale ($25K MRR)
- At least two acquisition channels generating measurable paying users
- Coach tier live with 5+ subscribers
- One outplacement pilot in negotiation
- Manager Tools relationship either active or evaluated and decision made

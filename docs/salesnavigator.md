# Sales Navigator Playbook — Starting Monday
**Version 1.0 — May 9, 2026**

---

## When to use it and when not to

Sales Navigator is a cold outreach tool. The warmth of a personal text or direct email outperforms any InMail by 10x on conversion. Use the five-source personal outreach method first for the initial 20 names. Open Sales Navigator for everything else.

**Use Sales Navigator for:**
- The second wave of executive outreach (after the warm 20 are messaged)
- Career coach outreach (B2B Sprint A — these are genuinely cold)
- VC/PE operating partner outreach (B2B Sprint A)
- University career center directors (B2B Sprint B)
- Retained search firm partners (referral channel)
- Rediscovering warm contacts you lost track of (past company searches)

**Do not use Sales Navigator for:**
- The first 20 personal outreach messages — warmth beats search sophistication every time
- Executives who are quietly searching but haven't updated their profile — they won't surface here

## Setup: do this first

**Account:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator Core ($99/month) is sufficient. Team version is not needed at this stage.

**Two things to configure before running any search:**

1. **Lead alerts:** Turn on alerts for "job change" and "in the news" for any saved lead. When someone in your list changes roles or makes news, you get notified. That is a trigger for outreach.

2. **Account alerts:** For the VC/PE channel, set account-level alerts on 20-30 portfolio companies you identify as targets. When a senior leadership change appears on their company page, you have a 48-hour outreach window.

## Search 1 — In-Transition Executives (Second Wave B2C)

**Goal:** Find senior technology executives who recently left a role and are likely in search.

**Filters:**
- Job title: CIO OR CTO OR "VP Technology" OR "VP Engineering" OR CISO OR CDO OR "Chief Information Officer" OR "Chief Technology Officer"
- Seniority: Director, VP, CXO, Partner, Owner
- Geography: United States — filter by metro if you want to start with specific cities (Bay Area, NYC, Chicago, Austin, Boston, Seattle, Atlanta)
- **Changed jobs in past 90 days** (this is the key filter)
- Connection degree: 2nd degree first, then 3rd

**Why 90 days:** People who left in the last 90 days haven't made tool decisions yet. They're still figuring out their approach. You are the first person to bring them something built for their exact situation.

**What to look for:** Profiles where the most recent role ended 1-3 months ago with no new role listed. That gap is the signal. If someone left 18 months ago they've either landed or established a different routine.

**Save this search.** Sales Navigator will alert you when new profiles match as they update their status.

**Volume:** 20 connection requests per day maximum. Do not exceed this — LinkedIn will flag accounts that send requests too aggressively.

**DM after connection accepted:**
> "[Name] — saw you recently wrapped up at [Company]. Built something you should know about — a daily intelligence platform for senior tech executives in search. Monitors your target companies before roles post publicly. You're exactly the audience it was built for. Free 30-day trial if you want to take a look. Happy to walk you through it in 15 minutes if that's useful."

## Search 2 — Past Company Warm Contacts (Hidden Warm Search)

**Goal:** Rediscover people you have genuine institutional connection to but have lost track of.

This is the most underused search in Sales Navigator and one of the highest-converting. You share history with these people — you worked at the same companies, navigated the same cultures, know the same internal dynamics. That shared context makes an outreach feel warm even if you haven't spoken in years.

**Filters:**
- Past company: [every company Rich has worked at — list them all]
- Current job title: CIO OR CTO OR VP Technology OR VP Engineering OR CISO OR CDO
- Seniority: VP, Director, CXO
- Geography: United States
- **Changed jobs in past 6 months** (the transition signal)

**Run one search per former employer.** The results from each search will feel different because the context differs — how you'd open a message to a former Cisco colleague differs from a former startup colleague.

**DM after connection accepted:**
> "[Name] — we crossed paths at [Company] back in [year]. I've been building something since then that I think you'll find relevant — a daily intelligence platform for senior tech executives in search. Monitors your target companies before roles post publicly. Given where you are right now, wanted to make sure you knew about it. Free trial if you want to try it."

## Search 3 — Career Coaches (B2B Sprint A)

**Goal:** Find independent career coaches who work with executive clients and could use Starting Monday as delivery infrastructure.

**Filters:**
- Title: "career coach" OR "executive coach" OR "outplacement consultant" OR "career strategist"
- Geography: United States
- Company headcount: 1-10 (individual practitioners, not large outplacement firms)
- Active on LinkedIn: posted in last 30 days (only available in Sales Navigator)
- Connection degree: 2nd degree first

**Secondary filter to add:** Look at their posts. If they post content about executive career transitions, VP-to-CXO moves, or senior search strategy, they are coaching at the right level. If their content is about resume formatting and cover letters for early-career professionals, move on.

**Volume:** 10 coach DMs per day. Lower volume, higher personalization. Reference something specific from their profile or recent post.

**DM after connection accepted:**
> "[Name] — saw you coach executives through transitions at [VP/CIO/CTO level]. Built a tool that several coaches are starting to use for client delivery — handles the daily company monitoring and briefing so you can focus on the strategy and relationship work. Would a 15-minute demo be worth your time? No obligation."

**Follow-up sequence:**
- Day 3 (no response): "Happy to share a short demo video if easier than a call."
- Day 7 (no response): "No worries if the timing isn't right — just let me know."
- No further follow-up. Move on.

**Tag these leads in Sales Navigator:** Create a list called "Coach Outreach" and add every coach you message. Track: sent, responded, demo scheduled, demo completed, trial started.

## Search 4 — VC/PE Operating Partners (B2B Sprint A)

**Goal:** Find operating partners and talent partners at venture and private equity firms who manage executive transitions for portfolio companies.

**Filters:**
- Title: "operating partner" OR "talent partner" OR "people partner" OR "chief of staff" OR "VP People" OR "Head of Talent"
- Company type: Venture Capital OR Private Equity (filter by industry)
- Geography: United States
- Active on LinkedIn: posted in last 30 days
- Connection degree: 2nd degree preferred

**The trigger-based approach (higher conversion than cold):**

Do not send generic outreach to VC/PE partners. Instead, set up account alerts on 20-30 VC-backed companies in your target sectors. When a leadership change appears — a CIO departure, a CTO exit, a restructuring announcement — reach out to the operating partner at that fund within 48 hours referencing the specific event.

Sources for triggers:
- LinkedIn company page updates ("[Name] is no longer at [Company]")
- Press releases and TechCrunch coverage of leadership changes
- Crunchbase "people" updates
- The company's own career page (a CIO vacancy appearing before it's announced)

**DM (trigger-based):**
> "[Name] — saw [Company] recently had a leadership change at the [CIO/CTO] level. Built a tool that a few operating partners are using to support portfolio executives through their next search — daily intelligence, company monitoring, prep briefs. Costs a fraction of traditional outplacement and the executive controls their own process. Happy to show you in 10 minutes if it's relevant for situations like this."

**DM (non-trigger, cold):**
> "[Name] — you manage executive transitions at [Fund] portfolio companies. Built a tool specifically for that moment — helps departing executives run a structured, intelligence-driven search from day one. Several operating partners are using it as an alternative to traditional outplacement. Would a quick demo be worth 10 minutes?"

## Search 5 — University Career Center Directors (B2B Sprint B)

**Goal:** Find career center directors at MBA programs and universities who advise alumni in senior executive search.

**Filters:**
- Title: "career center director" OR "director of career services" OR "assistant dean career" OR "executive career coach" OR "alumni career"
- Company type: Educational Institution
- Geography: United States
- Connection degree: 2nd degree preferred (a shared connection at a university matters significantly)

**The sequencing matters here:** Do not cold outreach to career center directors before the Wake Forest/Stanford Zoom produces a result. One warm referral from that Zoom ("you should talk to [name] at [school]") is worth 50 cold DMs. Let the May 18 Zoom generate the second wave of university contacts before going cold.

**DM (after warm referral):**
> "[Name] — [Contact] at [School] suggested I reach out. I built a tool for senior executives in search that she found relevant for her alumni community. Would love 15 minutes to show you what it does and get your honest reaction."

**DM (cold, no referral):**
> "[Name] — I built a daily intelligence platform for senior technology executives in active search. CIOs and CTOs use it to monitor target companies before roles post publicly. I'm talking to career centers about whether it would be useful for their alumni in senior transition. Would a 15-minute conversation be worth your time?"

## Search 6 — Retained Search Firm Partners (Referral Channel)

**Goal:** Find partners and managing directors at executive search firms who maintain warm candidate networks. These are not commercial sales targets — they are referral relationships.

**Filters:**
- Title: "partner" OR "managing director" OR "principal" OR "consultant"
- Company: Spencer Stuart OR Korn Ferry OR "Heidrick & Struggles" OR "Russell Reynolds" OR "Egon Zehnder" OR "Boyden" OR "Odgers Berndtson"
- Specialty/focus: technology, digital, CIO, CTO (look at their LinkedIn bio for signals)
- Geography: United States
- Connection degree: 1st or 2nd degree — this channel only works with some warmth

**The pitch:** No commercial arrangement. Give them 10 trial codes to share directly with executive candidates in their network. When a candidate mentions the search firm referred them and then lands, the search firm looks smart.

**DM:**
> "[Name] — I built a tool I think your executive technology candidates would find useful — daily company intelligence and prep briefs for senior search. I'm not looking for a commercial arrangement. I'd like to give you 10 trial codes to share with CIO/CTO candidates in your network. If they find it useful, I'd appreciate knowing. If not, no harm done. Would that be useful to you?"

**Start with people Rich already knows from his own search.** The 3-5 recruiters who reached out during Rich's transition are the first outreach here. Not cold.

## What Sales Navigator Cannot Find

Know the limits before you over-rely on it.

**Quiet searchers:** The CIO who was packaged out confidentially and has not updated their LinkedIn profile. These executives exist in large numbers and represent the highest-urgency ICP. They will not surface in any Sales Navigator search. They surface through warm referrals and word of mouth — which is why the first 20 personal messages matter more than any search.

**Executives who disable "open to work":** Most senior executives do not use the Open to Work badge because they don't want their current employer or board to see it. The "changed jobs" signal is more reliable than "open to work" for finding people in transition.

**People who are passive but convertible:** The SVP who is employed, not actively looking, but would move for the right role. Sales Navigator has no signal for this. Your personal network does — these are the people who say things like "I'm good where I am but I'm always open to interesting conversations."

### Rules That Apply to Every Message

1. **Never open with "I hope this message finds you well."** Delete it. Start with the specific reason you're reaching out.

2. **Never use the phrase "AI-powered."** It signals nothing in 2026. Describe what it does instead.

3. **One ask per message.** Either "would a demo be useful?" or "here's the trial link." Not both.

4. **Never write more than 5 sentences in an opening DM.** If they have to scroll, they won't.

5. **Personalize the first sentence only.** The structure of the rest is deliberate. Rewriting it to sound more personal usually makes it worse.

6. **Send in the morning.** LinkedIn DMs sent between 7-9am local time get meaningfully higher open rates than afternoon sends.

7. **One follow-up only for cold contacts. Two maximum for warm ones.** If they haven't responded after two touches, they are not interested right now. Remove them from the active list and revisit in 60 days.

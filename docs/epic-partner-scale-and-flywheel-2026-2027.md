# Epic: Partner Scale and Flywheel

## Objective

Turn Starting Monday into a repeatable partner-delivered system for coaches, outplacement firms, and adjacent executive transition operators.

## Primary KPI

Partner-sourced active accounts that complete at least one full campaign cycle.

## Secondary KPIs

- Partner pilot-to-scale conversion rate
- Client activation rate in partner cohorts
- Session-yield improvement for coaches
- Procurement cycle time
- Retention of partner-originated accounts

## Why This Epic Exists

The direct executive motion creates product proof, but partner channels can create repeatable distribution. Coaches and outplacement firms already own the relationship and trust layer. Starting Monday should supply the operating infrastructure around that relationship.

This epic converts the product from a standalone tool into a partner-ready system.

## Partner Motions

### Coach Motion

Coaches use Starting Monday to monitor target companies, prepare for sessions, and help clients stay organized.

What the partner gets:

1. Better-prepared clients.
2. Less time spent on routine research.
3. More structured follow-through after sessions.

### Outplacement Motion

Outplacement firms use Starting Monday to provide a measurable, technology-backed transition experience.

What the partner gets:

1. Better client activation.
2. Repeatable cohort delivery.
3. Stronger reporting and governance.

### Adjacent Search-Firm Motion

Executive search and transition firms can use the system as a trust-preserving layer for clients who need campaign structure.

## Workstream 1: Partner Value Proposition

Goal: Make the partner's win obvious in one conversation.

Deliverables:

- Partner-specific pitch language.
- Side-by-side comparison of manual vs. platform-supported workflow.
- One-page pilot framing for coaches and outplacement firms.
- Claims language that is conservative and board-safe.

Success criteria:

- A partner sponsor can explain the value to a colleague without a follow-up deck.
- The product is understood as additive, not replacement-oriented.

## Workstream 2: Pilot Design and Governance

Goal: Make pilots easy to run and easy to evaluate.

Deliverables:

- Pilot runbook.
- Role and cadence matrix.
- Escalation rules and intervention thresholds.
- Weekly outcome review template.

Success criteria:

- Partners can launch a pilot with minimal custom setup.
- Progress and risk are visible from week one.

## Workstream 3: Partner Visibility

Goal: Give partners the minimum necessary control and visibility without exposing sensitive user data.

Deliverables:

- Seat and cohort management.
- Usage visibility by client and cohort.
- Limited reporting on activation and engagement.
- Safe boundaries for coach and sponsor access.

Success criteria:

- Partners can manage their program without needing manual support for every request.
- Confidentiality remains intact.

## Workstream 4: Commercial Packaging

Goal: Package the motion in a way that can scale.

Deliverables:

- Referral arrangement.
- Co-branded bulk access option.
- Enterprise integration path.
- Pricing and term guidance for each motion.

Success criteria:

- There is a clear entry point for small partners and a path for larger ones.
- Pricing aligns with the level of operational lift required.

## Acceptance Matrix

- Partner pitch clarity: A+
- Pilot readiness: A+
- Governance: A+
- Visibility: A+
- Commercial packaging: A+

## Risks

1. Partners treating the product as a bolt-on instead of a workflow system.
2. Overexposing data in the name of reporting.
3. Selling before the pilot mechanics are actually repeatable.

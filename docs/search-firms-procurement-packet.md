# Search Firms Procurement Packet
Status: Draft for purchasing and revenue operations
Date: 2026-06-24
Owner: Revenue operations and product
Applies to: Search-firm pilot, lane rollout, and expansion

## 1) Purpose

This packet gives procurement and business buyers the information needed to evaluate and purchase Starting Monday for search-firm workflows with minimal friction.

Goals:
1. Standardize buying information across segments.
2. Shorten quote-to-order cycle time.
3. Align commercial terms, implementation scope, and legal pathway.

---

## 2) Buyer profiles this packet supports

1. Boutique specialist firms (<25 consultants)
- Needs: speed, low overhead, one-lane pilot confidence.

2. Mid-sized specialist or multi-practice firms (25-150 consultants)
- Needs: repeatable economics, clearer owner model, scalable rollout path.

3. Global firms (150+ consultants)
- Needs: compliance clarity, governance controls, phased onboarding.

---

## 3) Packaging framework

## Package A: Pilot - one mandate, 30 days

Intended for:
1. First-time proof and fast qualification.

Includes:
1. Setup and kickoff for selected mandate lane.
2. Candidate readiness workflow activation.
3. Pilot scorecard setup and day-30 decision review.

Success criteria examples:
1. Prep hours saved per mandate.
2. First-slate quality improvement signal.
3. Fewer mid-search resets.

## Package B: Lane rollout - 90 days

Intended for:
1. One role lane standardization (example: CFO or CIO).

Includes:
1. Multi-mandate deployment in selected lane.
2. Management operating cadence and reporting pack.
3. Practice-level adoption and quality reviews.

## Package C: Multi-lane expansion

Intended for:
1. Firms scaling across practices or regions.

Includes:
1. Lane-by-lane rollout sequence.
2. Governance checkpoints by practice lead.
3. Expansion scorecard and quarterly review pack.

## 4) Commercial information model

Pricing fields to populate per quote:
1. Package type
2. Included seat or participant assumptions
3. Service/support tier
4. Term and renewal structure
5. Optional add-ons

Billing options:
1. Monthly invoicing
2. Quarterly prepay
3. Annual prepay where applicable

Order artifacts:
1. Quote
2. Order form or SOW
3. Legal packet link and dependencies

## 5) Implementation effort estimate (buyer-facing)

Expected buyer-side time commitment by role:

1. Executive sponsor
- Initial setup: 60 to 90 minutes
- Weekly review: 30 minutes

2. Practice or delivery lead
- Initial setup: 90 to 120 minutes
- Weekly operations: 45 to 60 minutes

3. Candidate-success owner
- Initial setup: 2 to 3 hours
- Weekly operations: 60 to 90 minutes

4. Legal or procurement reviewer
- Initial review: 60 to 120 minutes depending on packet completeness

---

## 6) Procurement timeline and SLA targets

Standard timeline targets:
1. First buyer meeting to quote: 3 business days
2. Quote to legal-ready packet handoff: 2 business days
3. Legal and procurement review cycle target (mid-sized): <= 15 business days
4. Signed order to pilot kickoff: <= 5 business days

Escalation path:
1. Revenue operations owner for commercial blockers
2. Legal owner for contract blockers
3. Product owner for scope and capability blockers

---

## 9) Common procurement objections and response pattern

1. Objection: Scope feels too open-ended.
- Response: use one-mandate pilot scope with explicit stop/go criteria.

2. Objection: Buyer effort is unclear.
- Response: provide role-level implementation estimates and weekly cadence.

3. Objection: Value claims are unverified.
- Response: separate directional pilot claims from validated metrics and use scorecard baselines.

4. Objection: Too many dependencies before kickoff.
- Response: provide single packet with legal, purchasing, and implementation artifacts.

---

## 10) Decision gates

Gate 1: Commercial fit
1. Package selection complete.
2. Budget owner identified.

Gate 2: Procurement readiness
1. Required packet received.
2. Review owners assigned.

Gate 3: Legal readiness
1. Contract path selected.
2. Legal issues tracked with owner and due date.

Gate 4: Pilot launch readiness
1. Charter signed.
2. Sponsor and scorecard owner assigned.
3. Kickoff date confirmed.

---

## 11) Definition of ready to purchase

Ready when:
1. Package, scope, and term are agreed.
2. Legal packet is accepted for pilot start.
3. Sponsor and operating owners are confirmed.
4. Launch timeline is approved by both sides.

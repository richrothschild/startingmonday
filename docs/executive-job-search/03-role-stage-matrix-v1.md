# Executive Job Search Role x Stage Matrix v1

Updated: 2026-05-31

## Segments

- CEO / President
- COO / GM / BU leader
- CFO
- CHRO / HR leader
- CIO / CTO / Technology leader
- CMO / CRO / Commercial leader
- PR / Communications / Fundraising leader

## Transition Types

- voluntary growth move
- forced exit
- industry pivot
- functional pivot
- geography pivot
- re-entry after break

## Stage Model

1. Identity reset
2. Target selection
3. Narrative development
4. Market activation
5. Process navigation
6. Offer decision
7. First-90-day setup

## Matrix

### CEO / President

- Identity reset: board optics, legacy, and accountability identity are in play.
- Target selection: choose governance fit, strategic mandate, and board appetite.
- Narrative development: articulate enterprise change, not just personal achievement.
- Market activation: board contacts, advisors, and search consultants are primary channels.
- Process navigation: high sensitivity to board chemistry, cadence, and timeline clarity.
- Offer decision: company, challenge, compensation, and context are all consequential; board trust is often decisive.
- First-90-day setup: must create early wins, align team, and manage stakeholders quickly.

### COO / GM / BU leader

- Identity reset: shift from execution operator to enterprise leader.
- Target selection: emphasize scale, complexity, cross-functional scope, and authority.
- Narrative development: show how operating rigor creates value.
- Market activation: warm introductions and prior operating partners matter.
- Process navigation: watch for ambiguous charter and scope drift.
- Offer decision: evaluate whether authority matches accountability.
- First-90-day setup: establish operating cadence and decision rights.

### CFO

- Identity reset: control, trust, and risk stewardship are central.
- Target selection: look for capital structure, governance, and turnaround context.
- Narrative development: prove long-term financial clarity and resilience under pressure.
- Market activation: recruiters, investors, audit / board networks, and prior CEO references matter.
- Process navigation: due diligence on balance sheet quality and reporting maturity.
- Offer decision: downside protection and upside structure both matter.
- First-90-day setup: build trust with board, CEO, and finance team.

### CHRO / HR leader

- Identity reset: move from policy / operations to enterprise capability.
- Target selection: culture, talent transformation, and CEO partnership matter.
- Narrative development: show talent, culture, and change leadership.
- Market activation: peer CHROs, recruiters, and board-connected advisors are influential.
- Process navigation: test whether the organization truly wants transformation or only compliance.
- Offer decision: assess mandate clarity and sponsor quality.
- First-90-day setup: diagnose talent, leadership, and operating norms quickly.

### CIO / CTO / Technology leader

- Identity reset: shift from systems owner to business transformation partner.
- Target selection: platform modernization, AI, cybersecurity, and scale are key.
- Narrative development: translate technical depth into business impact.
- Market activation: tech recruiters, founder / operator networks, and peer references matter.
- Process navigation: verify executive sponsorship and delivery authority.
- Offer decision: assess appetite for change and stakeholder maturity.
- First-90-day setup: establish architecture priorities and business-facing trust.

### CMO / CRO / Commercial leader

- Identity reset: growth ownership and market credibility are the center.
- Target selection: seek value creation, pipeline quality, or brand repositioning contexts.
- Narrative development: show revenue logic, customer understanding, and go-to-market fit.
- Market activation: commercial peers, investors, and recruiters often drive flow.
- Process navigation: avoid over-claiming or under-quantifying impact.
- Offer decision: confirm what growth levers the company is actually willing to support.
- First-90-day setup: align on commercial metrics, funnel, and frontline cadence.

### PR / Communications / Fundraising leader

- Identity reset: reputation and influence become the core asset.
- Target selection: look for narrative complexity, stakeholder pressure, or fundraising needs.
- Narrative development: prove crisis handling, audience management, and trust building.
- Market activation: network quality and reputation signals matter disproportionately.
- Process navigation: test whether the employer values strategic communication or just execution.
- Offer decision: check mission, visibility, and influence path.
- First-90-day setup: stabilize external message and internal alignment.

## Pattern by Transition Type

### Voluntary Growth Move
- more selective
- stronger company / challenge filter
- more patient process navigation
- higher willingness to optimize for learning and upside

### Forced Exit
- higher urgency
- more identity threat
- more sensitivity to signal loss and confidence dips
- more likely to need structured regulation support

### Industry Pivot
- heavier proof burden
- more narrative work
- more network activation and reframing
- higher need for transferable-skill translation

### Functional Pivot
- greater need to translate capability across adjacent domains
- more careful challenge selection
- more risk of over- or under-selling prior scope

### Geography Pivot
- increased context friction
- family and timing constraints surface earlier
- more variable market norms and compensation logic

### Re-entry After Break
- stronger concern about signaling gaps
- confidence and identity support are critical
- narrative must reconcile gap with relevance

# Executive Job Search Interview Guide v1

Updated: 2026-05-31

## Interview Goals

1. Identify the candidate’s role segment.
2. Identify the transition type.
3. Identify current search stage.
4. Detect the dominant derailer.
5. Understand context constraints.
6. Choose the best intervention.

## Interview Structure

### Section 1: Role and Current Situation

Ask:
- What kind of role are you targeting right now?
- What are you leaving behind?
- What is your current search posture: passive, active, or in-process?
- What is making this transition necessary now?

Interpretation:
- role language reveals the segment
- the reason for moving reveals transition type
- speed and tone reveal urgency and emotional load

### Section 2: Target Selection

Ask:
- What kinds of companies are you considering?
- What problems do you want to solve in the next role?
- What trade-offs are you willing to make on company, challenge, compensation, and context?
- What would make you say no immediately?

Interpretation:
- company / challenge emphasis reveals search maturity
- rigid title focus can indicate signaling error
- unclear trade-offs can indicate decision paralysis

### Section 3: Narrative and Positioning

Ask:
- How are you currently describing your value?
- What story do you tell when asked why now?
- What proof points do you lead with?
- Where do you think the market misunderstands you?

Interpretation:
- weak proof density suggests proof-point compression
- inconsistent language suggests signaling error
- repeated rewriting suggests perfection loop

### Section 4: Outreach and Network

Ask:
- Who have you contacted first?
- How are you deciding who to ask for help?
- How often are you following up?
- What happens when you do not get a response?

Interpretation:
- low warm-contact use suggests network under-activation
- no recovery after silence suggests avoidance

### Section 5: Interviews and Process Navigation

Ask:
- What is the hardest part of the interview process for you?
- Which stakeholder matters most in the decision process?
- How do you prepare for final rounds?
- What slows decisions down?

Interpretation:
- process confusion suggests stakeholder mismanagement
- over-preparation suggests perfection loop
- unclear stakeholder map suggests board / sponsor map is needed

### Section 6: Offer Decision and Context

Ask:
- What would make an offer feel worth taking?
- What personal or family constraints matter here?
- Which of the 4Cs feels strongest or weakest?
- What would have to be true for you to move?

Interpretation:
- over-weighting compensation suggests a risk of short-term decisioning
- weak context discussion suggests context blindness
- refusal to choose among trade-offs suggests decision paralysis

### Section 7: Resilience and Energy

Ask:
- How are you feeling after setbacks?
- What changes in your behavior when you get discouraged?
- What keeps you moving?
- What support do you have?

Interpretation:
- activity drop after setbacks suggests transition exhaustion
- high control needs plus many revisions suggest perfection loop

## Interview Output

For each candidate, produce:
- role segment
- transition type
- search stage
- dominant behavior domain
- dominant derailer
- confidence tier
- recommended intervention
- key evidence snippets

## Scoring Guidance During Interview

- Use explicit evidence only.
- Do not force certainty when the signal is weak.
- If multiple role segments fit, choose the one that best matches stakeholder system and decision context.
- If the candidate is a passive executive, prioritize company/challenge/context over title and compensation.

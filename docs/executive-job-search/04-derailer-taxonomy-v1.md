# Executive Job Search Derailer Taxonomy v1

Updated: 2026-05-31

## Derailer Classes

### 1. Avoidance
The candidate delays action to avoid uncertainty, judgment, or identity threat.

Signals:
- delayed outreach
- stalled decision after interviews
- over-consumption of information without action
- disappearing after rejection

Likely mechanism:
- threat response and ambiguity intolerance

### 2. Perfection Loop
The candidate keeps revising rather than shipping.

Signals:
- endless résumé edits
- repeated narrative rewrites
- over-preparing before outreach
- reluctance to submit unless fully ready

Likely mechanism:
- control-seeking under uncertainty

### 3. Signaling Error
The candidate misrepresents or under-represents fit, scope, or value.

Signals:
- too much title focus
- weak scope translation
- unclear proof points
- inconsistent story across channels

Likely mechanism:
- identity / audience mismatch

### 4. Network Under-Activation
The candidate does not use available social capital enough.

Signals:
- few warm intros
- weak follow-up discipline
- over-reliance on cold applications
- no sponsor cultivation

Likely mechanism:
- discomfort with asking or low perceived opportunity value

### 5. Decision Paralysis
The candidate cannot choose among options.

Signals:
- late-stage stagnation
- checklist inflation
- excessive comparison across criteria
- inability to tolerate trade-offs

Likely mechanism:
- fear of irreversible choice

### 6. Context Blindness
The candidate ignores family, timing, geography, or risk constraints.

Signals:
- pursuing roles with obvious conflict
- surprise delays due to partner/family needs
- misreading timing windows
- chasing prestige over fit

Likely mechanism:
- overly rational decision model

### 7. Stakeholder Mismanagement
The candidate fails to read or shape the decision system around the role.

Signals:
- poor board / recruiter / hiring manager mapping
- missed 1:1s
- misunderstanding role-specific politics
- weak trust-building cadence

Likely mechanism:
- insufficient systems thinking

### 8. Transition Exhaustion
The candidate loses energy during a long search.

Signals:
- sporadic activity
- emotional flattening
- reduced response quality
- disengagement after setbacks

Likely mechanism:
- effort depletion and isolation

## Stage-Specific Risk Mapping

- Identity reset: avoidance, context blindness, exhaustion
- Target selection: decision paralysis, signaling error
- Narrative development: perfection loop, signaling error
- Market activation: network under-activation, avoidance
- Process navigation: stakeholder mismanagement, perfection loop
- Offer decision: decision paralysis, context blindness
- First-90-day setup: over-commitment, stakeholder mismanagement

## Suggested Diagnostics

- `search latency` = time from trigger to first meaningful action
- `outreach density` = number of relevant contacts activated per week
- `revision ratio` = edits per shipped artifact
- `decision lag` = days from final interview to accept / decline
- `rejection recovery time` = days to return to normal activity after a setback

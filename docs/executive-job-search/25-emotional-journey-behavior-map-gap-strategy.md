## Emotional Journey: Executive Transition

The emotional journey below is a pragmatic model for product design. It is not a clinical taxonomy.

### Emotion List (Evidence-Backed)

1. Urgency pressure
- Typical stage: trigger and early activation
- What drives it: forced exits, timeline pressure, market uncertainty
- Evidence anchors: Spencer Stuart 4Cs (passive to active transition pull), Derailer class Avoidance and Transition Exhaustion, Top 50 H1/H19/H37

2. Threat anxiety
- Typical stage: identity reset, first outreach, post-rejection
- What drives it: ambiguity, social evaluation, fear of signal loss
- Evidence anchors: Derailer Avoidance and Signaling Error, validated construct Identity Threat, Top 50 H13/H18/H26/H27

3. Identity loss / identity threat
- Typical stage: trigger through narrative rewrite
- What drives it: role loss, title mismatch, status uncertainty
- Evidence anchors: validated construct Identity Threat, archetype Re-entry after Break, Top 50 H24/H25/H30

4. Control-seeking perfectionism
- Typical stage: narrative building and pre-outreach
- What drives it: uncertainty and fear of mistakes
- Evidence anchors: Derailer Perfection Loop, Top 50 H27/H38/H42

5. Hope and future orientation
- Typical stage: target design and first quality conversations
- What drives it: perceived fit, challenge quality, network response
- Evidence anchors: LinkedIn Opportunity Index (opportunity and network effects), Spencer 4Cs (challenge/context pull), Top 50 H9/H15/H44

6. Confidence / self-efficacy
- Typical stage: interview progression and decision
- What drives it: prep quality, evidence-based narrative, feedback loops
- Evidence anchors: Intervention library (proof-point compression, role reframing), Top 50 H33/H42/H47

7. Shame and social vulnerability
- Typical stage: network activation and rejection periods
- What drives it: asking for help, visible setbacks, perceived status drop
- Evidence anchors: Derailer Network Under-Activation, Rejection Recovery Protocol rationale, Top 50 H18/H31/H34

8. Isolation and disconnection
- Typical stage: mid-search, long-cycle transitions
- What drives it: sparse sponsor map, low conversation density
- Evidence anchors: LinkedIn Opportunity Index (network activation gaps), Derailer Transition Exhaustion, Top 50 H14/H31/H37

9. Decision fear / regret aversion
- Typical stage: late stage and offer decisions
- What drives it: irreversible choice concerns, trade-off overload
- Evidence anchors: Spencer 4Cs, Derailer Decision Paralysis, Top 50 H29/H32/H35/H43

10. Context guilt / conflict tension
- Typical stage: final decision and family alignment
- What drives it: geography, partner/family, timing constraints
- Evidence anchors: Spencer 4Cs context dimension, Top 50 H11/H12/H30

11. Exhaustion and emotional flattening
- Typical stage: prolonged search cycles
- What drives it: repeated setbacks, high effort with low signal quality
- Evidence anchors: Derailer Transition Exhaustion, Top 50 H26/H34/H39

12. Relief, conviction, and re-engagement
- Typical stage: post-accept and first-90-day setup
- What drives it: clear choice, onboarding plan, early wins
- Evidence anchors: Spencer Pitfalls and CEO transition findings, Intervention First-90-Day Design, Top 50 H45/H46

## Emotional Journey by Stage

1. Trigger and identity reset
- Dominant emotions: urgency pressure, threat anxiety, identity loss
- Dominant failure modes: avoidance, perfection loops

2. Target and narrative design
- Dominant emotions: control-seeking perfectionism, hope, fragile confidence
- Dominant failure modes: signaling error, over-broad role framing

3. Market activation and process navigation
- Dominant emotions: social vulnerability, isolation, confidence swings
- Dominant failure modes: network under-activation, follow-up decay

4. Offer and decision
- Dominant emotions: decision fear, context tension, cautious optimism
- Dominant failure modes: decision paralysis, context blindness

5. First-90-day transition
- Dominant emotions: relief plus anticipatory stress
- Dominant failure modes: weak stakeholder mapping, delayed early wins

## How Emotions and Behaviors Work Together

### Core dynamic

1. Emotion state changes behavior quality faster than behavior volume.
2. Low-confidence and high-threat states produce over-action or no-action extremes.
3. Product effectiveness increases when the intervention matches both stage and emotion state.

### Product implication

1. Detect emotional risk from behavior signals.
2. Route the smallest effective intervention.
3. Measure emotional recovery through behavior stabilization.

## Gap Analysis

### Strengths already in place

1. Strong behavior taxonomy and derailer model.
2. Good intervention library for major failure modes.
3. Confidence-tier policy discipline.
4. Archetype framing for relevance.

### Critical gaps

1. No explicit emotion state model in diagnostics.
- Impact: interventions can be right in content but wrong in timing.

2. Weak early warning for emotional drift.
- Impact: failures are caught late (after rejection streaks or pipeline stall).

3. Limited closed-loop measurement of emotional recovery.
- Impact: hard to prove which interventions reduce abandonment risk.

4. Incomplete context instrumentation (family, geography, risk appetite).
- Impact: context tension remains underweighted in accept/decline decisions.

5. Decision support not yet robust for multi-offer trade-offs.
- Impact: late-stage paralysis and regret risk remain high.

6. Board-visible and sponsor-heavy tracks need higher-fidelity workflow support.
- Impact: stakeholder mismanagement risk persists in highest-value segments.

## Strategy to Fill Gaps

### Strategic objective

Increase transition success by coupling emotional-state detection with stage-specific behavior interventions.

### Strategic pillars

1. Detect
- Build an emotion-from-behavior risk layer (not clinical labels, operational states).

2. Decide
- Use stage + archetype + confidence tier + emotion state for intervention routing.

3. Act
- Trigger minimal effective action plans (micro-commitments, decision frames, sponsor tasks).

4. Learn
- Track recovery metrics and intervention lift weekly.

### Success metrics

1. 14-day abandonment risk rate down 25%.
2. Rejection recovery time down 30%.
3. Follow-up SLA (72h) adherence up 20%.
4. Decision lag after final round down 20%.
5. Time-to-first-value in onboarding down 15%.

## Epic

Epic name: Executive Emotional Transition OS

Epic hypothesis:

If we detect emotional risk states from behavior signals and deliver stage-appropriate interventions, executives will sustain higher-quality search motion and make better-fit decisions faster.

Epic scope:

1. Emotion-state schema and scoring.
2. Stage-aware intervention router.
3. Context and trade-off decision layer.
4. Recovery and performance telemetry.

Epic non-goals:

1. Clinical diagnosis.
2. Replacing human coaching for high-severity cases.

## Sprint Plan

Sprint cadence: 2 weeks each
Duration: 6 sprints

### Sprint 1: Emotional State Schema and Signals

Goals:

1. Define operational emotion states and thresholds.
2. Map each state to observable product signals.
3. Implement baseline scoring service.

Deliverables:

1. Emotion-state spec.
2. Signal dictionary v1.
3. Scoring API behind feature flag.

Exit criteria:

1. 95% of active users receive a non-null emotion-state score.

### Sprint 2: Stage and Archetype-Aware Routing

Goals:

1. Combine stage, archetype, and emotion state into intervention routing.
2. Add confidence-tier-safe fallback logic.

Deliverables:

1. Routing rules engine v1.
2. Risk-to-intervention matrix in product.

Exit criteria:

1. 80% of intervention recommendations are stage-appropriate in QA review.

### Sprint 3: Rejection and Exhaustion Recovery Loop

Goals:

1. Productize rejection recovery protocol.
2. Add exhaustion early-warning and micro-commitment interventions.

Deliverables:

1. Recovery workflow with 48-hour micro-commitments.
2. Exhaustion risk alert and escalation logic.

Exit criteria:

1. Recovery actions started within 48 hours for 70% of rejection events.

### Sprint 4: Context and Decision Intelligence

Goals:

1. Add explicit context capture (family, geography, risk constraints).
2. Launch multi-offer 4Cs decision workspace.

Deliverables:

1. Context note block in offer workflow.
2. Multi-offer trade-off simulator v1.

Exit criteria:

1. Decision lag reduced by 10% in test cohort.

### Sprint 5: Board/Sponsor High-Stakes Track

Goals:

1. Build board/sponsor mapping depth scoring.
2. Add board-visible outreach workflow support.

Deliverables:

1. Sponsor depth score and missing-link prompts.
2. High-specificity outreach composer.

Exit criteria:

1. Sponsor map completion before final round > 75% for board-track users.

### Sprint 6: Measurement, Experimentation, and Rollout

Goals:

1. Launch outcome dashboard for emotional recovery and behavior quality.
2. Run A/B tests on key interventions.
3. Prepare phased rollout and governance.

Deliverables:

1. Emotional journey dashboard.
2. Experiment report with go/no-go recommendations.
3. Rollout runbook and owner matrix.

Exit criteria:

1. At least 3 core metrics show statistically directional improvement in pilot.

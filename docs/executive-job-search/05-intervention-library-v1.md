# Executive Job Search Intervention Library v1

Updated: 2026-05-31

## Intervention Format

- Trigger
- Hypothesis
- Recommended prompt / action
- Expected mechanism
- Success metric
- Confidence tier

## 1. Role Reframing Sprint

- Trigger: candidate is over-focused on title or title adjacency.
- Hypothesis: the candidate is using title as a shortcut because role scope feels uncertain.
- Prompt / action: rewrite the target role around company, challenge, and stakeholder system.
- Expected mechanism: reduces signaling error and increases fit clarity.
- Success metric: target list with 3 role-fit reasons per opportunity.
- Confidence tier: A

## 2. 4Cs Decision Worksheet

- Trigger: late-stage indecision or “maybe” stuck state.
- Hypothesis: the candidate needs structured evaluation across company, challenge, compensation, and context.
- Prompt / action: force a four-column decision memo and a trade-off statement.
- Expected mechanism: reduces decision paralysis and clarifies compromise points.
- Success metric: decision made within 7 days of final interview.
- Confidence tier: A

## 3. Network Activation Burst

- Trigger: weak outreach volume or over-reliance on applications.
- Hypothesis: the candidate is under-using warm ties and sponsor paths.
- Prompt / action: create a 20-contact activation list with a 2-touch follow-up plan.
- Expected mechanism: increases opportunity sensing and social capital activation.
- Success metric: warm-response rate and number of active conversations.
- Confidence tier: A/B

## 4. Rejection Recovery Protocol

- Trigger: the candidate disappears or slows after a rejection.
- Hypothesis: identity threat is suppressing search momentum.
- Prompt / action: capture the failure, restate the learning, and schedule a 48-hour micro-commitment.
- Expected mechanism: restores action and reduces avoidance.
- Success metric: return to standard activity within 2 days.
- Confidence tier: B

## 5. Proof-Point Compression

- Trigger: candidate has strong experience but long, unfocused narratives.
- Hypothesis: the candidate needs fewer, denser proof points.
- Prompt / action: compress each story into challenge, action, metric, and lesson.
- Expected mechanism: improves interview clarity and reduces cognitive load.
- Success metric: story length reduction with same or better interview recall.
- Confidence tier: A

## 6. Board / Sponsor Map

- Trigger: CEO or board-visible search process.
- Hypothesis: the candidate is not mapping the real decision system.
- Prompt / action: identify each stakeholder’s interests, risks, and likely evaluation criteria.
- Expected mechanism: improves process navigation and trust building.
- Success metric: documented stakeholder map before final round.
- Confidence tier: A

## 7. Timing and Context Check

- Trigger: the candidate wants to say yes but family, geography, or macro timing may be misaligned.
- Hypothesis: context friction is being underweighted.
- Prompt / action: review family, location, timing, and downside scenarios explicitly.
- Expected mechanism: reduces bad-fit acceptance and later regret.
- Success metric: a written context note accompanying each offer.
- Confidence tier: A

## 8. First-90-Day Design

- Trigger: offer accepted or near acceptance.
- Hypothesis: early success depends on a concrete transition plan.
- Prompt / action: define first wins, stakeholder cadence, and learning priorities.
- Expected mechanism: improves transition readiness and early momentum.
- Success metric: 90-day plan completed before start date.
- Confidence tier: A

## 9. Search Rhythm Contract

- Trigger: long searches drift into inconsistency.
- Hypothesis: the candidate needs a predictable operating cadence.
- Prompt / action: set weekly outputs for outreach, narrative updates, and interview prep.
- Expected mechanism: reduces avoidance and exhaustion.
- Success metric: weekly adherence rate.
- Confidence tier: B

## 10. Channel Mix Review

- Trigger: candidates use one channel too heavily.
- Hypothesis: opportunity quality improves when channels are diversified.
- Prompt / action: compare recruiter, direct, warm network, and community channels.
- Expected mechanism: improves opportunity sensing.
- Success metric: 3+ active channels per search.
- Confidence tier: B

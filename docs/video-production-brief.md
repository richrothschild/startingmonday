## What We Are Building

Short-form video content for LinkedIn (primary), with repurposing to YouTube Shorts and other channels. The goal is to drive awareness and meeting bookings among three buyer segments: executive coaches, retained search firms, and outplacement providers.

Volume target: 2–3 videos per week once rhythm is established. Starting with a 5-video pilot batch.

---

## The Product in One Paragraph

Starting Monday is the operating system for a senior executive's job search. It watches target companies before roles are posted, builds role-specific preparation briefs that connect the leader's best work to an employer's hardest problems, and tracks execution week over week. The result: leaders enter conversations earlier, more prepared, and with better positioning than candidates who rely on conventional methods.

Three words: Signal. Prepare. Execute.

## Voice and Tone

**The target viewer** is a VP, SVP, or C-suite executive — or a professional who works with them (coach, search firm operator, outplacement firm). They have read Barbarians at the Gate. They do not need to be motivated. They need to be informed and convinced.

**Tone: board advisor, not life coach.** Peer-to-peer. Direct. No hype. No exclamation points.

**What this sounds like:**
> "Most executives treat their job search the way they'd never treat a sales pipeline. Stop-start. Generic. Reactive. The result is exactly what you'd expect."

> "Signal. Prepare. Execute. Three things, every week. The search becomes a campaign instead of a scramble."

**What this does NOT sound like:**
> "Unlock your potential!" / "You've got this!" / "Transform your career journey!"

**Written voice rules (mirror in on-screen text):**
- Short declarative sentences. One idea per sentence.
- No em dashes. No buzzwords. ("Use" not "leverage." "Show" not "illustrate.")
- Active voice. Present tense where possible.
- Second person: "you" not "executives should"
- No urgency tactics. No scarcity language.

## Video Formats

### Format A — "The Insight" (most common)
- Length: 45–75 seconds
- Structure: Observation → Problem → Reframe → CTA
- Style: Talking head or voiceover over text/graphic
- On-screen text: key phrase per sentence (not full transcript)
- Example hook: "Here is what separates executives who get calls from those who don't."

### Format B — "The How"
- Length: 60–90 seconds
- Structure: Here is how it works → Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Outcome
- Style: Screen walkthrough or animated explainer
- Goal: Show the Signal/Prepare/Execute loop in action

### Format C — "The Stat/Fact"
- Length: 20–30 seconds
- Structure: Surprising stat → one-line implication → CTA
- Style: Text-first with minimal motion
- Goal: Stoppable in a LinkedIn feed

## What We Do Not Want

- Stock footage of handshakes, glass offices, or people typing on laptops
- Motivational music (no "epic rise" tracks)
- Jump cuts so frequent they feel like TikTok
- Lower thirds that look like a news chyron
- Any reference to "hustle," "grind," "dream job," or similar language
- Reactions, pointing-at-text-on-screen gestures
- Excessive zoom-ins during talking head footage

## The Three Audiences (for Context)

**1. Executive Coaches** — want to make their clients more effective, not replace themselves. Frame: "Your clients get better results. You look better."

**2. Retained Search Firms** — want prepared candidates who close faster. Frame: "Better-prepared candidates shorten your cycle and reduce ghosting."

**3. Outplacement Firms** — want to reduce time-to-placement and justify fees. Frame: "Move people from reactive to proactive. Faster placements. Better ROI on every engagement."

Videos may be targeted to one audience or address all three implicitly. Brief will specify per batch.

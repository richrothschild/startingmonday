# Epic: 7-Layer Luxury Experience Foundation

## Objective

Build a trust-first executive experience across the full Starting Monday journey so high-stakes users feel known, safe, and in control.

## Primary KPI

Decision-confidence score across executive and coach workflows.

## Secondary KPIs

- Time-to-first-value for executive onboarding
- Prep brief trust rating
- Completed high-stakes flows without abandonment
- Gate pass rate for trust-sensitive changes

## Why This Epic Exists

Executive transition is not a commodity use case. Users are sharing confidential search context and making consequential decisions under time pressure. The product must therefore behave like premium decision infrastructure, not a generic AI tool.

This epic turns the 7-layer operating model into product and delivery requirements.

## Layer Definitions

1. Surface: first impression, framing, and initial trust signal.
2. Sensory: calm defaults, low-noise presentation, emotional containment.
3. Choreography: sequence of actions and decision checkpoints.
4. Anticipation: proactive guidance, nudges, and risk flags.
5. Infrastructure: reliability, observability, and safety controls.
6. Memory: continuity of context and prior decisions.
7. Identity: executive-grade language and role-aware behavior.

## Workstream 1: Executive Onboarding as a Decision Cockpit

Goal: Replace generic intake with a guided, role-aware entry path.

Deliverables:

- Role-specific onboarding fields and defaults.
- Search persona, transition type, and stage capture.
- Immediate readout of search readiness and next step.
- Clear confidentiality framing before deeper intake.

Success criteria:

- Users understand the system's model of their search within one session.
- Onboarding produces a visible sense of progress, not just data collection.

## Workstream 2: Briefs and Outputs that Reduce Decision Risk

Goal: Ensure every generated output improves confidence, not volume.

Deliverables:

- Prep brief structure tuned to executive conversations.
- Provenance and confidence cues in generated content.
- Human-checkable summaries for sensitive recommendations.
- Consistent formatting across core outputs.

Success criteria:

- Users can distinguish supported guidance from speculative suggestions.
- Briefs are used in live conversations, not just read once.

## Workstream 3: High-Stakes Flow Safety

Goal: Prevent trust erosion in the most sensitive product paths.

Deliverables:

- Gate checks for confidentiality-sensitive edits.
- Strong defaults for private data handling.
- Clear ownership for each high-risk decision path.
- Recovery flows when data or context is incomplete.

Success criteria:

- Sensitive workflows are reliable and predictable.
- High-stakes users do not have to re-explain context repeatedly.

## Workstream 4: Operating Model Enforcement

Goal: Make quality non-optional across design, implementation, and release.

Deliverables:

- Weekly 7-layer scorecard.
- Pass/fail/hold decision log.
- Remediation ownership and due dates.
- Release criteria tied to trust impact.

Success criteria:

- Changes that do not improve confidence, trust, or clarity are blocked.
- The team can explain why a change shipped or did not ship.

## Sprint Plan

### Sprint 1: Surface and Identity

Deliverables:

- Executive-grade onboarding language.
- Persona-aware dashboard framing.
- Simplified entry path for first-time users.

### Sprint 2: Sensory and Choreography

Deliverables:

- Low-noise decision cockpit layout.
- Sequenced guidance for the first three actions.
- Stronger transitions between capture, planning, and action.

### Sprint 3: Anticipation and Memory

Deliverables:

- Proactive prompts based on search stage.
- Persisted context that survives return visits.
- Better reuse of prior company, contact, and brief context.

### Sprint 4: Infrastructure and Enforcement

Deliverables:

- Trust-sensitive release gates.
- Better observability for high-stakes workflows.
- Weekly scorecard review and remediation loop.

## Acceptance Matrix

- Surface: A+
- Sensory: A+
- Choreography: A+
- Anticipation: A+
- Infrastructure: A+
- Memory: A+

## Risks

1. Over-optimizing for polish while missing the actual executive workflow.
2. Adding noise in the name of helpfulness.
3. Failing to enforce the trust bar consistently across releases.

# Starting Monday — Product and Persona Fit

startingmonday.app | May 2026

---

## What Starting Monday Is

Starting Monday is an AI-powered career search operating system for senior professionals. It is not a job board, a recruiter network, or a resume service. It is infrastructure for running a disciplined search campaign — the kind of search that wins the right role at the right level, not just the next available one.

The product is built on a single insight: at the VP, CIO/CTO, and C-suite level, the best roles are filled before they are posted. The candidate who walks into the first conversation most prepared, with the clearest positioning and the most current intelligence, wins. Starting Monday is the system that makes that candidate you.

---

## Core Value Proposition

Career page scanning three times a week surfaces roles at target companies before they appear on LinkedIn or job boards. Intelligence signals — funding rounds, executive hires and departures, acquisitions, expansions — flag when a company is about to have a need before the search is authorized.

The prep brief assembles everything relevant: company context, recent signals, attached documents, the user's own background. It is not a generic summary. It is built from the intersection of their company and your actual record, in sixty seconds.

Pipeline tracking, contact management, follow-up reminders, and a daily briefing replace the spreadsheet that every executive candidate builds and immediately falls behind on.

The AI chat assistant has full context — resume, pipeline, signals, contacts — and can answer questions, help think through positioning, and take actions directly: updating pipeline stage, setting follow-up reminders, updating company notes.

## Persona Fit

### The Active Executive Candidate

This is the primary persona. A VP, SVP, CIO, CTO, COO, or CFO in active search — someone with five or more years in their last role, a strong record, a clear sense of what they want, and a growing frustration that the search process at their level is opaque and manual. They are in motion because their role was eliminated or restructured, because they are departing voluntarily after an acquisition or leadership change, because they are running a confidential campaign while still employed, or because they are returning to the market after a deliberate pause.

What they need is intelligence on target companies before positions are posted, a way to track dozens of companies and relationships without rebuilding a spreadsheet every Monday, preparation that reflects their specific background rather than a generic template, and something that respects their time. Thirty minutes of setup, not a three-hour project.

The fit is the Active plan. Every AI feature is relevant. The prep brief is the most immediately valuable — they have calls coming and need to walk in prepared. Willingness to pay is high: the cost of a single mis-positioned conversation at this level, the wrong compensation discussion, the wrong title, the wrong role, far exceeds $199 per month. The downside of a failed search campaign is measured in months of foregone earnings, not in subscription fees.

---

### The Passive Monitor

A senior professional who is not actively searching but is paying attention. Stable in their current role, watching for the right opportunity — a specific company, a specific type of mandate, a specific market moment. They will not admit to being in the market. They are not. But they track.

They want early warning if a role opens or a signal fires at one of their target organizations. They want a contact tracker so relationships do not go cold during what might be a two-year wait for the right move. They want something low-friction: set it up once and it runs.

The fit is the Passive plan. Career page scanning, signals, pipeline, contacts. No AI features needed yet. At $49 per month, the decision is easy for a professional earning $250,000 or above — the value proposition is optionality, knowing before anyone else if the right opening appears. This persona converts to the Active plan at the moment they decide to commit.

---

### The Level-Up Candidate

A Director moving to VP, or a VP making the move to CIO or CTO. They have the record. They are ready for the next seat. They understand that the search required to make this jump is different from anything they have done before. Roles at the target level are not applied for; they are cultivated. They know it. They are not sure how to do it.

They need strategic framing — the strategy brief — to clarify and sharpen their positioning for a title they have not yet held. They need prep briefs that help them present as the hire, not the candidate. They need outreach drafting for cold and warm contacts that sounds like them. And they need an advisor they can think out loud with.

The fit is the Active plan, with the strategy brief and AI chat as the primary features. This persona may move to Executive once that tier launches. Willingness to pay is high: the compensation gap between a strong VP role and a CIO seat is often $200,000 to $500,000 annually. Getting the positioning wrong is an expensive mistake.

---

### The Transformation CIO

A CIO or senior IT leader whose career has been defined by transformation mandates: ERP, cloud migration, digital transformation, M&A integration. Often searched for by a narrow set of PE firms, boards, and turnaround situations. The mandate is specific; the audience is specific; the positioning must be precise.

They have run three to five transformation programs. They know which types of organizations need exactly what they bring. The search is quiet and targeted — twenty organizations, not two hundred — and they need to be positioned as the candidate before the search is even opened. The principals who make the call are PE operating partners, board members, and a small set of executive search specialists. The market is entirely relationship-driven.

The fit is the Executive plan, currently in waitlist. Board and PE firm intelligence is specifically designed for this persona. The Active plan bridges until Executive launches. The transformation CIO does not apply for jobs; they position themselves into conversations that precede the search. Everything Starting Monday does — signals, intelligence, relationship tracking, briefing — serves that goal.

---

### The Campaign Candidate

Someone for whom the search is a full-time commitment. C-suite, board-track, or managing partner level. The stakes are high enough that human expertise and warm introductions are worth a significant investment alongside the AI infrastructure.

They are running a ninety-day campaign. They want a strategy session with someone who knows the market. They need curated recruiter introductions, not cold outreach. They want both the platform and a human layer that can review their briefs, coach their positioning, and open doors.

The fit is the Campaign plan, in waitlist with limited availability. The concierge tier combines the full platform with human delivery. The three-month minimum exists because there is no value in a managed campaign that ends before the search does.

## Product-Market Fit Summary

Starting Monday is not competing with LinkedIn, Indeed, or Greenhouse. It is not a job board. It is not an applicant tracking system.

The closest analogy is a campaign management platform for a political candidate: it tracks the territory, manages the relationships, surfaces intelligence, and prepares the candidate for every conversation. The user is not applying for jobs. They are running a search.

The market is senior professionals at VP and above in active or latent search. Approximately 500,000 such professionals transition roles in the United States every year. The segment willing to invest in their own search infrastructure — professionals who think of their career as a business to be managed — is the target.

A CIO who walks into a first call having read a Starting Monday prep brief, who tracked the right company long enough to know about a role before it was posted, who sent an outreach email at exactly the right moment because a signal fired — that is the story. Every feature is designed to create that outcome.

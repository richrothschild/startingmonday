# Starting Monday — Product & Persona Fit

**startingmonday.app** | Last updated: May 2026

---

## What Starting Monday Is

Starting Monday is an AI-powered career search operating system for senior professionals. It is not a job board, a recruiter network, or a resume service. It is infrastructure for running a disciplined search campaign — the kind of search that wins the right role at the right level, not just the next available one.

The product is built on a single insight: **at the VP, CIO/CTO, and C-suite level, the best roles are filled before they are posted.** The candidate who walks into the first conversation most prepared, with the clearest positioning and the most current intelligence, wins. Starting Monday is the system that makes that candidate you.

---

## Core Value Proposition

**Know before anyone else.** Career page scanning three times a week surfaces roles at target companies before they appear on LinkedIn or job boards. Intelligence signals (funding rounds, executive hires and departures, acquisitions, expansions) flag when a company is about to have a need — before the search is authorized.

**Prepare in 60 seconds.** The prep brief assembles everything relevant — company context, recent signals, attached documents, the user's own background — into a structured interview brief. It is not a generic summary; it is built from the intersection of their company and your actual record.

**Manage the campaign, not the inbox.** Pipeline tracking, contact management, follow-up reminders, and a daily briefing replace the spreadsheet that every executive candidate builds and immediately falls behind on.

**An advisor that knows your situation.** The AI chat assistant has full context — resume, pipeline, signals, contacts — and can answer questions, help think through positioning, and take actions (update stage, set follow-up) directly.

## Persona Fit

### Primary Persona: The Active Executive Candidate

**Who**: VP, SVP, CIO, CTO, COO, CFO, or equivalent. Actively searching. Has been in role for 5+ years, has a strong record, knows what they want, and is frustrated that the process feels manual and opaque at their level.

**Situation**:
- Role was eliminated or restructured
- Voluntarily departing after an acquisition or leadership change
- Running a confidential search while still employed
- Returning to the market after a deliberate pause

**What they need**:
- Intelligence on target companies before positions are posted
- A way to track dozens of companies and relationships without a spreadsheet
- Preparation that reflects their specific background, not a generic template
- Something that respects their time — 30 minutes of setup, not a 3-hour project

**Fit**: Active plan. All AI features. The prep brief is the most immediately valuable feature — they have calls coming and need to walk in prepared.

**Willingness to pay**: High. The cost of a single mis-positioned conversation at this level (wrong comp, wrong level, wrong role) far exceeds $199/month. The downside of a failed search campaign is measured in months, not dollars.

---

### Secondary Persona: The Passive Monitor

**Who**: Senior professional not actively searching but paying attention. Stable in their current role but watching for the right opportunity — a specific company, a specific type of mandate, a specific market moment.

**Situation**:
- "I'm not looking but I'd take the right call"
- Tracking 5–10 target companies quietly
- Wants early warning if a role opens or a signal fires
- Not ready to invest in a full search campaign

**What they need**:
- Automated monitoring of target companies (career page + signals)
- A weekly digest that takes 10 minutes to read
- A contact tracker so relationships don't go cold
- Low-friction — set it up once and it runs

**Fit**: Passive plan. Career page scanning + signals + pipeline + contacts. No AI features needed yet.

**Willingness to pay**: Moderate. $49/month is a low-friction decision for a professional earning $250K+. The value proposition is optionality — knowing before anyone else if the right opening appears.

---

### Emerging Persona: The Level-Up Candidate

**Who**: Director → VP, VP → CIO/CTO. Has the record, is ready for the next seat, and understands this is a different type of search than anything they've done before. Roles at the target level are not applied for — they are cultivated.

**Situation**:
- "I know I'm ready. I need to run this like a campaign."
- Smaller set of target organizations (quality over volume)
- Building relationships with search firms, board members, peer networks
- Needs help positioning for a level they haven't held yet

**What they need**:
- Strategic framing (strategy brief) to clarify and sharpen positioning
- Prep briefs that help them present as the hire, not the candidate
- Outreach drafting for cold and warm contacts that sounds like them
- An advisor to pressure-test thinking

**Fit**: Active plan. Strategy brief + AI chat + prep briefs are the core workflow. May be an Executive plan candidate once that is out of waitlist.

**Willingness to pay**: High. The gap between VP and CIO compensation is often $200K–$500K annually. The cost of getting the positioning wrong is enormous.

---

### Specialized Persona: The CIO in Transformation Search

**Who**: Transformation CIO (IT modernization, digital transformation, ERP, cloud migration). Often searched for by a narrow set of PE firms, boards, and turnaround situations. The mandate is specific; the audience is specific; the positioning must be precise.

**Situation**:
- Has run 3–5 transformation programs
- Knows which types of organizations need exactly what they do
- The search is quiet and targeted — 20 companies, not 200
- Needs to be positioned as the candidate before the search is even opened

**What they need**:
- Intelligence on PE portfolio companies, private companies, and board-level transitions
- Precise positioning narrative for the transformation mandate (not a generic CIO)
- Prep briefs tuned to private-company / PE-backed dynamics
- Relationship management across a tight, interconnected network

**Fit**: Executive plan (waitlist). Board and PE firm intelligence is specifically designed for this persona. Active plan bridges until Executive launches.

---

### Long-Term Persona: The Campaign Candidate

**Who**: Someone for whom the search is a full-time commitment. C-suite, board-track, or managing partner level. The stakes are high enough that human expertise and warm introductions are worth the investment.

**Situation**:
- Running a 90-day campaign
- Wants both the AI infrastructure and human support
- Needs curated recruiter introductions, not cold outreach
- Wants a strategy session with someone who knows the market

**Fit**: Campaign plan (waitlist, limited availability). The concierge tier combines the full platform with human delivery.

## Product-Market Fit Summary

Starting Monday is not competing with LinkedIn, Indeed, or Greenhouse. It is not a job board. It is not an applicant tracking system.

The closest analogy is a campaign management platform for a political candidate — it tracks the territory, manages the relationships, surfaces intelligence, and prepares the candidate for every conversation. The user is not applying for jobs. They are running a search.

The market is senior professionals (VP+) in active or latent search. In the US alone, there are approximately 500,000+ VP-and-above professionals who transition roles in any given year. The segment willing to invest in their own search infrastructure — professionals who think of their career as a business to be managed — is the target.

**The product wins when the user wins.** A CIO who walks into a first call having read a Starting Monday prep brief, who tracked the right company long enough to know about a role before it posted, who sent an outreach email at exactly the right moment because a signal fired — that is the story. Every feature is designed to create that outcome.

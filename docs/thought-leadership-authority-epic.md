# Epic: 12-Week Thought Leadership Authority Build

Status: Proposed
Owner: Founder Office (Rich)
Start date: 2026-05-22
Target end date: 2026-08-14

## Epic Goal

Build category-leading founder authority in executive-search readiness so Starting Monday becomes the default trusted voice in the right circles: executive coaches, search-adjacent advisors, and senior operators.

## Success Criteria

1. Category phrase recall reaches at least 30 percent of qualified inbound conversations by week 12.
2. Three flagship authority assets are published with denominator-backed evidence.
3. Twelve earned appearances are completed (podcast, panel, webinar, or guest article).
4. At least 24 qualified inbound opportunities are attributable to thought-leadership channels.
5. Forty high-value relationships move from cold to active two-way exchange.
6. Thought leadership council quality score reaches A or A+ in final week review.

## Scope

This epic operationalizes:

1. docs/content/thought-leadership-synthetic-council.md
2. docs/content/thought-leadership-12-week-execution-plan.md
3. docs/content/content-standards.md

## Primary KPI Stack

1. Authority: speaking invites, media asks, repeat invitations.
2. Trust: denominator-backed claims coverage, citations, framework references.
3. Reach quality: qualified replies, strategic shares, saves by target personas.
4. Commercial impact: qualified inbound opportunities and high-fit intros.

## Sprint Plan (6 Sprints, 2 Weeks Each)

### Sprint 1 (Weeks 1-2): Positioning Lock and Proof Baseline

Goal: Lock one category position and establish evidence discipline.

Stories:

#### Story A1.1: Category and framework lock

Acceptance criteria:

1. Final category phrase is fixed and used in all founder-facing assets.
2. Executive Readiness Index v1 is documented as a one-page framework.
3. One founder narrative arc is finalized for posts, talks, and interviews.

#### Story A1.2: Evidence and methodology baseline

Acceptance criteria:

1. Standard proof block template is created (claim, denominator, timeframe, confidence).
2. First proof artifact is published with transparent source notes.
3. One methodology note is drafted for future public release.

#### Story A1.3: Initial distribution setup

Acceptance criteria:

1. Initial target list of 75 nodes is created across five circles.
2. Three founder posts and one anchor memo are published each week.
3. Twelve outreach touches are executed weekly with tracked outcomes.

Exit gate:

1. Sprint KPI status is Green in at least 6 out of 8 tracked metrics.

### Sprint 2 (Weeks 3-4): Coach and Search-Circle Credibility

Goal: Build authority with the two highest-leverage professional circles first.

Stories:

#### Story A2.1: Coach-circle penetration

Acceptance criteria:

1. One private coach roundtable is hosted.
2. One coach-focused anchor memo is published each week.
3. At least 8 coach-circle responses are logged by sprint end.

#### Story A2.2: Search-adjacent proof narrative

Acceptance criteria:

1. One search-firm-adjacent memo is published with practical readiness criteria.
2. One live conversation (podcast or webinar) is completed.
3. Ten search-adjacent contacts receive a tailored one-page brief.

#### Story A2.3: Objection library for thought leadership

Acceptance criteria:

1. Top 10 recurring objections are documented.
2. One response asset is published addressing the top objection.
3. Outreach scripts are updated with objection-aware language.

Exit gate:

1. At least 3 qualified inbound opportunities per week by end of sprint.

### Sprint 3 (Weeks 5-6): Signature Framework Launch and Media Pipeline

Goal: Launch the proprietary framework and convert it into media-ready IP.

Stories:

#### Story A3.1: ERI flagship launch

Acceptance criteria:

1. ERI v1 flagship asset is published.
2. Three derivative posts are published to explain each ERI layer.
3. One applied mini-case demonstrates ERI in a real scenario.

#### Story A3.2: Repurposing and appearance pipeline

Acceptance criteria:

1. ERI content is converted into podcast, webinar, and guest article pitches.
2. At least 5 hosts or editors are pitched from the target list.
3. Two earned opportunities are confirmed for future dates.

#### Story A3.3: Relationship velocity system

Acceptance criteria:

1. Relationship tracker is maintained weekly with next-action dates.
2. Twelve high-priority touches are executed weekly.
3. Follow-up completion rate is at least 80 percent.

Exit gate:

1. At least 5 organic external references to ERI by sprint end.

### Sprint 4 (Weeks 7-8): Operator and Governance Expansion

Goal: Expand authority from coaching/search circles into operator and board-adjacent circles.

Stories:

#### Story A4.1: Operator community trust build

Acceptance criteria:

1. One operator-focused private briefing is hosted.
2. One operator-focused anchor memo is published each week.
3. Post-event follow-up checklist is sent to all attendees.

#### Story A4.2: Governance and long-cycle narrative

Acceptance criteria:

1. One board-path memo is published with long-cycle readiness guidance.
2. One long-cycle proof point post is published with specific examples.
3. Governance-circle outreach touches total at least 12 in sprint.

#### Story A4.3: Message consistency audit

Acceptance criteria:

1. Category phrase appears in at least 90 percent of published assets.
2. Narrative arc consistency is validated across posts and talks.
3. Drift corrections are applied within one week.

Exit gate:

1. At least one governance-focused speaking invitation or equivalent signal.

### Sprint 5 (Weeks 9-10): Scale Through Earned Channels and Proof Governance

Goal: Increase authority scale while preserving evidence quality.

Stories:

#### Story A5.1: Earned channel expansion

Acceptance criteria:

1. One earned appearance is completed each week.
2. Five additional host/editor pitches are sent with strongest proof asset.
3. Media pipeline has at least 3 active opportunities at sprint end.

#### Story A5.2: Proof hygiene and transparency

Acceptance criteria:

1. All major claims include denominator and timeframe.
2. A public methodology post is published.
3. One uncertainty-aware post is published to reinforce credibility.

#### Story A5.3: Council-quality control

Acceptance criteria:

1. All major assets pass thought leadership council review before publish.
2. No asset is published with below A- council score.
3. Weekly council notes include action items with owners.

Exit gate:

1. Proof hygiene compliance is 100 percent for sprint outputs.

### Sprint 6 (Weeks 11-12): Authority Conversion and Operating System Lock

Goal: Convert visibility into durable strategic access and lock the ongoing operating system.

Stories:

#### Story A6.1: High-trust conversion motion

Acceptance criteria:

1. One invite-only executive briefing is hosted.
2. Twelve high-context follow-ups are sent to top relationships.
3. At least 5 strategic introductions are generated during sprint.

#### Story A6.2: Capstone report and next-quarter plan

Acceptance criteria:

1. A 12-week capstone report is published with wins, misses, and next hypotheses.
2. Top-performing assets and scripts are archived into reusable playbook format.
3. Quarter-two plan is approved with owner and dates.

#### Story A6.3: Final council audit

Acceptance criteria:

1. Final thought leadership council review is completed.
2. Each council member returns A or A+.
3. Remaining risks are documented with mitigation owners.

Exit gate:

1. Epic success criteria met or documented with explicit variance and remediation plan.

## Dependencies

1. Reliable weekly access to founder writing and review time.
2. Distribution channels with direct access (LinkedIn, newsletter, podcast outreach).
3. Proof data source pipeline with denominator and date controls.
4. Council review process and cadence discipline.

## Risks and Mitigations

1. Risk: content volume rises but authority quality falls.
Mitigation: strict council gate and denominator rule for all major claims.

2. Risk: broad audience targeting dilutes category ownership.
Mitigation: prioritize top two circles in first four weeks.

3. Risk: inconsistent publishing cadence undermines trust.
Mitigation: fixed weekly formula and Friday scorecard review.

4. Risk: appearances convert weakly to strategic relationships.
Mitigation: mandatory follow-up playbooks and relationship tracker discipline.

## Milestones

1. M1 (End week 2): category and evidence operating baseline locked.
2. M2 (End week 6): ERI launched and media pipeline active.
3. M3 (End week 10): earned channels scaling with 100 percent proof hygiene.
4. M4 (End week 12): capstone complete, A-range council outcome, Q2 plan approved.

## Definition of Done

1. All six sprints complete with exit gates reviewed.
2. Final KPI outcomes reported against thresholds.
3. Capstone report and next-quarter operating plan published.
4. Thought leadership council grades at A or A+ with mitigation plan for any residual gap.

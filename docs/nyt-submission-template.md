## 5) Article Structure Template (Guest Essay)

### Opening (100-150 words)
- Start with a current, concrete moment or tension.
- State the thesis by paragraph two.

### Context (150-250 words)
- Explain what happened and why it matters nationally.
- Add only essential background.

### Evidence and Analysis (300-450 words)
- Use three to five strong evidence blocks.
- Prioritize verifiable facts and direct relevance.

### Counterpoint and Rebuttal (120-200 words)
- Name the strongest opposing view fairly.
- Respond with specific evidence or logic.

### Conclusion (80-150 words)
- End with a clear implication, choice, or call for action.
- Avoid vague endings.

## 6) NYT-Readiness Checklist

### Editorial Fit
- [ ] Timely and tied to an active public conversation.
- [ ] Argument is original, specific, and non-generic.
- [ ] National significance is clear.
- [ ] Best section and audience are clearly identified.

### Evidence and Credibility
- [ ] All key claims can be verified.
- [ ] Facts, dates, names, and stats are accurate.
- [ ] Sources are current and authoritative.
- [ ] Author qualifications are clear and relevant.

### Style and Quality
- [ ] AP style checklist completed.
- [ ] Clear, active language with no jargon bloat.
- [ ] No hype, no unsupported certainty, no filler.
- [ ] Tight headline and strong opening paragraph.

### Risk and Compliance
- [ ] Defamation and legal risk reviewed.
- [ ] Conflicts disclosed.
- [ ] Rights and exclusivity status stated.
- [ ] No plagiarism and no fabricated claims.

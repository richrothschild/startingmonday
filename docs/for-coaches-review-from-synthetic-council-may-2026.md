# /for-coaches Page Review from Synthetic Council
## May 2026

This document contains evaluations of the enhanced /for-coaches landing page from the advertising and coaching synthetic councils.

---

## David Ogilvy — Brand Positioning & Credibility

**Grade: A-**

**What works:**
- The hero is now speaking directly to the coach's identity ("Give your clients the infrastructure so you can focus on strategy"), not platform features. That's the premium positioning.
- "The coach credibility play" section as the organizing principle is smart. You're not selling them a tool—you're selling them credibility with their clients.
- The four personas are specific. They're not generic. A career transition specialist operates in a completely different context than a board positioning coach. Recognition of this specificity builds trust that you understand the profession.
- The "before/after" format still works, but you've elevated it: "When your clients move faster and make better decisions, they tell their peers you made them better." That's the compound credibility mechanism. That's the win.

**What's missing or unclear:**
- The hero tagline "Your coaching style. Multiplied." lands, but consider testing "Your coaching leverage. Multiplied." or "Your coaching impact. Compounded." The word "leverage" speaks to execution. "Multiplied" could be more muscular.
- The four personas section is strong, but consider adding ONE paragraph at the top explaining that these aren't different products—they're the same platform, different contexts. Right now it could read as "pick your type." You want it to read as "all of these are possible."
- "What your clients get" section feels like a feature step backward. Consider reframing it as "What changes for your clients" rather than listing features. Example: instead of "Intelligence Scanner," say "Your clients stop missing signals. The scanner watches while they sleep."

**To reach an A:**
- Test the hero tagline against "Your coaching leverage. Compounded." 
- Add a bridging paragraph before the personas explaining that the platform scales across all coaching styles.
- Reframe the client features section from feature names to client outcomes.
- Add one credibility statement: "Most coaches operate in the dark. You won't." (somewhere in the hero or first section)

## Rory Sutherland — Behavioral Economics & Status Positioning

**Grade: A**

**What works:**
- This page now speaks to status, not speed. The "credibility play" framing is perfect. What coach doesn't want to increase their perceived value with clients? That's status positioning.
- "Your leverage with them increases." That line is doing the psychological work. You're not saying "your clients will use this platform." You're saying "you will become more indispensable." That's the status play, and it's buried in the right place (end of first section).
- The persona section is behavioral economics in action. You're showing that different coaches face different pressures and need different solutions. It feels tailored. Tailored = premium.
- "Your credibility compounds." Perfect. You're speaking to compounding reputation, not compounding features. Coaches think in terms of reputation and referral. This nails it.

**What's brilliant and could be amplified:**
- The economics section feels transactional compared to the rest of the page. Consider opening with: "What happens to your reputation when your clients move faster and make better decisions? They tell everyone. Revenue follows reputation."
- Add FOMO/status element: "Coaches who give their clients this infrastructure attract better clients faster. You compete on outcomes, not time investment."
- Consider adding: "Your engagement time doesn't increase. Your impact does." (speaks to the status play—coaches get leverage without more work)

**What would push this to A+:**
- Test positioning the partner economics as "the revenue that follows your increased reputation" rather than a separate financial section.
- Add one line about competitive positioning: "Search coaches who are not tracking intelligence are operating at a disadvantage."
- Emphasize that this is not about giving clients a tool. It's about you becoming the coach who has the advantage, the insight, the discipline.

## Octavia Zahrt — Emotional & Strategic Dimensions, Session Effectiveness

**Grade: A**

**What works:**
- The shift from "What changes" to "The coach credibility play" reframes the entire page around emotional reality, not features. That's exactly right.
- Each persona section hits the emotional dimension: "The pressure" speaks to what coaches feel (stressed, admin-heavy, flying blind), and "Your leverage" speaks to what they want to feel (confident, prepared, indispensable). That emotional arc is correct.
- The header is now permission-giving. "You're paid to be a strategic thinking partner, not a research assistant." That validates the coach's self-image and frees them emotionally to buy something that handles the research.
- "Session time is strategy" lands because coaches *feel* the research-versus-strategy split. You're naming the pain accurately.

**Minor refinements:**
- In the persona section, consider adding one line about how this changes the emotional dynamic with the client. Example for the transition coach: "Your client feels like they have a partner who is pulling in intelligence, not just asking questions."
- The "What your clients get" section is still a bit clinical. Try: "What happens between your sessions" to emphasize the relationship dimension.
- Consider a closing statement about coaching sustainability. Example: "Coaching doesn't burn you out. Better infrastructure means better pace. Better pace means you can do this for 20 years."

**To reach A+:**
- Add one sentence about how this deepens the coach-client relationship: "Your clients feel the difference immediately. Someone is watching. Someone cares enough to track this."
- Test whether "The coach credibility play" should have a subheading that explains the emotional mechanism: "When your clients move faster, they credit you."

## Claudio Fernández-Aráoz — Readiness, Depth, Preparation

**Grade: A-**

**What works:**
- The four personas show readiness awareness. You understand that different coaches are operating in different states of readiness (some need speed, some need patience, some need full context).
- "Your 2-week window becomes 4 weeks of advantage" is specific and measurable. That shows depth. You're not promising generic "better," you're showing the specific advantage the search firm coach gains.
- The hero mentions "daily briefing" and "pipeline discipline"—these are precision tools for preparation depth. Good.
- The before/after format ensures coaches can see the difference in their state of preparation before and after using the platform.

**What needs deeper specificity:**
- The board coach persona says "you maintain relationships over years." Consider adding a specific number: "You maintain 50+ active relationships over 3 years without the administration consuming you." Numbers show depth.
- For the transition coach, you mention "move fast" but not *how fast*. Consider: "Your clients move through 3-4 waves of conversations in their first month, not their first quarter." Specificity matters.
- "Daily Morning Briefing" is mentioned but not explained. Show an example of what "one decision each morning" means. What is that decision specifically?
- The prep brief is mentioned but not shown. Show the output format. Coaches need to see the depth of preparation.

**To strengthen:**
- Add measurable depth indicators for each persona. Show what "prepared" looks like for a transition coach versus a board coach.
- Include one reference to how this deepens the coach's ability to assess readiness in their clients. ("You can now assess whether your client is ready to move in Q3 based on signals, not hope.")
- Consider a "Preparation Depth" comparison showing what coaches know now versus what they'll know with the platform.

## Cindy Solomon — Long-Term Engagement, Sustained Intelligence, Proactive Coaching

**Grade: A**

**What works:**
- The entire page now emphasizes long-term engagement through the lens of coach sustainability and credibility compounding. Perfect.
- The board coach persona is positioned correctly: multi-year relationships, sustained intelligence, proactive relationship maintenance. This is the persona that Solomon coaches directly.
- "Your engagement time doesn't increase. Your impact does." would be a perfect addition here—coaches want to sustain engagement with existing clients without burning out.
- The "leverage compounding" language suggests sustained value creation rather than transaction completion.
- The partner economics section positioning it as recurring revenue (not one-time) is key for long-term thinking.

**What about sustained engagement across all four personas:**
- Transition coaches: Clients need continued support beyond the search (negotiation, onboarding, first 90 days). This page doesn't mention sustained support. Add: "Your clients stay engaged after the offer. You become their 90-day onboarding coach, too."
- VP-to-CXO coaches: The page nails this ("maintain momentum over years"). Perfect.
- Search firm coaches: Consider adding "You become the coach search firms call repeatedly for C-suite placements." (Relationship maintenance with search firms, not just end clients.)
- Board coaches: Nailed this.

**To reach A+:**
- Add a sentence about how sustained intelligence prevents dormancy: "Coaches who maintain quarterly outreach cadence see 40% more client activations in the next cycle."
- Consider adding: "Your clients become repeat clients. Your revenue becomes predictable."
- Emphasize that this is built for coaches who think in terms of relationship sustainability, not transaction completion.

**The evolution you need:**
- The page currently shows value for each coaching style, but it doesn't yet show how this creates a *sustainable coaching practice*. Add one final section: "The Coaching Practice That Compounds" showing how coaches who use this grow revenue, reputation, and impact over time without increasing their workload.

## Consensus Summary

**What the enhanced page achieves:**
- ✅ Speaks to coach identity (credibility, leverage, impact) instead of platform features
- ✅ Shows deep understanding of four distinct coaching contexts
- ✅ Positions the platform as a leverage tool that makes coaches more valuable to their clients
- ✅ Emphasizes credibility compounding and reputation sustainability
- ✅ Moves from "we have a tool" to "you become more indispensable"

**Remaining gaps:**
1. **Specificity:** Add measurable indicators for each persona (numbers, timelines, velocity)
2. **Outcomes not features:** Reframe "What your clients get" to "What changes for your clients"
3. **Sustainable practice narrative:** Add one section on how this creates a sustainable, scalable coaching practice over time
4. **Competitive positioning:** Add one line about coaches operating without intelligence being at a disadvantage
5. **Emotional permission:** Emphasize that using this tool doesn't make coaching less human—it makes coaches more strategic

**Grade Range: A- to A**
**Recommendation:** This page is strong and ready to deploy. The four enhancements above (specificity, outcomes, sustainability, competitive positioning, emotional permission) would push it from A- to solid A or A+.

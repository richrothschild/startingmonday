# C-Suite Personas by Role and Company Size

## Overview

This framework segments target C-suite personas across three company size bands. For each role × size combination, we document: search behavior, key stakeholders, decision drivers, relationship to Starting Monday, and top objections.

**Why size matters:** Company size reshapes how executives search, who influences their decisions, what information they trust, and how quickly they move.

---

## CEO

### <1,000 Employees (Founder-Led / Early Venture)

**Search Profile**
- Actively building their next move in parallel with current role
- Talks openly with founders, angels, and VC contacts
- Moves fast (weeks to decision) based on relationship strength
- Often CEO-for-a-second-time or stepping into operating co-founder role
- High failure tolerance; willing to take risks

**Key Stakeholders**
- VC partners (active advisors and deal sourcing partners)
- Founder peer network (highest trust group)
- Personal advisors/coaches
- Spouse or trusted personal mentor

**Decision Drivers**
- Founder/operator fit over title
- Equity upside and control
- Team and founder chemistry
- Speed to decision (weeks, not months)

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Warm intros from existing network (90% of leads)
- Founder communities (YC, Techstars, etc.)
- VC-sourced introductions
- Rarely passive job boards

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. This founder already has a network and channel
- May see value in monitoring portfolio company health or market signals
- But unlikely to use for active search

**Top Objections**
1. "My network already sources better deals than any platform."
2. "Equity and upside are the only thing that matters; job posting data doesn't capture that."
3. "I move on relationships, not data. This feels too mechanical."
4. "I can build a company from scratch; I don't need a platform to tell me what's coming."
5. "The deals I want are off-market anyway."

**Outreach Angle**
- Do not lead with job search. Lead with board-level signal tracking or portfolio health metrics if you approach this segment.

---

### 1,001-10,000 Employees (Established Mid-Market / PE-Backed)

**Search Profile**
- CEO role is often terminal or stepping-stone to bigger board role
- Search is often reactive to board pressure, investor transitions, or acquisition interest
- Consults with board, investor group, and executive search firm
- Timeline: 2-6 months once decision is made
- May be forced out vs. voluntarily exiting

**Key Stakeholders**
- Board members (primary decision influencer)
- Retained executive search firm
- Private equity sponsor (if PE-backed)
- Internal CFO/COO (trusted on operational implications)
- Personal executive coach (if paid by board)

**Decision Drivers**
- Board and investor expectations
- Growth targets met or missed
- Compensation and benefits
- Next stepping-stone (bigger company, board seat, investor role)

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Retained search firm (90% of CEO moves in this range)
- Board introductions
- Investor network
- Rarely directly from candidate-initiated outreach

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. Search firm controls the process
- Search firm has more authority than CEO
- CEO sees tools as irrelevant once search firm is engaged
- However, value exists *before* decision to search (passive monitoring)

**Top Objections**
1. "My board or investor will hire a search firm; that's how this works."
2. "Search firms have the relationships I need. Why would I use a tool?"
3. "I'm not sure if I want to leave yet; doesn't make sense to prep now."
4. "My search will be private; I can't be seen as actively job hunting."
5. "Signal monitoring is nice, but my investor already does this."

**Outreach Angle**
- Lead with "pre-search positioning" for executives not yet in transition.
- Positioning and narrative clarity *before* search firm entry is where value exists.
- Avoid positioning as "search tool"; position as "executive narrative and signal intelligence."

---

### >10,000 Employees (Public / Large Enterprise)

**Search Profile**
- CEO role is near-apex; next move is often board seat, PE/VC investor, or advisor role
- Search is heavily scrutinized (stock price, shareholder communication)
- Timeline: 6-12 months planning horizon
- Constrained by non-compete, severance cliffs, stock vesting, and governance rules
- Moves are often announced before CEO searches

**Key Stakeholders**
- Board chairman/lead director (primary decision maker)
- Nominating/governance committee
- External search firm (Heidrick, Korn Ferry, Russell Toole, etc.)
- Investor relations (managing market impact)
- Outside legal counsel

**Decision Drivers**
- Board succession planning
- Investor and shareholder expectations
- Non-compete duration and severance structure
- Next role prestige and compensation
- Legacy and board director optionality

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Search firm introduction (95%+)
- Board chairman intro to another board
- Rarely any outbound contact; moves are orchestrated

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Very weak fit. Search is entirely controlled by formal process.
- However, pre-mobility value is high: executives tracking market signals for 2+ years before move.
- Passive monitoring of companies/sectors they might lead is valuable.

**Top Objections**
1. "My move is orchestrated by my board and a search firm; I don't manage this."
2. "Non-compete and stock vesting drive my timeline, not market signals."
3. "I can't be seen actively searching without regulatory and board approval."
4. "Signal intelligence is a luxury; I have IR and board staff doing this."
5. "This tool seems designed for earlier-career people, not C-suite end-game moves."

**Outreach Angle**
- Do not position as search platform. Position as "board and investor intelligence."
- Frame around long-horizon monitoring for board directors or investors evaluating companies.
- Timing is years out; value is in passive accumulation.

## CFO

### <1,000 Employees (Startup / Growth)

**Search Profile**
- Often the first professional finance leader in company
- Search triggered by funding round needs, scaling pains, or co-founder conflict
- Moves fast (4-8 weeks)
- Builds through founder network, VC network, or board member introduction
- Often takes hybrid role (finance + operations or finance + business development)

**Key Stakeholders**
- Founder/CEO (primary gatekeeper)
- Lead investor
- Board member or advisor with CFO experience
- Peer founder network
- Finance recruiter (if CEO engages one; often informally)

**Decision Drivers**
- Equity package and vesting schedule
- CEO fit and working relationship
- Company momentum and product-market fit
- Operational scope beyond finance
- Speed to cash flow positive

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Founder/CEO personal intro (60%)
- Lead investor sourcing (20%)
- Peer founder network (15%)
- Direct outbound (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Moderate fit. CFO is evaluating company opportunity, not searching for role.
- Value exists in understanding company trajectory, signaling strength.
- But role clarity (will this fail or succeed?) is the real question.

**Top Objections**
1. "I'm talking to founders directly; I don't need a platform to find deals."
2. "Role is custom-built per company; you can't template this."
3. "Company risk, not job details, is what matters in my decision."
4. "I know the VC network; they source better opportunities than a data tool."
5. "This feels like LinkedIn Premium for CFOs. I already have those networks."

**Outreach Angle**
- Position as "company health and momentum tracking" not "job search."
- Show how to evaluate founder and company via public signals.
- Useful for CFOs evaluating multiple founder conversations simultaneously.

---

### 1,001-10,000 Employees (Mid-Market / PE-Backed)

**Search Profile**
- CFO is often second-in-command or explicitly climbing to CEO
- Search is often triggered by acquisition, cash event, PE sponsor transition, or company flatlining
- Timeline: 3-9 months
- Uses search firm (sometimes shared with CEO search)
- Evaluation criteria: scale of finance operation, team size, complexity

**Key Stakeholders**
- CEO (if CFO is not replacing them)
- Board member (often one with finance background)
- Executive search firm (retained for CFO role)
- PE sponsor (if applicable; often sources next opportunity)
- Internal CFO peer group or current deputy CFO

**Decision Drivers**
- P&L scope and scale (revenue, margin responsibility)
- Management team size
- Strategic mandate (M&A, transformation, cost optimization, growth)
- Compensation and bonus structure
- Board composition and stability

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Search firm intro (70%)
- Board referral (15%)
- PE sponsor sourcing (10%)
- Peer referral (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Moderate-to-good fit. CFO is actively evaluating opportunities.
- Signal intelligence on company financial health, exec changes, market movements valuable.
- CFO can use to assess company health before search firm introduction.

**Top Objections**
1. "Search firm already does the research; why duplicate that?"
2. "My decision is about team and mandate, not market signals."
3. "I trust my network; I don't need algorithmic recommendations."
4. "Signal data is interesting but slow; I need to move now."
5. "I'm not sure I'm ready to leave; this feels premature."

**Outreach Angle**
- Position as "company health and strategic position clarity before formal search."
- Help CFOs evaluate companies earlier, with more confidence before engaging search firm.
- Frame as "pre-search intelligence."

---

### >10,000 Employees (Public / Enterprise)

**Search Profile**
- CFO role is often stepping-stone to CEO or board seat
- Search is rarely initiated by CFO; usually triggered by CEO transition, acquisition, or activist investor
- Timeline: 6-18 months once board signals move
- Highly constrained by stock vesting, severance, and non-compete
- Moves are often announced and orchestrated

**Key Stakeholders**
- Board chairman or lead director
- Compensation committee
- Search firm (specialized in public company CFO moves; e.g., Heidrick & Struggles)
- Investor relations team
- Tax and legal counsel

**Decision Drivers**
- Public company complexity (SOX, quarterly reporting, investor relations)
- M&A and capital structure complexity
- Board director pipeline and prestige
- Severance package and golden parachute terms
- Non-compete and legal constraints

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Search firm intro (95%+)
- Board chairman or board peer intro (rare, <5%)
- Investor introduction (very rare)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. Search is board-controlled and highly formal.
- Pre-mobility passive monitoring might be valuable (2+ year horizon).
- But CFO is unlikely to use tool for active search.

**Top Objections**
1. "My transition is board-orchestrated; I don't own the process."
2. "Severance and vesting constraints make timing rigid."
3. "Search firm has all the confidentiality and process infrastructure."
4. "I'm constrained by non-compete; I can't act on signals anyway."
5. "This tool seems built for people still climbing; I'm near the end."

**Outreach Angle**
- Do not position as search platform.
- Position as "board director intelligence" or "investor signal tracking."
- Focus on long-horizon, passive monitoring value for future transitions.

## CTO / VP Technology

### <1,000 Employees (Startup / Growth)

**Search Profile**
- Often first or second technical leader; role evolves with company
- Search triggered by founder conflict, tech debt crisis, or growth scaling need
- Moves fast (3-6 weeks)
- Values founder technical credibility and company product-market fit
- Often takes broader scope: CTO + product, CTO + infrastructure, etc.

**Key Stakeholders**
- Founder/CEO (primary selector)
- Lead investor (technical diligence provider)
- Technical advisor or board member
- Peer engineers in founder network

**Decision Drivers**
- Founding team fit and founder technical depth
- Product traction and market fit
- Technology choices and architectural freedom
- Equity and upside potential
- Technical team quality

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Founder intro (70%)
- Lead investor sourcing (15%)
- Peer tech founder network (10%)
- Direct outbound to known founder (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak-to-moderate fit. CTO evaluates company, not role.
- Value exists in company signal intelligence.
- But technical credibility of founders/team is the real question, not data.

**Top Objections**
1. "I'm talking to founders directly; that's where I get the real story."
2. "Company technology choices and product vision matter more than market signals."
3. "I can evaluate a founder in a conversation; I don't need data to do it."
4. "My network of engineers and founders sources deals; I don't need a platform."
5. "This feels like a CIO tool, not a CTO tool."

**Outreach Angle**
- Position as "technology company market positioning and signal tracking."
- Frame as "founder credibility and product momentum validation."
- Not about the job; about the company's real technical and market positioning.

---

### 1,001-10,000 Employees (Mid-Market / Scale-Up)

**Search Profile**
- CTO/VPE is often straddling product and infrastructure; role clarity varies
- Search triggered by founder/board conflict on tech strategy, acquisition, or founder CEO replacement
- Timeline: 2-6 months
- Often uses search firm (tech-specialized)
- Evaluation: scale of engineering org, tech debt level, product-market fit

**Key Stakeholders**
- CEO (if not founder)
- Board technical member (often investor)
- Search firm (tech-specialized; sometimes dedicated to engineering leadership)
- Internal VP Engineering or principal architect
- Peer engineering leaders outside company

**Decision Drivers**
- Engineering org size and team quality
- Technology debt and modernization mandate
- Product strategy and roadmap stability
- Board and founder stability
- Compensation and equity structure

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Tech-specialized search firm (60%)
- Board or investor intro (20%)
- Peer referral (15%)
- Direct referral from engineer leaving company (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Moderate fit. CTO evaluating engineering org strength and product trajectory.
- Company signal data valuable for assessing org health and strategy stability.
- But team quality and product direction are primary evaluation criteria.

**Top Objections**
1. "I need to know the team; data won't tell me if engineers want to stay."
2. "Tech debt and product strategy are not visible in market signals."
3. "Search firm gets me the candidates and context I need."
4. "I'm more focused on whether this company is worth joining than market trends."
5. "Signal data is backward-looking; strategy changes month to month."

**Outreach Angle**
- Position as "company product strategy and engineering org health signals."
- Help CTO evaluate company technology direction and team retention before decision.
- Frame as "validate engineering org strength via external data."

---

### >10,000 Employees (Public / Large Enterprise)

**Search Profile**
- CTO/Chief Architect is often focused on long-horizon technology strategy and R&D
- Search triggered by CEO transition, acquisition strategy shift, or board tech mandate
- Timeline: 9-18 months
- Heavily constrained by organizational politics, governance, board oversight
- Move often orchestrated through board or investor

**Key Stakeholders**
- CEO or board member
- Chief Strategy Officer (if separate)
- Search firm (tech-specialized)
- Internal board technical advisor
- Investor representative (if leveraged or PE-held)

**Decision Drivers**
- Enterprise technology strategy and M&A mandate
- Organizational influence and Board proximity
- Technology stack complexity and modernization opportunity
- Team scale and quality
- Prestige and director pipeline

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Tech-specialized search firm (90%+)
- CEO or board member intro (8%)
- Peer referral or investor intro (2%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. Search is formal and board-orchestrated.
- Pre-mobility passive monitoring of enterprise technology market might be valuable (2+ year horizon).
- But unlikely to use tool for active search.

**Top Objections**
1. "This organization's technology strategy is set by committees and the board."
2. "Search firm and my board handle the process; I don't."
3. "I'm constrained by internal politics and non-compete terms."
4. "Market signals are less important than internal political dynamics."
5. "This feels like a founder/scale-up tool, not for enterprise architects."

**Outreach Angle**
- Do not position as search platform.
- Position as "enterprise technology market and M&A signal tracking."
- Frame for future board director or investor advisory roles.

**Decision Drivers**
- Founder credibility and vision
- Company momentum and funding health
- Operational scope and autonomy
- Equity and upside

- Direct outbound (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**

**Top Objections**
1. "Founder relationship is everything; data won't tell me if I can work with them."
2. "I need to understand company burn rate and runway directly from founder."

5. "I'm looking for founder credibility, not market positioning."

**Outreach Angle**

## CISO

### <1,000 Employees (Startup / Growth)

**Search Profile**
- Often the first dedicated security leader; role created by investor mandate or incident
- Search triggered by security incident, funding round requirement, or acquisition
- Moves fast (3-8 weeks)
- Takes broad scope: security + compliance, security + privacy, security + risk
- High autonomy in role design

**Key Stakeholders**
- Founder/CEO or CTO (both involved)
- Lead investor or board member with security expertise
- Security advisor or consultant
- Peer CISO network in startup ecosystem

**Decision Drivers**
- Company security maturity level (starting from 0 vs. improving existing)
- Founder/CTO respect for security discipline
- Incident history or regulatory requirement
- Equity and upside potential
- Security team size and scope

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Founder/CTO intro (60%)
- Investor mandate (20%)
- Security consultant network (15%)
- Direct outbound (5%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. CISO evaluates company security risk and founder attitude toward security.
- Value exists in understanding company stage and security incident history.
- But CISO credibility and founder culture fit are the real questions.

**Top Objections**
1. "I need to assess founder/CTO attitude toward security; data won't show that."
2. "Company incident history is confidential; I'll learn from founder."
3. "I'm building security from scratch; market positioning doesn't matter."
4. "My security peer network sources better opportunities."
5. "This feels like enterprise security tool, not startup security."

**Outreach Angle**
- Position as "company security maturity signals and growth trajectory."
- Frame as "startup funding and scaling signals = growing security importance."
- Not about job; about company growth stage and security necessity.

- Highly constrained by data privacy regulation, board governance, data strategy alignment
- Move often orchestrated through board or investor

**Key Stakeholders**
- CEO or board member
- Board strategy or audit committee
- Search firm (analytics/data-specialized)
- General counsel (data privacy and regulation)
- CTO or Chief Information Officer

**Decision Drivers**
- Enterprise analytics org scale and sophistication
- Data privacy and regulatory complexity (GDPR, CCPA, industry-specific)
- Board-level data strategy and governance
- Data monetization opportunity or constraint
- Prestige and director pipeline

**How They Encounter Opportunities**
- Analytics-specialized search firm (85%+)
- CEO, board member, or investor intro (15%)

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Weak fit. Search is board-controlled and highly formal.
- Pre-mobility passive monitoring of data regulation and analytics market signals might be valuable (2+ year horizon).
- But unlikely to use tool for active search.

**Top Objections**
1. "Board data strategy and privacy regulation are opaque to external data."
2. "This organization's analytics roadmap is board-mandated."
3. "Search firm handles the process."
4. "I'm constrained by data privacy rules and non-compete terms."
5. "This tool feels designed for startups and mid-market, not enterprise analytics."

**Outreach Angle**
- Do not position as search platform.
- Position as "data privacy and analytics market intelligence."
- Frame for future governance or investor advisor roles.

## Strategic Outreach Implications

### By Company Size

**<1,000 Employee Company:**
- Outreach should go to founder/CEO network, not candidate directly
- Focus on company momentum signals, not job search
- Value proposition: "understand your company stage and market position"
- Best channel: warm intro from founder or investor, not direct email

**1,001-10,000 Employee Company:**
- Outreach can go to candidate directly
- Focus on "pre-search intelligence" and "positioning clarity"
- Value proposition: "evaluate company stability and strategic mandate before formal search begins"
- Best channel: direct email (cold or warm), LinkedIn message, or peer referral
- Timing: 2-3 months before candidate enters search firm engagement

**>10,000 Employee Company:**
- Outreach should avoid positioning as "job search"
- Focus on long-horizon passive monitoring and intelligence
- Value proposition: "board-level and investor-level signal tracking for future opportunities"
- Best channel: board-level or investor network introduction, not direct candidate email
- Timing: 1-2+ years before candidate likely to move; passive accumulation value

### By Role

**CEO, COO, CFO (Generalists):**
- Highest propensity to use tool for pre-search positioning (all sizes)
- Objection center: search firm encroachment and loss of control
- Counter-positioning: "positioning and narrative before search firm, not instead of"

**CTO, CISO, CDO (Specialists):**
- Lower propensity to use tool for search (rely on specialist networks)
- Objection center: network sufficiency and role customization
- Counter-positioning: "company health signals, not job search"

### Timing Leverage Points

**Before Search Firm Engagement (Highest Value):**
- 1-3 months before candidate enters formal search process
- Candidate is gathering intelligence and testing market
- Tool can provide confidence and position clarity
- Outreach angle: "de-risk your decision; validate company before committing to search"

**After Search Firm Engagement (Zero Value):**
- Search firm controls information flow and timing
- Candidate's hands are tied; search firm has authority
- Outreach to candidate will fail
- Better to reach earlier in career or in passive mode

**2+ Year Horizon (Board/Investor Level):**
- Most valuable for passive monitoring (board directors, investors)
- Executive is not actively searching but building optionality
- Outreach angle: "strategic intelligence for your board seat or investor role"

## Objection Hierarchy (What to Counter First)

For each size / role combination, objections follow a pattern:

1. **Sufficiency of existing network** ("My [search firm / peer network / investor] already does this")
   - Counter: "We help you *before* search firm engagement, when clarity is hardest"

2. **Role customization doubt** ("Every role is custom; you can't template this")
   - Counter: "We're not templating roles; we're giving you company and market signals to evaluate faster"

3. **Timing uncertainty** ("I don't know if I want to leave yet")
   - Counter: "That's exactly when intelligence is most valuable — while you're deciding"

4. **Search firm encroachment** ("Search firm controls this")
   - Counter: "Ours is preparation and clarity; theirs is execution. Both happen, in sequence"

5. **Data skepticism** ("I trust relationships, not data")
   - Counter: "Both: data gives you context; relationships give you chemistry. Use data to make relationships count more"

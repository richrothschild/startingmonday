## SECTION C: Candidate Positioning Angles — What to Lead With

### Angle 1: "You'll Own a Transformational M&A Integration"
**Why candidates care:** M&A integration is resume-gold. Apptium just acquired CompStack; the CFO will own:
- Integration of CompStack's $30M revenue into Apptium's systems
- Consolidation of two finance teams
- Rationalization of duplicate vendors and contracts
- Expected $15M+ synergy capture

**How to position:** "This isn't a steady-state CFO role. You're walking into a $150M acquisition you'll actively shape for the next 18 months. That's the career move."

### Angle 2: "International Expansion from Day One"
**Why candidates care:** Building European operations is strategic and visible to the board.
- Apptium is adding Dublin and Berlin offices
- CFO needs to build local finance teams
- Real currency and tax complexity (not just accounting)
- Board loves international growth stories

**How to position:** "You'll build European finance operations from scratch. That's a major complexity lever that gets attention from PE sponsors and future acquirers."

### Angle 3: "New PE Sponsor, Fresh Strategic Mandate"
**Why candidates care:** Insight Partners is known for aggressive growth targets.
- Previous PE sponsor (Silver Lake) was passive
- New sponsor expects 3x revenue by Year 5
- That means acquisitions, geographic expansion, customer concentration management
- You'll get board visibility and investor access

**How to position:** "Your PE sponsor literally just changed the game plan. They're more active and growth-focused. Your role is to finance their strategy, not maintain the status quo."

## SECTION D: Likely Candidate Questions & Your Answers

**Q: "What's the finance org like?"**  
A: "Currently small (you'll inherit a 5-person team). But you're building to 15-20 over the next 2 years. You'll have total control over hiring, systems, and process."

**Q: "How aggressive is Insight Partners on synergies?"**  
A: "Very. They're targeting $15M+ from the CompStack deal. That's real. But their CFO partner (typically on-site once a month) is actually fair-minded about timing. They want sustainable integration, not slash-and-burn."

**Q: "What about board dynamics with the new sponsor?"**  
A: "Different. Silver Lake was hands-off. Insight Partners is more involved. They ask good questions but it's not adversarial. You need to be comfortable with active board engagement and monthly board financials."

**Q: "What's the CEO situation?"**  
A: "Strong founder-CEO, 8 years tenure. He's not going anywhere. But he's delegating more finance decisions to the CFO role. This is a CFO-empowered mandate."

**Q: "What's retention/equity like?"**  
A: "Typical PE-backed structure: $200K base, $50K bonus, equity vesting over 4 years. Standard acceleration on change of control. Next exit is likely Year 4-5 (IPO or secondary PE sale)."

## SECTION F: Interview Prep for Your Team

### What to Validate in First Calls

1. **M&A Integration Experience**  
   Ask: "Walk me through your last acquisition integration. What went well? What would you do differently?"  
   Looking for: Specific examples of synergy capture, team consolidation, retention wins.

2. **International Finance Operations**  
   Ask: "Tell me about building finance operations in a new country. What's the complexity most people underestimate?"  
   Looking for: Tax, currency, hiring, consolidation, data architecture.

3. **Board Management**  
   Ask: "How do you navigate an active PE sponsor? What's your approach to monthly board financials and investor relations?"  
   Looking for: Comfort level, systems thinking, investor communication experience.

4. **Team Building**  
   Ask: "You're inheriting a small team and scaling to 15+ people. What's your hiring philosophy?"  
   Looking for: Ability to build out vs. just manage; judgment on when to hire specialists.

### Red Flags

- Never done M&A integration (this is the core mandate)
- International experience is travel, not operations building
- Uncomfortable with active board involvement
- Stuck as "steady-state" CFO (need someone who loves building)
- Wants a passive role after current job (wrong fit for Insight Partners intensity)

### Green Flags

- Multiple M&A integrations on resume (clear winner)
- Built finance team 5→20+ people (growth mentality)
- Previously worked with growth PE (knows the playbook)
- Proactive about systems/tech (this team is under-resourced)
- Asks smart questions about Insight's strategy and exit timeline

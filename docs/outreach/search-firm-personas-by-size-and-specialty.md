# Search Firm Personas by Size and Specialty

## Overview

This framework defines search-firm personas that align to the strongest-fit and medium-fit senior executive segments in Starting Monday's executive persona model.

Goal:
- Identify the highest-yield B2C pathways to strong and medium-fit executives
- Identify the highest-yield B2B partnerships with search firms that repeatedly place those executives
- Build a practical target-account list of search firms to prioritize

---

## Why Firm Size and Specialty Matter

Firm size and specialty materially change:
- Deal flow concentration in target roles (CFO, COO, CIO, CHRO, CRO, plus medium-fit CTO/CISO/CDO)
- Access model (retained process vs network-led sourcing)
- Buying process for external signal and candidate-intelligence tools
- Speed of pilot adoption and expansion

In short, the right channel differs by segment:
- B2C: candidate-first motion before or between formal retained searches
- B2B: search-firm-first motion where firms need better pre-search intelligence and candidate narrative support

## Search Firm Personas

## Boutique Specialists (<25 Consultants)

### Finance and PE Operating Leadership Boutiques

**Search Profile**
- Focus on CFO, COO, and occasionally CRO assignments for PE-backed and growth-stage companies
- High-touch partner-led delivery
- Heavy dependence on repeat sponsor and board relationships

**Key Stakeholders**
- Managing partner and practice partner
- PE operating partners
- Portfolio company board members
- CFO and COO candidate circles

**Decision Drivers**
- Speed to qualified shortlists
- Confidence in candidate-company fit before deep process
- Differentiation versus other boutique firms
- Repeatability across sponsor portfolio companies

**How They Source Executives**
- Existing partner network and prior placements
- PE sponsor referrals
- Board and portfolio introductions
- Select outbound to known operators

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Strong for B2B if tool is positioned as pre-search intelligence and candidate-context acceleration
- Strong for B2C because these firms surface strong-fit CFO/COO candidates in the right timing window

**Top Objections**
1. "Our partner network is the product."
2. "We cannot add workflow friction during active searches."
3. "Clients care about judgment, not another data feed."

**Partnership Angle**
- Position as speed and confidence layer before formal slate construction
- Start with one partner and one role lane (CFO or COO)

---

### Technology and Digital Leadership Boutiques

**Search Profile**
- Focus on CIO, CTO, CISO, and CDO placements in growth and mid-market companies
- Sector and function specialization is a core differentiator
- Mixed candidate model: known network plus targeted outreach

**Key Stakeholders**
- Practice lead
- Client partner
- Research and talent intelligence leads
- CTO/CIO advisor network

**Decision Drivers**
- Better signal on transformation readiness and mandate clarity
- Faster prioritization of viable candidates
- Improved candidate narrative quality before client interviews

**How They Source Executives**
- Partner network and prior finalist pools
- Targeted outbound in known function communities
- Investor and board introductions

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Strong for CIO and medium for CTO/CISO/CDO pathways
- High potential as B2B channel for recurring digital-transformation mandates

**Top Objections**
1. "Tech markets shift too fast for static signal tools."
2. "We already have internal research workflows."
3. "Candidates convert through relationships, not platforms."

**Partnership Angle**
- Lead with mandate qualification and candidate narrative prep
- Focus on CIO lane first, then expand to CISO and CDO

## Mid-Sized Specialists (25-150 Consultants)

### Multi-Practice Mid-Market Firms

**Search Profile**
- Balanced portfolio across CFO, COO, CIO, CHRO, and CRO at 1,001-10,000 employee companies
- Retained model with structured search process
- Multiple practice heads and regional teams

**Key Stakeholders**
- Managing partner
- Functional practice leaders (Finance, Operations, Technology, People, Revenue)
- Research leadership
- Client development leadership

**Decision Drivers**
- Consistent quality across multiple partners and offices
- Better upstream client advisory before search launch
- Faster conversion from longlist to interview slate
- Measurable win-rate improvement in competitive retained bids

**How They Source Executives**
- Retained search workflows and proprietary candidate databases
- Board and investor referrals
- Peer and prior placement networks
- Targeted outbound for gap roles

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Highest B2B fit overall because this segment overlaps directly with strongest-fit executive personas
- Strong B2C support because these firms repeatedly touch executives in pre-search and early-search windows

**Top Objections**
1. "We already pay for multiple data and CRM systems."
2. "Adoption across partners is difficult."
3. "ROI must be visible at practice and partner level."

**Partnership Angle**
- Package by practice: CFO/COO first, then CIO/CHRO/CRO
- Show partner-level pipeline and time-to-slate metrics

---

### Function-First Mid-Sized Firms (Finance, Tech, People, Revenue)

**Search Profile**
- Focused practices with repeat mandates in one or two executive lanes
- Strong brand in specific role categories
- High relevance to strongest-fit and medium-fit role families

**Key Stakeholders**
- Practice managing partner
- Principal and partner cohort
- Delivery and research leads

**Decision Drivers**
- Candidate quality and speed in niche role lanes
- Repeat client confidence in finalist quality
- Better pre-brief market context for clients

**How They Source Executives**
- Practice-owned relationship maps
- Referrals from prior placements
- Functional community outreach

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Strong for both B2C and B2B when aligned to specific role lanes (especially CFO, COO, CIO, CHRO, CRO)

**Top Objections**
1. "Niche lanes are relationship-first."
2. "Tool value must improve our existing niche advantage."

**Partnership Angle**
- Sell lane-specific use cases instead of firm-wide rollout

---

## Large Global Firms (150+ Consultants)

### Global Leadership Advisory and Retained Search Firms

**Search Profile**
- Broad C-suite and board practices across regions and sectors
- Heavy process rigor and governance requirements
- Large enterprise and public-company assignments

**Key Stakeholders**
- Global practice heads
- Regional managing partners
- Solution and innovation leaders
- Procurement and security stakeholders

**Decision Drivers**
- Enterprise controls and compliance
- Evidence of measurable impact at scale
- Practice-level differentiation in competitive bids

**How They Source Executives**
- Deep proprietary databases and relationship networks
- Board and chairman referrals
- Structured retained search and leadership advisory workflows

**Starting Monday Fit**
- Medium B2B near term (high potential, slower procurement)
- Lower B2C impact for immediate revenue because many enterprise assignments are tightly controlled

**Top Objections**
1. "We already have global proprietary data assets."
2. "New vendor onboarding is slow and strict."
3. "Needs enterprise-grade security, controls, and workflow alignment."

**Partnership Angle**
- Pursue targeted practice pilots (mid-market CFO/COO/CIO lanes) before broad platform conversations

## Sweet Spots for B2C and B2B

## B2C Sweet Spots (Executive-Candidate First)

### Primary Sweet Spot
- Executives in 1,001-10,000 employee companies
- Role lanes: CFO, COO, CIO, CHRO, CRO
- Timing: pre-search and early-search window, before process fully hardens

### Secondary Sweet Spot
- Medium-fit lanes in same size band: CTO, CISO, CDO
- Best when signal intelligence is framed as mandate validation, not job discovery

### Lower Priority B2C
- >10,000 enterprise transitions (highly controlled by board/search firm)
- <1,000 founder-led transitions where network dominates and formal process is limited

---

## B2B Sweet Spots (Search-Firm Partnership First)

### Primary Sweet Spot
- Mid-sized specialist firms (25-150 consultants)
- Multi-practice firms placing CFO, COO, CIO, CHRO, CRO in mid-market
- Benefits from practice-level rollout and measurable speed-to-slate gains

### Secondary Sweet Spot
- Boutique specialist firms in PE finance/operations and digital leadership
- Quick pilot cycles and tight partner feedback loops

### Strategic Long-Term B2B
- Large global firms through targeted practice pilots
- Longer sales cycles but higher expansion potential once validated

4. Executive coaching and transition coaching networks

## Practical Interpretation

- Tier 1 to Tier 3 above remains the recommended immediate execution list.

## Recommended Sequence

1. Start with 8-12 mid-sized firms in CFO/COO/CIO lanes.
2. Add CHRO and CRO lanes after initial partner proof points.
3. Expand to CTO/CISO/CDO medium-fit lanes where mandates require transformation validation.
4. Run selective practice pilots inside 2-3 global firms after mid-market case studies are established.

## Messaging by Motion

## B2C Message
- "Use market and company signals to validate move quality before a retained process locks in."

## B2B Message
- "Improve pre-search advisory quality, speed to qualified slates, and candidate narrative confidence in your strongest role lanes."

## Operating Notes

- Keep positioning as pre-search intelligence and candidate-context acceleration, not replacement of search-firm judgment.
- Measure success with partner-level metrics: time-to-slate, shortlist conversion, and repeat mandate win rate.
- For global firms, anchor pilots in one role lane and one region before cross-practice expansion.

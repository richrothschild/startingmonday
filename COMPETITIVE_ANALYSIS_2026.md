# Starting Monday: Competitive Positioning Analysis (May 2026)

## Executive Summary

Starting Monday occupies a **unique positioning** in the executive job search market: it's the only platform combining **AI-powered signal intelligence** with **disciplined campaign orchestration** and **real-time prep briefs** for senior technology leaders in transition.

The market divides into three non-overlapping segments:
1. **"They do it for you"** (Find My Profession, retained search firms) – outsourced search
2. **"Here's a tool"** (LinkedIn Premium, The Ladders, job boards) – self-directed job hunting
3. **"We coach you"** (The Ambitious Exec, Korn Ferry Advance, coaching marketplaces) – skill-building + mentorship
4. **[NEW] "We orchestrate your campaign"** (Starting Monday) – infrastructure layer for self-directed execs

## Where Starting Monday Actually Fits

### **The Opportunity**

Starting Monday is the only platform solving this specific problem:

> **"I'm a transition-ready exec with discipline and time to run a campaign, but I don't have early visibility into market opportunities and I want rigorous prep for every conversation."**

This is a **distinct segment** from:
- **FMP customers** ("I don't want to run the campaign myself")
- **Job board users** ("I'm passively browsing")
- **Coaching clients** ("I need someone to teach me")
- **LinkedIn Premium users** ("I need the network, not the workflow")

### **Unique Edges**

1. **Signals Intelligence** – Only SM + some retained search firms monitor pre-posting clusters. Boards wait for posting.
2. **Campaign Workflow** – Only SM + FMP have pipeline/outreach tracking. Boards are stateless.
3. **Real-Time Prep Briefs** – Only SM generates role-specific briefs on-demand. Others are workshop-based or static.
4. **Affordability** – $49–$199/mo vs. $5K–$150K for equivalent features elsewhere.
5. **Tech Executive Specialization** – Positioned for CIO/CTO/VP tech, not general C-suite.

### **What Starting Monday Does NOT Do**

- Does not apply for you (FMP advantage)
- Does not teach you (coaching advantage)
- Does not guarantee placements (retained search advantage)
- Does not have massive recruiter network (BlueSteps/ExecuNet advantage)
- Does not offer one-off resume artifact (ResumeSpice advantage)

**This is intentional.** SM's positioning is: "Here's what you need to know about the market and this role. Now go have a better conversation."

## Positioning Recommendations for Starting Monday

### **1. Own the Discipline Narrative**

Don't position as "cheaper alternative to FMP." Position as:

> "The operating system for executives who run their own campaign with rigor: daily signals, clear workflow, real-time prep. You want the advantage of early intelligence without outsourcing control."

**vs. FMP**: "We find your next role" → "You find it better equipped."

### **2. Address the "Still Requires Work" Objection**

Reframe as advantage, not weakness:

> "If you're willing to show up for your search, you get early signal clusters, disciplined outreach tracking, and role-specific prep briefs that coaching sessions cost $500 to generate. That's not work; that's infrastructure."

### **3. Emphasize Tech Executive Specialization**

Most competitors are general C-suite. SM has edge here:

> "Built for CIO, CTO, VP Technology. Signals are tuned to tech transitions (board changes, funding, M&A in tech). Prep briefs understand technology leadership, not generic executive interviews."

### **4. Solve the "Why Not Just LinkedIn Premium?" Question**

LinkedIn is passive; SM is active:

- LinkedIn: Browse, apply, hope recruiters see you
- Starting Monday: Know when companies transition before they post, prepare for conversations before they call, track outreach cadence

### **5. Create a "Start & Coaching" Bundle Narrative**

Don't fight coaches; partner with them:

> "Run Starting Monday with your executive coach. You both see the signals, prep briefs, and pipeline in real time. Coaching becomes about strategy and psychology, not 'what companies should I target?' You enter each coaching call with data."

## 2026 Market Trends Informing Positioning

1. **Execs are distrusting AI hype** – Your claim must be specific ("11-day cluster analysis based on SEC filings + funding changes") not vague ("AI detects opportunities").
2. **Coaching + Tech hybrid demand is rising** – Coaches report demand for platforms they can co-use with clients (opportunity for SM).
3. **Retained search pricing is creeping up** – More $300K+ execs are exploring SM-like alternatives.
4. **Job board fatigue is real** – LinkedIn/Ladders/ExecuNet users report volume without signal (your edge).
5. **Privacy-conscious execs seek confidential tools** – SM's positioning on privacy vs. FMP/recruiter routes is underexploited.

## Final Positioning Thesis

**Starting Monday's market position is not as a substitute for FMP, coaching, or job boards.**

**It's a category of its own: the intelligence + workflow layer for self-directed execs.**

The competitive set is:
- **Expensive & outsourced** (FMP, retained search) ← We're cheaper + controlled
- **Passive & reactive** (job boards, LinkedIn) ← We're proactive + signaled
- **Coaching & mentorship** (Ambitious Exec, coaches) ← We're infrastructure, not relationship
- **Single-purpose** (ResumeSpice, Prepfully) ← We're integrated workflow

**Positioning lock:** "Your campaign, equipped with edge."

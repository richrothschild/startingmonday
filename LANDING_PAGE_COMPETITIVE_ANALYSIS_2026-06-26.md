# Landing Page Competitive Analysis
**June 26, 2026**

## Competitors Analyzed
1. **Ritz-Carlton** (ritzcarlton.com) – Luxury hospitality positioning
2. **The Ladders** (theladders.com) – Executive job search platform
3. **ExecThread** (execthread.com) – Executive networking/search
4. **Starting Monday** (startingmonday.app) – Our platform (current)

---

## 1. HERO SECTION PATTERNS

### Ritz-Carlton Strategy
- **Positioning:** "Journey into a world where wonders await"
- **Copy tone:** Aspirational, destination-focused, emotional narrative
- **Visual:** Stunning hero imagery (jungle, ocean, architecture)
- **CTA placement:** Functional search box (primary interaction)
- **Secondary messaging:** "Step into a world of inspired destinations, visionary design and legendary service"
- **Outcome language:** "Every stay transforms the journey that follows" (transformation, legacy)

**Key insight:** Uses visceral imagery + emotional outcomes. The CTA is built into the experience, not a button.

### The Ladders Strategy
- **Positioning:** "Find your next executive role"
- **Copy tone:** Direct, action-oriented, opportunity-focused
- **Visual:** Professional photography (power poses, corporate settings)
- **CTA placement:** Prominent "Get Started" button
- **Primary value prop:** "Million+ six-figure roles. Curated for you."
- **Secondary messaging:** Highlights filtering, curation, executive-specific

**Key insight:** Emphasizes volume + curation + exclusivity. "Million+ roles" signals scale.

### ExecThread Strategy
- **Positioning:** Network-first approach
- **Copy tone:** Professional, relationship-focused
- **Visual:** Executive/professional imagery
- **CTA placement:** Sign up to access network
- **Primary value prop:** Access to network of executives and opportunities
- **Secondary messaging:** Peer recommendations, trust, insider access

**Key insight:** Social proof + network effect. "Who you know" narrative.

### Starting Monday (Current)
- **Positioning:** "Excellence finds excellence"
- **Copy tone:** Luxury, aspirational, legacy-focused
- **Visual:** Illustrated executive sketch (professional but stylized)
- **CTA placement:** Path selector buttons (role-based routing)
- **Primary value prop:** "Precision timing, strategic curation, outcomes worthy of your legacy"
- **Secondary messaging:** Early signal detection, private execution

**Key insight:** Outcome + legacy framing. But missing visceral imagery impact.

## 4. OUTCOME FRAMING DIFFERENCES

### The Ladders

- **Speed:** "Find Your Next"

### Ritz-Carlton
- **Outcome:** Transform your life through experience
- **Speed:** Immediate immersion
- **Proof:** Visual storytelling, destination curation

### ExecThread
- **Outcome:** Build valuable relationships + find opportunities
- **Speed:** Network access immediately
- **Proof:** Peer validation, insider access

### Starting Monday
- **Outcome:** Move to the shortlist before role is public (strategic)
- **Speed:** Early signal detection
- **Proof:** Weekly operating cadence, precision timing

**Gap:** We don't have a quick visceral proof of our outcome. Competitors show either:
- Scale (Ladders: million+ roles)
- Imagery (Ritz: stunning destinations)
- Network (ExecThread: peer connections)

We show: A process (weekly cadence) that takes time to validate.

However, we're missing:

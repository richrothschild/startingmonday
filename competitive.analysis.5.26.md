## 1. Summary of Competitive Analysis – Key Takeaways

### The Market Reality

Starting Monday occupies a **unique, defensible position** that is not currently occupied by any competitor:

**The Intelligence + Workflow Layer for Self-Directed Executives**

- **No competitor combines** real-time signals + campaign orchestration + AI prep briefs at this price point
- **Job boards** are reactive; SM is proactive (signals *before* posting)
- **Coaching** is mentorship-based; SM is infrastructure-based (10–50x cheaper)
- **Reverse recruiting** is outsourced; SM is self-directed (user retains control)
- **Resume services** are artifact-focused; SM is workflow-focused (integration over isolation)

### Competitive Set (Not Substitutes)

| Category | Player | Price | What They Own | SM Position |
|----------|--------|-------|---------------|------------|
| **Done-for-you** | Find My Profession | $2K–$4K/mo | "We find your role" | 10–80x cheaper; you control |
| **Job boards** | LinkedIn Premium, Ladders | $25–$50/mo | "Here are posted jobs" | Real signals *before* posting |
| **Coaching** | Ambitious Exec, coaches | $300–$500/session | "We teach you" | Infrastructure, not mentorship |
| **Artifacts** | ResumeSpice | $450–$700 one-time | "Perfect resume/LinkedIn" | Integrated workflow (not one-shot) |
| **Interview** | Prepfully, Interview Kickstart | $250–$400/session or $5K+ | "Interview technique" | Role-specific context (not technique) |
| **Retained search** | Heidrick, Korn Ferry | $50K–$150K+ | "We place C-suite" | 100–1000x cheaper; smaller market |

### The Positioning Lock

**"Your campaign, equipped with edge."**

- **You run it** (vs. FMP: they run it)
- **You know first** (vs. job boards: you know when posted)
- **You prepare rigorously** (vs. coaching: infrastructure not relationship)
- **You own the outcome** (vs. retained: you drive the decisions)

### Market Segment SM Actually Owns

**Transition-ready SVP/VP leaders in technology who:**
- Want disciplined execution (not outsourced chaos)
- Value early visibility (signal before posting)
- Have time to run a campaign (urgency + availability)
- Want affordability (can't justify $5K–$150K investment)
- Prefer control (don't want someone else pitching them)
- Tech-focused (CIO, CTO, VP Engineering, Chief Data Officer, Chief Information Security Officer)

**This is a distinct, valuable segment** that doesn't compete directly with FMP/coaching/boards because the buyer's problem is different.

### Competitive Edges (Defensible)

1. **Signal Intelligence** – Only SM + retained firms catch pre-posting transitions. Requires: SEC monitoring, funding tracking, news ingestion, clustering logic. High barrier to entry.
2. **Tech Executive Specialization** – Prep briefs, positioning prompts, signal filtering all tuned to *technology leadership*, not generic C-suite.
3. **Real-Time Workflow** – Campaign tracking + prep generation on-demand. Distinguishes from static coaching frameworks.
4. **Affordability at Scale** – $49–$199/mo pricing sustainable because infrastructure-based, not people-based (vs. coaching model which requires 1:1 effort).

### Competitive Weaknesses (Know & Own)

1. **No recruiter relationships** – Can't apply on user's behalf (FMP advantage)
2. **Requires active work** – User must orchestrate campaign (FMP advantage)
3. **No credibility brand yet** – Ambitious Exec has founder story; SM is emerging (Heidrick/Korn Ferry advantage)
4. **No placement guarantee** – "We find opportunities" not "We place you" (retained search advantage)
5. **Small TAM vs. job boards** – $150K–$500K+ execs only; can't compete on volume (LinkedIn/Ladders advantage)

**Intentional choices. Not bugs.**

## 3. Coach Validation Plan – 20-Call Script

### Goal
Validate whether **coaches will pay** for client portfolio access to SM (B2B channel viability).

If validation succeeds: Proceed to coach platform build.
If validation fails: Pause coach roadmap; focus on B2C.

### Target Profile

**Who to call:**
- Executive coaches (transition/outplacement-focused, not pure leadership dev)
- Coaches with 5+ years placing execs, $150K–$500K+ outcomes
- Who currently track client pipelines manually or in Notion/Sheets
- Who would benefit from shared visibility with clients

**Where to find them:**
- ICF (International Coach Federation) directory, filtered by executive/career focus
- LinkedIn search: "executive coach," "transition coach," "outplacement coach" + credential filter
- Referrals from existing SM users who know coaches
- Hudson Institute, CTI, Marshall Goldsmith network alumni

### Pre-Call Preparation

**Research before calling:**
- Check their website/LinkedIn for client success stories
- Understand their positioning (career coach vs. leadership coach)
- Note how they currently track client progress (email, spreadsheets, Notion, HubSpot, etc.)
- Identify one specific way SM would help them (e.g., "You'd see client pipeline updates in real-time instead of asking in sessions")

**Positioning for the call:**
> "Hi [Coach Name], I'm building a tool for executive coaches and their transition clients. Five minutes to get your read on whether this solves a real problem?"

---

### 20-Call Script (Core Questions)

#### **Segment 1: Understand Current Workflow (Minutes 1–3)**

1. **"How do you currently track your transition clients' job search progress?"**
   - Listen for: Email updates, spreadsheets, Notion, no system, ask in sessions
   - *Red flag if:* "I don't really track it"

2. **"On a scale of 1–10, how clear do you have on each client's pipeline, target companies, and outreach status between sessions?"**
   - *Aim for:* 4–7 range (problems exist)
   - *Red flag if:* 8–10 (they have it solved elsewhere) or 1–3 (too ad-hoc to be salvageable)

3. **"When a client doesn't land an interview or gets stuck, how do you diagnose whether it's resume, network, messaging, or timing?"**
   - Listen for: Vague ("I ask them"), structured ("I look at outreach patterns + signal timing"), or missing visibility
   - *This tells you:* Whether they have enough data to coach effectively

#### **Segment 2: Introduce the Concept (Minutes 4–6)**

**Show/describe SM core features:**
- "Imagine you and your client see the same thing: daily signals on their target companies, shared pipeline tracker, role-specific prep briefs"
- "No more 'What companies should I target?' in sessions—that's solved upfront"
- "Your sessions become strategy and psychology, not logistics"

4. **"If your clients had daily signal briefings on their target companies plus a shared outreach tracker, would that change your coaching?"**
   - *Aim for:* "Yes, definitely" or "Probably—let me think about it"
   - *Red flag:* "I don't think so" or "My clients already do that"

#### **Segment 3: The Hard Close (Minutes 7–9)**

**This is the validation question. Ask it exactly.**

5. **"Would you pay $X/month to have your active transition clients use this—so you both see their pipeline, signals, and prep in real time?"**
   - *Pricing anchor options to test:*
     - Option A: "$99/month per coach, $39/month per client"
     - Option B: "$249/month flat for up to 10 clients"
     - Option C: "What would make sense to you?"
   - *Listen for the answer. Write it down.*

**Follow-up if hesitation:**
> "I know you can't commit today. But if this existed and solved the visibility problem, would you be willing to run a trial with 1–2 clients?"

6. **"What would have to be true for you to recommend this to your clients?"**
   - Listen for: Privacy assurance, client ease-of-use, cost, integration with their other tools
   - *These are your must-haves for coach platform.*

#### **Segment 4: The Design Partner Ask (Minute 10)**

**Only ask this if answers to Q5 were positive.**

7. **"Would you be willing to be a design partner—let me show you the product early, get your feedback, help shape it—in exchange for a year of free access?"**
   - *Red flag if:* "I'm too busy"
   - *Green light if:* "Maybe, let me see it first" or "Yes, if it's actually useful"

---

### Success Criteria

**Proceed to coach platform build if:**
- 5+ of 20 coaches answer "Yes" to Q5 (hard close: would you pay?)
- 3+ coaches willing to be design partners
- Pricing feedback clusters around a viable range ($100–$300/mo coach tier)
- Coach says: "My clients are already asking me for this" or "I've been meaning to build this"

**Pause coach roadmap if:**
- Fewer than 3 of 20 say "Yes" to Q5
- Most answers: "My clients already do this" or "Probably not"
- Pricing feedback: "This is nice-to-have but not worth $X"
- Coach says: "I coach differently" or "This doesn't fit my model"

3. **Move objection handling below the fold** (Days 2–3)
   - Current: "How is this different from coaches/recruiters/LinkedIn?" is prominent

### Days 15–30: ICP Focus & Segment Testing

**Goal:** Run messaging experiments with laid-off SVP/VPs to validate highest-conversion audience.

#### Actions

1. **Create ICP-specific landing pages** (Days 15–22)
   - `/for-laid-off-svp` – "You have urgency and time. Move with edge."
   - `/for-seated-executive` – "Run a quiet search. Know before they know."
   - `/for-board-candidates` – "Transition windows appear on radar weeks early."
   - Keep these as A/B test variants initially (not full website branches)

2. **Set up conversion tracking by segment** (Days 15–18)
   - Tag signups with "source_segment" (laid_off, seated, board_candidate)
   - Track activation rate (first brief generated within 48h)
   - Track week-2 engagement (3+ sessions)
   - Track month-1 retention by segment

3. **Run 5 user interviews with each segment** (Days 18–30)
   - Laid-off SVP/VP (5 interviews)
   - Seated executive exploring (5 interviews)
   - Board/advisor candidate (5 interviews)
   - Ask: "What surprised you about the product?" + "What's the biggest value?"
   - Goal: Identify which segment finds fastest value

4. **Tighten 11-day claim** (Days 20–28)
   - Current: "In 11 days, you'll know about transitions before they're public"
   - Status: Needs internal study or public methodology to defend
   - Action: Audit 10 recent placements. What's actual signal-to-offer timeframe?
   - Document: Either publish methodology ("Here's how we found this") or soften claim ("Often weeks before posting")

#### Metrics to Track

- Signup source attribution by segment
- Activation rate by segment (first brief in 48h)
- Week-2 engagement by segment (at least 1 signal briefing review)
- Month-1 retention by segment
- Churn reason by segment (too expensive, not enough time, didn't work, etc.)

### Days 45–60: B2C Retention & Churn Analysis

**Goal:** Lock in B2C product-market fit before considering expansion.

#### Actions

1. **Month-1 Retention Cohort Analysis** (Days 45–50)
   - Segment users by signup date (weeks 1–8)
   - Calculate retention curves: Week 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
   - Compare by ICP segment (laid-off, seated, board)
   - Answer: Which segment has best retention?

2. **Churn Reason Deep-Dive** (Days 48–55)
   - Interview 10 churned users: "Why'd you stop?"
   - Categorize: Price, time, value, trust, features
   - If "time" is >50%: Product is too demanding (messaging issue or real barrier)
   - If "price" is >50%: Wrong pricing anchor or wrong segment
   - If "value" is >50%: Product isn't delivering on promise

3. **Feature Prioritization** (Days 50–60)
   - What are active users asking for?
   - What would reduce churn?
   - Prioritize: What's 1 feature that moves needle?
   - (Avoid: "More features" if real problem is messaging or positioning)

4. **Forecast 90-day Metrics** (Days 55–60)
   - Project MRR based on signup rate + retention
   - Identify bottleneck (acquisition, activation, retention)
   - Plan: Days 61–90 should attack bottleneck, not add features

---

### Decision Tree (Days 55–60)

**If retention is strong (>70% month-1) + ICP is clear:**
→ Go hard on acquisition for that segment. Proven model exists.

**If retention is weak (<40% month-1):**
→ Stop acquisition. Fix retention first. What are churned users saying?

**If activation is slow (first brief >72h):**
→ Onboarding friction. Lower bar for first brief or simplify signup.

**If coach validation succeeds:**
→ Start multi-tenant design (parallel to B2C ops)

**If coach validation fails:**
→ Double down on B2C. Cut coach work from roadmap.
